January 7 

Season of the Witch (2011)

95 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 28/100 (27 reviews)
14th-century knights transport a suspected witch to a monastery, where monks deduce her powers could be the source of the Black Plague.
Director:
Dominic Sena
Stars:
Nicolas Cage, Ron Perlman, Claire Foy, Stephen Campbell Moore
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sounds like the beginning of a decent SyFy program, but as a feature film -- really? The last time Nic Cage partnered with director Dominic Sena, Gone in Sixty Seconds (2000) resuscitated Cage's career -- for the second time if we're remembering correctly. It was the era when Angelina Jolie was barely a movie star, too. Anyway, their reunion heralds Cage's first appearance in what looks to be another fascinatingly weird year of career choices. Conjuring up images of one of the most disastrous centuries in these troubled times? Folding in witches and the Black Plague? And a PG-13 rating? Sounds like a potential miscue for veteran production company/newly minted distributor Relativity Media, who picked up the film from Lionsgate, who teased us with a few come-and-go release dates last year.

Country Strong (2010)

117 min   -   Drama | Music

Metascore: 45/100 (30 reviews)
A rising country-music songwriter works with a fallen star to work their way fame, causing romantic complications along the way.
Director:
Shana Feste
Stars:
Garrett Hedlund, Gwyneth Paltrow, Leighton Meester, Tim McGraw
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Clearly taking advantage of Garrett Hedlund's icon-making appearance in TRON: Legacy, Country Strong also seems destined to remind us all of Jeff Bridges' Oscar-winning role in Crazy Heart, which was released almost one year ago to the day. I appreciate Gwyneth majorly playing against type, but I think she should have gone Method here; imagine her performance after weeks of whiskey blackouts and singing nothing but "Willin'" by Little Feat.

January 14 

The Green Hornet (2011)

119 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 39/100 (39 reviews)
Following the death of his father, Britt Reid, heir to his father's large company, teams up with his late dad's assistant Kato to become a masked crime fighting team.
Director:
Michel Gondry
Stars:
Seth Rogen, Jay Chou, Christoph Waltz, Cameron Diaz
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's hard to remember the initial excitement we felt for this take on the classic Bruce Lee TV show way back when Kevin Smith, or even Kung Fu Hustle (2004)'s Stephen Chow, was going to make it. At the same time, we're kinda glad they didn't. After a few delays, the addition of Rogen and Gondry and a last minute 3-D conversion, we're just hoping this won't end up looking like an extended outtake from Be Kind Rewind (2008) -- a "Sweded" version of the '60s series with a bigger budget and more pot jokes. Meanwhile, who would've guessed a year ago that Waltz might be the best part of this movie?

The Dilemma (2011)

111 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 46/100 (32 reviews)
A man discovers that his best friend's wife is having an affair.
Director:
Ron Howard
Stars:
Vince Vaughn, Kevin James, Winona Ryder, Jennifer Connelly
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Will Ron Howard finally make a fun movie? We hope so, and we also have fingers crossed for the celebrity count to keep increasing for this comedy, so it'll become the anti-Valentine's Day (2010). Love knowing that Winona beat out Kate Beckinsale and Uma Thurman for the starring role, and that she'll be caught with a tattooed-and-pierced Channing Tatum.

Barney's Version (2010)

134 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 67/100 (33 reviews)
The picaresque and touching story of the politically incorrect, fully lived life of the impulsive, irascible and fearlessly blunt Barney Panofsky.
Director:
Richard J. Lewis
Stars:
Paul Giamatti, Rosamund Pike, Jake Hoffman, Macha Grenon
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: No surprise: Paul Giammati's performance anchors this drama, an adaptation of Mordecai Richler's last novel. Giamatti has a certain reputation for saving the films he's in (Cold Souls and The Hawk Is Dying come to mind), yet critics seem to be divided as to whether his work saves what is otherwise an aimless take on an overstuffed, unwieldy novel. We're always willing to give the actor the benefit of the doubt.

Ong-bak 3 (2010)

99 min   -   Action

Metascore: 38/100 (4 reviews)
Ong Bak 3 picks up where Ong Bak 2 had left off. Tien is captured and almost beaten to death before he is saved and brought back to the Kana Khone villagers. There he is taught meditation and how to deal with his Karma, but very soon his arch rival returns challenging Tien for a final duel.
Directors:
Tony Jaa | Panna Rittikrai
Stars:
Tony Jaa, Dan Chupong, Sarunyu Wongkrachang, Primorata Dejudom
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We hear this trilogy goes out with a Buddhist whisper, but it must have been hard to contend with the expectations raised by the first movie, then all the rumors and folklore generated on the set of the sequel (really a prequel), where Tony Jaa went MIA for two months over budget frustrations, though his departure was initially blamed on a sort of mental/spiritual breakdown. Hopefully an honest and thorough making-of documentary will be released with the inevitable Blu-ray trilogy.

En ganske snill mann (2010)

113 min   -   Comedy | Crime | Drama

Metascore: 68/100 (14 reviews)
Ulrik is reluctantly let out of prison after serving 12 years for murder. He has to cope with his gang, his ex, a few women - and a snitch. His son has a fiancé. Her family doesn't approve of murder. They have a nursery, they have principles. Ulrik is a somewhat gentle man. But how gentle can you be? ...and he's losing his hair.
Director:
Hans Petter Moland
Stars:
Stellan Skarsgård, Bjørn Floberg, Jorunn Kjellsby, Gard B. Eidsvold
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Norwegian director Hans Petter Moland reunites with his Aberdeen (2000) star Stellan Skarsgård (perfectly cast in The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011) remake, FWIW) for a dry-humor crowdpleaser that won raves from the Berlin International Film Festival last year. Art-house audiences, perhaps eager to create a new hit after championing a handful of recent films, could help this one breakthrough with good word-of-mouth.

January 21 

The Company Men (2010)

104 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 69/100 (34 reviews)
The story centers on a year in the life of three men trying to survive a round of corporate downsizing at a major company - and how that affects them, their families, and their communities.
Director:
John Wells
Stars:
Ben Affleck, Chris Cooper, Tommy Lee Jones, Suzanne Rico
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: More comforting than you might think on first glance, the feature debut of TV mega-creator John Wells ("ER", "The West Wing") has been compared to Up in the Air since it unfurled at Sundance this time last year, where the worst reviews said it's a solid alternative to Hollywood offerings on the same subject of the effects of the withered economy. And despite all the early Oscar buzz and with a firm commitment from the Weinsteins to market the picture, The King's Speech (2010) became the studio's darling, and all things Affleck were focused on The Town (2010), so the downsized businessmen found themselves having to fend for themselves -- again!

No Strings Attached (2011)

108 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 50/100 (36 reviews)
A guy and girl try to keep their relationship strictly physical, but it's not long before they learn that they want something more.
Director:
Ivan Reitman
Stars:
Natalie Portman, Ashton Kutcher, Kevin Kline, Cary Elwes
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ashton and Natalie hook up for the first in a series of friends-with-benefits comedies coming in the next year or so, though this is the one we're holding out for. While Natalie Portman pretty much can do no wrong, we wouldn't say the same of her co-star and her director. Hopefully Paramount keeps it edgy here with a hardcore title like the one originally announced, F__kbuddies. (Meanwhile, screenwriter Liz Meriwether is earning attention for her script titles.) It would be great if the story honestly addresses the intricacies of having an FWB -- and the awesomeness.

The Way Back (2010)

133 min   -   Adventure | Drama | History

Metascore: 66/100 (33 reviews)
Siberian gulag escapees travel 4,000 miles by foot to freedom in India.
Director:
Peter Weir
Stars:
Jim Sturgess, Ed Harris, Colin Farrell, Dragos Bucur
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Revamped indie distributor Newmarket Films has Oscar hopes for the latest picture from Peter Weir (Gallipoli (1981), The Truman Show (1998)), who is second only to Terrence Malick when it comes to selecting projects. Weir's adaptation of Slavomir Rawicz's novel The Long Walk -- a cult work widely believed to include bountiful factual liberties -- reportedly is a brutal, hard-to-endure affair. A hard sell for winter audiences? Perhaps. Though Jim Sturgess should benefit from a toughening-up, and the actors surrounding him -- Ed Harris, Mark Strong, and Colin Farrell (featuring a chest tattoo of Stalin's face) -- creates one of the strongest casts of the new year.

January 28 

The Mechanic (2011)

93 min   -   Action | Crime | Thriller

Metascore: 49/100 (35 reviews)
Follows an elite hit man as he teaches his trade to an apprentice who has a connection to one of his previous victims.
Director:
Simon West
Stars:
Jason Statham, Ben Foster, Donald Sutherland, Tony Goldwyn
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Selfishly, I'm happy that Jason Statham is still single, however, if you're going to star in Charles Bronson remakes, shouldn't you have a Jill Ireland in your life? Otherwise, the casting here is flawless and director Simon West (Lara Croft: Tomb Raider (2001), Con Air (1997)) clearly is in his element. Also, The Expendables confirmed that Statham should keep his action films free of future Neveldine/Taylor crud and goo.

An American seminary student travels to Italy to take an exorcism course.
Director:
Mikael Håfström
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Mikael Hafstrom is back in 1408 (2007) territory, which is a good sign for success, but does the exorcism idea seem a little overworked lately?

Biutiful (2010)

148 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 58/100 (33 reviews)
This is the story of Uxbal, a man living in this world, but able to see his death, which guides his every move.
Director:
Alejandro González Iñárritu
Stars:
Javier Bardem, Maricel Álvarez, Hanaa Bouchaib, Guillermo Estrella
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Expect a re-edited version of the latest from Alejandro González Iñárritu, since the buzz from Cannes, Telluride, and Toronto was supportive but not necessarily favorable. The combination of Iñárritu's visual dynamics and Bardem's brooding, award-winning performance (best actor at Cannes) clearly is worth anticipating in any form, despite all the dark subject-matter tones. Iñárritu's contemporaries, such as Werner Herzog, Guillermo del Toro, and Julian Schnabel are rallying around the picture, which is also Mexico's official submission for the Best Foreign Language Film Oscar.

From Prada to Nada (2011)

107 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 39/100 (10 reviews)
A Latina spin on Jane Austen's "Sense and Sensibility," where two spoiled sisters who have been left penniless after their father's sudden death are forced to move in with their estranged aunt in East Los Angeles.
Director:
Angel Gracia
Stars:
Camilla Belle, Alexa PenaVega, Kuno Becker, Tina French
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Jane Austen's Sense and Sensibility gets a Latina spin, with the surprise pairing of Camilla Belle and former spy kid Alexa Vega. We wish (a) that co-writer Fina Torres (Woman on Top (2000)), who was originally set to direct, was able to keep the gig or (b) that the producers dug a bit deeper for an original script from another emerging filmmaker.

Kaboom (2010)

86 min   -   Comedy | Mystery | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 64/100 (24 reviews)
A sci-fi story centered on the sexual awakening of a group of college students.
Director:
Gregg Araki
Stars:
Thomas Dekker, Haley Bennett, Chris Zylka, Roxane Mesquida
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Gregg Araki pays homage to his Teenage Apocalypse Trilogy from the 90s but this makes it two films in a row since the haunting Mysterious Skin (2004) (where he reached a storytelling zenith) that have been called rebellious, mischievous, and entertaining... but not vital. You know, in the wake of the Oscar nominations, that sounds like just like kind of movie I'd like to see.
