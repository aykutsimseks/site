February 4 

Sanctum (2011)

108 min   -   Adventure | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 42/100 (33 reviews)
An underwater cave diving team experiences a life-threatening crisis during an expedition to the unexplored and least accessible cave system in the world.
Director:
Alister Grierson
Stars:
Rhys Wakefield, Allison Cratchley, Christopher James Baker, Richard Roxburgh
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: As a producer, we imagine that James Cameron functions more like an actual director, which makes us feel restless for Sanctum's release. How will the best-known filmmaker in the world top Avatar? In truth, he won't. Instead, he's wrapping his patented 3D technology around a story inspired by a real-life ordeal experienced by one of his collaborators, producer-adventurer Andrew Wight, who was trapped in a cave system along with 14 other divers after a freak stormed sealed shut their entrance. We love that Cameron kept his cast Australian for the dramatization.

The Roommate (2011)

91 min   -   Drama | Horror | Thriller

Metascore: 23/100 (16 reviews)
College student Sara finds that her new roommate Rebecca has an obsession with her, which quickly turns violent.
Director:
Christian E. Christiansen
Stars:
Minka Kelly, Leighton Meester, Cam Gigandet, Aly Michalka
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sounds like a better plot than anything "Gossip Girl" has sighed out over its third season; we've always loved Blair more than Serena, and The Roommate is shaping up to be another undeniable camp-thriller from Screen Gems (Obsessed). Another reason we're anticipating this one? The presence of Minka Kelly, a cheery spot from (500) Days of Summer (and, of course, "Friday Night Lights").

Cold Weather (2010)

96 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Mystery

Metascore: 64/100 (21 reviews)
A guy who moves back to Portland, Oregon becomes involved in the mystery of his ex-girlfriend's disappearance.
Director:
Aaron Katz
Stars:
Cris Lankenau, Raúl Castillo, Robyn Rikoon, Trieste Kelly Dunn
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Indie audiences and critics alike have praised the latest work from writer-director Aaron Katz; it seems his mystery-genre experiment is something we haven't seen before. Portland, Oregon has never been more in focus, and there's always been a fertile filmmaking scene there. This one is so on our late-winter radar.

The Other Woman (2009)

102 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 37/100 (16 reviews)
A drama that details the story of a woman's difficult relationship with her stepson.
Director:
Don Roos
Stars:
Natalie Portman, Scott Cohen, Lisa Kudrow, Charlie Tahan
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Black Swan's success and Natalie Portman's Oscar nomination have helped put her collaboration with Don Roos (The Opposite of Sex (1998)) back on the map, though it's been available on VOD since the first of the year. I'm patiently awaiting the next great Roos-Kudrow movie collaboration after they turned Web Therapy (2008) from an online program to a Showtime hit.

February 11 

Just Go with It (2011)

117 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 33/100 (31 reviews)
On a weekend trip to Hawaii, a plastic surgeon convinces his loyal assistant to pose as his soon-to-be-divorced wife in order to cover up a careless lie he told to his much-younger girlfriend.
Director:
Dennis Dugan
Stars:
Adam Sandler, Jennifer Aniston, Brooklyn Decker, Nicole Kidman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Audiences passed on The Dilemma and I have to say this premise is just as unappealing. A Hawaiian vacation with Jennifer Aniston, Nicole Kidman, and Sports Illustrated swimsuit model Brooklyn Decker must be hard work for Adam Sandler, though. At least the press is being kind to Jennifer Aniston; instead of trying to romantically link her to Sandler, they're focusing on her "beachy" hair styles, rumored baby-food diet (please), and gal-paling with Nicole.

Gnomeo & Juliet (2011)

84 min   -   Animation | Comedy | Family | Romance

Metascore: 53/100 (28 reviews)
The neighboring gardens of Montague and Capulet are at war, but the gnomes, Gnomeo and Juliet, are in love.
Director:
Kelly Asbury
Stars:
James McAvoy, Emily Blunt, Maggie Smith, Ashley Jensen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Tangled is still breaking box-office records in Europe, but G&J is shaping up to be another Disney miscue. Did you know that John Lasseter nixed the project initially as he was establishing Pixar at the studio?

Cedar Rapids (2011)

87 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 70/100 (38 reviews)
Tim Lippe has no idea what he's in for when he's sent to Cedar Rapids, Iowa to represent his company at an annual insurance convention, where he soon finds himself under the "guidance" of three convention veterans.
Director:
Miguel Arteta
Stars:
Ed Helms, John C. Reilly, Anne Heche, Isiah Whitlock Jr.
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Miguel Arteta's Youth in Revolt (2009) didn't get a fair shake last year (chalk it up to now-standard Weinstein Company antics) but we're primed for his new film, mainly because it affords Ed Helms the opportunity to be a leading man -- even if his Tim Lippe seems like a close relative of Stu Price. Riding a decent wave of buzz out of Sundance, Fox Searchlight, whose comedies have fumbled a bit of late, has a champion here.

The Eagle (2011)

114 min   -   Action | Adventure | Drama

Metascore: 55/100 (35 reviews)
In Roman-ruled Britain, a young Roman soldier endeavors to honor his father's memory by finding his lost legion's golden emblem.
Director:
Kevin Macdonald
Stars:
Channing Tatum, Jamie Bell, Donald Sutherland, István Göz
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Kevin Macdonald (State of Play (2009)) reunites with his The Last King of Scotland (2006) screenwriter, Jeremy Brock, to dust off another small-screen BBC production. Channing Tatum brings the muscle mass, and his continued ascent to leading manhood will be assisted here by a remarkable supporting cast (and the fact that he doesn't really have to learn an accent). The whole questing son/missing father aspect has us referring to the movie as Tron: Antiquity.

Justin Bieber: Never Say Never (2011)

105 min   -   Documentary | Music

Metascore: 52/100 (22 reviews)
Follows Justin Bieber with some footage of performances from his 2010 concert tour.
Director:
Jon M. Chu
Stars:
Justin Bieber, Boys II Men, Miley Cyrus, Sean Kingston
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: One reason to shut down the Internet: while Justin Bieber says kind, inspiring things to the teenagers who adore him, Web hags and trolls start petitions to ship him off to North Korea. I think we should make him the face of the anti-bullying movement as well.

February 18 

I Am Number Four (2011)

109 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 36/100 (30 reviews)
Aliens and their Guardians are hiding on Earth from intergalactic bounty hunters. They can only be killed in numerical order, and Number Four is next on the list. This is his story.
Director:
D.J. Caruso
Stars:
Alex Pettyfer, Timothy Olyphant, Dianna Agron, Teresa Palmer
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Upon its announcement, Number Four was generating more news bits than tentpoles twice its size, thanks to the myriad names in front of and behind the camera: newly bankable director D.J. Caruso; disgraced memoirist James Frey (who co-wrote the source material -- really!); "Smallvile" duo Alfred Gough and Miles Millar; "Glee" star Dianna Agron (who mesmerized in that show's first-season finale); emerging leading man Timothy Olyphant; and Alex Pettyfer, who's being groomed for stardom despite an iffy career start and bratty reputation. So, yeah, it's been a news-making project, but I'm not sure the alien-teen mystery is on the minds of thrill-seeking audiences. Pettyfer, whose oft-delayed Beastly (2011) looks D.O.A., appears to be the one with everything to lose here -- either he LaBouefs this time or it might be over?

Unknown (2011)

113 min   -   Action | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 56/100 (38 reviews)
A man awakens from a coma, only to discover that someone has taken on his identity and that no one, (not even his wife), believes him. With the help of a young woman, he sets out to prove who he is.
Director:
Jaume Collet-Serra
Stars:
Liam Neeson, Diane Kruger, January Jones, Aidan Quinn
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Orphan director Jaume Collet-Serra is poised for a breakthrough, and a thriller from a hot screenwriting duo (Oliver Butcher and Stephen Cornwell have three other projects in development, including with Vin Diesel) could be another Taken (2008)-level hit for star Liam Neeson. Very enthused for the pairing of Neeson and Diane Kruger!

Big Mommas: Like Father, Like Son (2011)

107 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime

Metascore: 22/100 (14 reviews)
Malcolm Turner (Martin Lawrence) and his stepson Trent (Brandon T. Jackson) go under cover at an girls school to flush out a killer.
Director:
John Whitesell
Stars:
Martin Lawrence, Brandon T. Jackson, Jessica Lucas, Michelle Ang
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Who has been doing big-lady drag longer, Martin Lawrence or Tyler Perry? Aside from the Wild Hogs sequel being mothballed by Disney, Lawrence is enjoying somewhat of a renaissance with his successful 2010 stand-up tour flowing right into the third Big Momma caper and the announcement of Bad Boys 3 (2017) for a 2012 release. I feel a bit bad for Brandon T. Jackson, who was supposed to breakout with Percy Jackson & the Olympians: The Lightning Thief (2010) but I hope he brings some of the swagger he revealed in the otherwise forgettable Lottery Ticket (2010). And move over Nia Long as love interest because here comes ... Faizon Love.

Vanishing on 7th Street (2010)

92 min   -   Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 50/100 (14 reviews)
The population of Detroit has almost completely disappeared, but a few remain. As daylight disappears they realize that the Dark is coming for them.
Director:
Brad Anderson
Stars:
Hayden Christensen, Thandie Newton, John Leguizamo, Jacob Latimore
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Contentedly indie director Brad Anderson is in "Twilight Zone" mode here, but every review we've come across indicates a strong premise weakened by slack characterizations and bad dialogue. Also, the bar where everyone looks to survive the coming darkess is named "Sunny's". Ugh! Here's an especially readable review. I assume this one will come and go quietly and, hopefully, Anderson will reunite with Christian Bale for the in-development project Concrete Island.

I Am (2010)

78 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 38/100 (18 reviews)
Director Tom Shadyac speaks with intellectual and spiritual leaders about what's wrong with our world and how we can improve both it and the way we live in it.
Director:
Tom Shadyac
Stars:
Tom Shadyac, Desmond Tutu, Noam Chomsky, Ray Anderson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If you haven't heard the story yet: Liar Liar (1997) director Tom Shadyac took a spill on his bicycle that left him with some serious injuries and, once he emerged from a dark period, he set out to meet with world leaders and thinkers who inspire him, instead of making another broad, big-budget comedy. Two things interest me here: Shadyac's next move and how this newest experiment in movie distribution will pan out.

Immigration Tango (2010)

An American couple and a foreign couple test the limits of friendship and love when they switch partners and get married for green cards.
Director:
David Burton Morris
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Flying low on the radar is this politically themed romantic comedy; distributor Roadside Attractions is busy with two different Oscar campaigns (Biutiful (2010) and Winter's Bone (2010)) and I think the studio is letting these visa concerns sort themselves out.

También la lluvia (2010)

103 min   -   Drama | History

As a director and his crew shoot a controversial film about Christopher Columbus in Cochabamba, Bolivia, local people rise up against plans to privatize the water supply.
Director:

Stars:
Gael García Bernal, Luis Tosar, Karra Elejalde, Juan Carlos Aduviri

Brotherhood (2010)

76 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 52/100 (6 reviews)
Adam Buckley finds himself in the middle of a convenience store robbery during his last night as a pledge for a college fraternity. When the initiation ritual goes horribly wrong, and every move proves disastrous, Adam is forced to confront a new challenge all together, and he has to take a stand.
Director:
Will Canon
Stars:
Trevor Morgan, Lou Taylor Pucci, Arlen Escarpeta, Jon Foster
Watch Trailer

Drive Angry (2011)

104 min   -   Action | Comedy | Fantasy | Thriller

Metascore: 44/100 (21 reviews)
A vengeful father escapes from hell and chases after the men who killed his daughter and kidnapped his granddaughter.
Director:
Patrick Lussier
Stars:
Nicolas Cage, Amber Heard, William Fichtner, Billy Burke
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Patrick Lussier's last movie, My Bloody Valentine, almost qualifies as a new camp-horror classic (admit it, either you liked it or have friends who did), so we're oddly hopeful for his union with Nic Cage, who cannot say no to a cheese plate.

Hall Pass (2011)

105 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 45/100 (36 reviews)
Rick and Fred, two husbands who are having difficulty in their marriages, are given a Hall Pass by their wives: for one week, they can do whatever they want.
Directors:
Bobby Farrelly | Peter Farrelly
Stars:
Owen Wilson, Jason Sudeikis, Christina Applegate, Jenna Fischer
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This story idea has been kicking around since 2005 at least; first it was at Fox before New Line took control as they nestled underneath the Warner Bros. umbrella. We understand the Farrelly Bros. are half sticking to their usual brand of ribald comedy and half stepping into Apatow territory with their exploration of married maleness. Will Owen Wilson ever write another screenplay?

Des hommes et des dieux (2010)

122 min   -   Drama | History

Metascore: 86/100 (29 reviews)
Under threat by fundamentalist terrorists, a group of Trappist monks stationed with an impoverished Algerian community must decide whether to leave or stay.
Director:
Xavier Beauvois
Stars:
Lambert Wilson, Michael Lonsdale, Olivier Rabourdin, Philippe Laudenbach
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Though it didn't earn the official nomination, France's selection for this year's Best Foreign Language Oscar is director Xavier Beauvois's best-received film to date. The story is loosely based on the kidnapping of the Cistercian monks of Tibhirine in Algeria in 1996.

Heartbeats (2010)

101 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 70/100 (21 reviews)
The story of three close friends who are involved in a love-triangle.
Director:
Xavier Dolan
Stars:
Xavier Dolan, Monia Chokri, Niels Schneider, Anne Dorval
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Canadian film has some traction in the States right now what with Incendies (2010) up for the Best Foreign Language Oscar and this romantic drama, the latest from 21-year-old Xavier Dolan, who writes, directs, and stars in his projects. Dolan's debut, I Killed My Mother (2009), generated a lot of attention on the film festival/indie circuit (it scooped three awards at Cannes), and the general consensus is that he will one day make a groundbreaking film -- even if he tends to demonize his female characters. But I personally like the fact that Dolan is fresh, caustic, and imperfect; he also looks like a French Morrissey, which is more than welcome.

The Grace Card (2010)

101 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 43/100 (14 reviews)
Everything can change in an instant...and take a lifetime to unravel. Every day, we have the opportunity to rebuild relationships by extending and receiving God's grace. Offer The Grace Card, and never underestimate the power of God's love.
Director:
David G. Evans
Stars:
Michael Joiner, Michael Higgenbottom, Louis Gossett Jr., Joy Parmer Moore
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: A Memphis-based church enters the Faith-based market, inspired by the success the Sherwood Baptist Church has found with its theatrical productions (they produced Fireproof (2008)).
