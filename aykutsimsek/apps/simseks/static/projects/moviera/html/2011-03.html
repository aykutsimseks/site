March 4 

Rango (2011)

107 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Western

Metascore: 75/100 (35 reviews)
Rango is an ordinary chameleon who accidentally winds up in the town of Dirt, a lawless outpost in the Wild West in desperate need of a new sheriff.
Director:
Gore Verbinski
Stars:
Johnny Depp, Isla Fisher, Timothy Olyphant, Abigail Breslin
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We love that Captain Jack and his original Pirates of the Caribbean director have created a new hero, one centered around Johnny Depp's first motion-capture performance. Furthermore, we smile every time we consider the fact that the film earned a PG rating in part because Dirt's residents tend to smoke and drink. We're advocates of Wild West realness.

The Adjustment Bureau (2011)

106 min   -   Romance | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 60/100 (41 reviews)
The affair between a politician and a ballerina is affected by mysterious forces keeping the lovers apart.
Director:
George Nolfi
Stars:
Matt Damon, Emily Blunt, Lisa Thoreson, Florence Kastriner
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Bourne Ultimatum co-writer George Nolfi makes his directorial debut with his loose adaptation of Phillip K. Dick's short story, "The Adjustment Team". Expect the fabric of the real world to be torn apart before we get an idea of what's keeping Emily Blunt and Matt Damon from becoming the sexiest on-screen couple of the year. Thanks to two of the biggest films from last year, ballerinas are in, as are visually effected alternate realities; combine this with lackluster box-office action that past couple weeks and we have our fingers crossed for Damon and Blunt to warp this mystery into a sleeper hit.

Take Me Home Tonight (2011)

97 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 42/100 (28 reviews)
Four years after graduation, an awkward high school genius uses his sister's boyfriend's Labor Day party as the perfect opportunity to make his move on his high school crush.
Director:
Michael Dowse
Stars:
Topher Grace, Anna Faris, Dan Fogler, Teresa Palmer
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Looks like Topher Grace's pet project is another retro-fest, and we understand its delay (we've been hearing about the movie since 2007) was due to audience testing, testing, testing and getting the right final cut -- complete with gratuitous cocaine use and other debauchery. Grace nurtured the story with "That '70s Show" writer/producers Jackie and Jeff Filgo, and they picked an ace outsider director in Michael Dowse (It's All Gone Pete Tong (2004)). With a cast that includes Anna Faris playing Topher's twin and Michael Biehn playing their dad, we think some fanboys might come-of-age by the time the credits roll.

Red State (2011)

88 min   -   Action | Crime | Horror | Thriller

Metascore: 50/100 (9 reviews)
Set in Middle America, a group of teens receive an online invitation for sex, though they soon encounter fundamentalists with a much more sinister agenda.
Director:
Kevin Smith
Stars:
Michael Parks, Melissa Leo, John Goodman, Michael Angarano
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I find Kevin Smith's Red State antics -- crowd-sourcing monies, holding faux auctions, waging anti-protest protests, etc. -- more entertaining than the combined weight of all the movies he made during the last decade. Surely he can expect to keep his name in the press (even if he does indeed retire from directing after Hit Somebody), but it doesn't take a business degree to conclude that the Red State tour and subsequent Halloween theatrical release is a continuation of a grand publicity stunt and not an exercise in counter-Hollywood distribution ideas.

Beastly (2011)

86 min   -   Drama | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 40/100 (26 reviews)
A modern-day take on the "Beauty and the Beast" tale where a New York teen is transformed into a hideous monster in order to find true love.
Director:
Daniel Barnz
Stars:
Alex Pettyfer, Vanessa Hudgens, Mary-Kate Olsen, Justin Bradley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Daniel Branz follows up the critically appraised, little seen Phoebe in Wonderland (2008) with a take on the Beauty and the Beast tale. But teen girls, who clearly are the target market here, might wait for this one on DVD, since the affair looks like a super-sized episode of both "Gossip Girl" and "Smallville" -- which isn't surprising once you realize the newly hatched/already struggling CBS Films (yes, an offshoot of the network) is behind this fantasy.

Happythankyoumoreplease (2010)

100 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 45/100 (21 reviews)
Captures a generational moment - young people on the cusp of truly growing up, tiring of their reflexive cynicism, each in their own ways struggling to connect and define what it means to love and be loved.
Director:
Josh Radnor
Stars:
Josh Radnor, Malin Akerman, Zoe Kazan, Michael Algieri
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Keith loved this one at Sundance, where it earned the Audience Award in the Dramatic category. Off-screen drama: originally set to be distributed by Hannover House (of Twelve (2010) infamy) last year, the filmmakers pulled a change-up and upgraded to Anchor Bay.

Loong Boonmee raleuk chat (2010)

114 min   -   Drama | Fantasy

Metascore: 87/100 (21 reviews)
On his deathbed, Uncle Boonmee recalls his many past lives.
Director:
Apichatpong Weerasethakul
Stars:
Thanapat Saisaymar, Jenjira Pongpas, Sakda Kaewbuadee, Natthakarn Aphaiwonk
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Such a loud noise has been made over this quiet movie, I'm hoping it isn't arriving with too much hype and expectation. Those who know and love the work of director Apichatpong Weerasethakul (Sang sattawat (2006)) and the excellent Sud pralad (2004)) can expect mood, mood, mood, and meditative bits as Boonmee prepares for his death. And if anyone needs it all to make narrative sense, you might be in for a trying experience.

I Saw the Devil (2010)

141 min   -   Crime | Drama | Horror | Thriller

Metascore: 67/100 (19 reviews)
A secret agent exacts revenge on a serial killer through a series of captures and releases.
Director:
Jee-woon Kim
Stars:
Byung-hun Lee, Min-sik Choi, In-seo Kim, Ho-jin Chun
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Undoubtedly, Kim Ji-woon's highly regarded horror/thriller flick, which unites Oldboy's Choi Min-sik and Lee Byung-hun -- who starred in Ji-woon's The Good, the Bad, the Weird and will reprise Storm Shadow for G.I. Joe 2 -- will earn a Hollywood remake. In fact, I expect an announcement soon after opening weekend. Sony, perhaps?

Abel (2010)

82 min   -   Comedy | Drama

About a peculiar young boy who, as he blurs reality and fantasy, takes over the responsibilities of a family man in his father's absence.
Director:
Diego Luna
Stars:
Christopher Ruíz-Esparza, Karina Gidi, José María Yazpik, Carlos Aragón

The Buzz: Possibly I had film-festival burnout when I first saw director Diego Luna's feature debut, which admirably handles the subject of a child's fragile mind/imagination with equal parts daring and grace. Abel is an odd boy and Abel the movie succeeds because it's more honest than it is cuddly or determined to find a happy ending. I remember thinking that Michael Jackson, Luna's character from Mister Lonely (2007), could totally help this kid out, or at least rescue him from a potentially troubled adolescence.

March 11 

Battle Los Angeles (2011)

116 min   -   Action | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 37/100 (35 reviews)
A squadron of U.S. Marines becomes the last line of defense against a global invasion.
Director:
Jonathan Liebesman
Stars:
Aaron Eckhart, Michelle Rodriguez, Bridget Moynahan, Ramon Rodriguez
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Warner Bros. is so amped by Jonathan Liebesman's handling of this movie (for Columbia Pictures) that the studio has handed him the reins for Wrath of the Titans (2012) (which was sounding promising until recently, when it was announced that the sequel will be converted to 3D like the first movie). Meanwhile, it's been such a sluggish year at the box office, perhaps people have been saving their money for this visual effects heavy bonanza that looks and feels like a first-person shooter.

Jane Eyre (2011)

120 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 76/100 (35 reviews)
A mousy governess who softens the heart of her employer soon discovers that he's hiding a terrible secret.
Director:
Cary Joji Fukunaga
Stars:
Mia Wasikowska, Michael Fassbender, Jamie Bell, Su Elliot
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sin Nombre (2009) director Cary Fukunaga might seem like an illogical pick to handle the umpteenth adaptation of Charlotte Bronte's beloved novel, but aren't both stories class-based morality tales more than anything? Gracefully emerging from Alice in Wonderland (2010), Mia Wasikowska might be our new favorite actress.

Red Riding Hood (2011)

100 min   -   Fantasy | Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 29/100 (36 reviews)
Set in a medieval village that is haunted by a werewolf, a young girl falls for an orphaned woodcutter, much to her family's displeasure.
Director:
Catherine Hardwicke
Stars:
Amanda Seyfried, Lukas Haas, Gary Oldman, Billy Burke
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Yes, yes, this is familiar territory for Twilight director Catherine Hardwicke -- right down to the love triangle central to the plot -- but we're looking forward to seeing what classically inspired horror might look and feel like to her. Nice to see Lukas Haas getting consistent work, as he segues from one Warner Bros. film (Inception) to another; he joins one of the more inspired main casts of the year. Both Twilight and Harry Potter fans might be engaged by this tale, whose script made the 2009 Black List.

Mars Needs Moms (2011)

88 min   -   Animation | Action | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 49/100 (22 reviews)
A young boy named Milo gains a deeper appreciation for his mom after Martians come to Earth to take her away.
Director:
Simon Wells
Stars:
Seth Green, Joan Cusack, Dan Fogler, Elisabeth Harnois
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: A mission to save Joan Cusack? We were intrigued until we saw the first trailer and everything that followed, and the box-office forecast is nightmarish for this adaptation of Bloom County cartoonist Berkeley Breathed's book. Aside from Monster House (2006), Robert Zemeckis's motion-capture obsession is mostly hollow to us.

Certified Copy (2010)

106 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 82/100 (23 reviews)
In Tuscany to promote his latest book, a middle-aged British writer meets a French woman who leads him to the village of Lucignano. While there, a chance question reveals something deeper.
Director:
Abbas Kiarostami
Stars:
Juliette Binoche, William Shimell, Jean-Claude Carrière, Agathe Natanson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Abbas Kiarostami has achieved auteur status for his films Taste of Cherry (1997) and The Wind Will Carry Us (1999), and his latest is his first project made outside Iran. Combine his merits, Juliette Binoche's Cannes award for Best Actress, and the campaign to liberate jailed Iranian filmmaker Jafar Panahi, and you have one of the most anticipated art-house films of the year.

Black Death (2010)

102 min   -   Action | Adventure | Drama | History | Horror | Mystery

Metascore: 71/100 (10 reviews)
Set during the time of the first outbreak of bubonic plague in England, a young monk is given the task of learning the truth about reports of people being brought back to life in a small village.
Director:
Christopher Smith
Stars:
Eddie Redmayne, Sean Bean, Carice van Houten, Kimberley Nixon
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Severance (2006) director Christopher Smith is back with an on-trend horror-mystery tale set in England circa 1348. More than one genre specialist has described Smith's latest as a road movie completely free of horror- or period-film clichés. Something tells us this will become a catalog film for Magnet; in other words, people will discover it in the years to come.

Kill the Irishman (2011)

106 min   -   Action | Biography | Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 50/100 (15 reviews)
The true story of Danny Greene, a tough Irish thug working for mobsters in Cleveland during the 1970's.
Director:
Jonathan Hensleigh
Stars:
Ray Stevenson, Christopher Walken, Vincent D'Onofrio, Val Kilmer
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The Punisher (2004) filmmaker Jonathan Hensleigh directs Punisher: War Zone (2008) star Ray Stevenson in the true story of a rebel gangster whose exploits led to mob defection, murder plots against future presidential candidates, and the erosion of Mafia influence around the country. With ace source material and a competent cast and crew, we're surprised this wasn't developed at a major studio; can these underdogs take on big-time entertainment? Is it true that Tara Reid was once set to co-star in this as Greene's lover?

March 18 

Limitless (2011)

105 min   -   Mystery | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 59/100 (37 reviews)
With the help of a mysterious pill that enables the user to access 100 percent of his brain abilities, a struggling writer becomes a financial wizard, but it also puts him in a new world with lots of dangers.
Director:
Neil Burger
Stars:
Bradley Cooper, Anna Friel, Abbie Cornish, Robert De Niro
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Alan Glynn's debut novel shoots onto the big screen courtesy of director Neil Burger (The Illusionist), who surprisingly didn't write the screenplay -- that task went to Leslie Dixon, who of late has been writing middling romantic comedies, mostly (and the Hairspray remake). This is a great opportunity for Bradley Cooper to excite those of us who think his post-Hangover performances are about as cool as Gerard Butler's post-300 career. Here's hoping the costs are kept low, because the subject matter will be a tough mainstream sell, regardless of all the handsomeness and beauty amongst the cast members.

Paul (2011)

104 min   -   Adventure | Comedy | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 57/100 (37 reviews)
Two British comic-book geeks traveling across the U.S. encounter an alien outside Area 51.
Director:
Greg Mottola
Stars:
Simon Pegg, Nick Frost, Seth Rogen, Mia Stallard
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We're sure everyone had a blast making this film, and director Greg Mottola is making a career out of road trips and misadventures. After Scott Pilgrim's failure (and don't get us wrong: we love that movie), we're a bit hesitant if such nerdy adventures have a chance outside of their core audience nowadays. Industry-watchers have been rethinking the power and influence of the Comic-Con set, and Paul, which sounds like a pitch direct from San Diego, could join the ranks of perceived non-starters who first generated hype at the event. Really we're thinking and imagining it'll simply be the latest in the beloved Pegg/Frost canon.

The Lincoln Lawyer (2011)

118 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 63/100 (31 reviews)
A defense attorney has a crisis of conscience when he represents a wealthy client who has a foolproof plan to beat the system.
Director:
Brad Furman
Stars:
Matthew McConaughey, Marisa Tomei, Ryan Phillippe, William H. Macy
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I know that author Michael Connelly writes the kind of books my friends' dads read -- and of course his novel Blood Work was turned into a second-tier Clint Eastwood movie. With The Lincoln Lawyer, Connelly and McConaughey both might find themselves at the beginning of a potential franchise; McConaughey knows the value of playing a lawyer (A Time to Kill (1996) remains his most successful film), and the supporting cast here is formidable. Let's see what the first trailer looks like ...

March 25 

Sucker Punch (2011)

110 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 33/100 (29 reviews)
A young girl is institutionalized by her abusive stepfather. Retreating to an alternative reality as a coping strategy, she envisions a plan which will help her escape from the mental facility.
Director:
Zack Snyder
Stars:
Emily Browning, Vanessa Hudgens, Abbie Cornish, Jena Malone
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Long in development and once set to star an entirely different cast (Amanda Seyfried, Emma Stone, Evan Rachel Wood, and others), we've rallied behind the lesser known actresses -- especially the refreshingly articulate Jena Malone -- who are ready to bring Zack Snyder's first original story to the big screen, during the coveted "300 slot" no less. Can you believe Snyder's career-making movie was released 5 years ago this month? Determined to court teen girls and boys (of all ages) alike, the action has been kept to a PG-13 rating, though you know the DVD will include edited-out bits such as the Browning-on-Hamm scene.

Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Rodrick Rules (2011)

99 min   -   Comedy | Family

Metascore: 51/100 (23 reviews)
Back in middle school after summer vacation, Greg Heffley and his older brother Rodrick must deal with their parents' misguided attempts to have them bond.
Director:
David Bowers
Stars:
Zachary Gordon, Devon Bostick, Robert Capron, Rachael Harris
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The first Wimpy Kid surprised a lot of people with its long box-office legs, and soon after opening weekend, Fox greenlighted Rodrick. In between the two movies, fans of Jenny Kinney's series were treated a new, fifth book, and we won't be surprised one bit if this franchise continues with a third chapter or perhaps straight-to-DVD sequels.

Miral (2010)

112 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 45/100 (19 reviews)
A drama centered on an orphaned Palestinian girl growing up in the wake of Arab-Israeli war who finds herself drawn into the conflict.
Director:
Julian Schnabel
Stars:
Freida Pinto, Hiam Abbass, Willem Dafoe, Asma Al Shiukhy
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: After middling reviews from Venice and Toronto's respective film festivals, director Julian Schnabel found himself back in the editing room, reworking this adaption of Rula Jebreal's novel that tells the story of Dar Al-Tifi orphanage founder Hind Husseini, played by Hiam Habbas (The Visitor (2007)/Etz Limon (2008)). Perhaps the decision to refocus the screenplay on the character played by Freida Pinto wasn't the best idea? That said, we have full faith in Schnabel, who has based his filmmaking career on single-subject studies with lasting and admirable results. The combination of Schnabel, Harvey Weinstein, some United Nations and rating drama has yielded a heap of publicity for the film as its release date has drawn near.

Potiche (2010)

103 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 68/100 (31 reviews)
When her husband is taken hostage by his striking employees, a trophy wife (Deneuve) takes the reins of the family business and proves to be a remarkably effective leader. Business and personal complications arrive in the form of her ex-lover (Depardieu), a former union leader.
Director:
François Ozon
Stars:
Catherine Deneuve, Gérard Depardieu, Fabrice Luchini, Karin Viard
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Almost ten years after 8 femmes (2002)'s breakout success, Francois Ozon reunited with Catherine Deneuve for another candy-colored, ladies-first comedy -- a potential companion piece to recent hit Made in Dagenham (2010).

Peep World (2010)

79 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 27/100 (9 reviews)
Four siblings implode after the release of a book written about them.
Director:
Barry W. Blaustein
Stars:
Michael C. Hall, Sarah Silverman, Rainn Wilson, Lewis Black
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Barry Blaustein's comedy looks to approximate the showdown that we imagine takes place when Augusten Burroughs (or another popular memoirist) convenes with his family. Aside from his note-perfect cast -- we hear Silverman is her typical foul-mouthed, brilliant self and Wilson extends his actorly range -- we sense that Blaustein's documentary experience (he helmed the excellent Beyond the Mat (1999) and the little seen Guys 'N Divas: Battle of the High School Musicals (2009) helped him create the underlying pathos that's part of the reason his movie is earning such strong reviews.

White Irish Drinkers (2010)

109 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 51/100 (16 reviews)
Brooklyn, 1975: two brothers looking for a way out of their working-class neighborhood make a pact to rob a local theater on the night of a Rolling Stones concert.
Director:
John Gray
Stars:
Nick Thurston, Geoffrey Wigdor, Stephen Lang, Karen Allen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Writer-director John Gray has been working in TV since his last feature, 1996's The Glimmer Man (1996) (wow), but his coming-of-age drama has earned solid reviews, mostly for the hard-knocks performances and the romantic subplot that's buried under the heist.
