May 6 

Thor (2011)

115 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 57/100 (40 reviews)
The powerful but arrogant god Thor is cast out of Asgard to live amongst humans in Midgard (Earth), where he soon becomes one of their finest defenders.
Director:
Kenneth Branagh
Stars:
Chris Hemsworth, Anthony Hopkins, Natalie Portman, Tom Hiddleston
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The God of Thunder has earned release-date positioning formerly enjoyed by Tony Stark in both Iron Man movies, but Thor is the Marvel Universe character with question marks over his head in terms of this movie's blockbuster/franchise potential. I love and trust Kenneth Branagh as a director of drama, and even die-hard comics fans should be impressed with his Shakespearean take on these gods and mortals. I am, quite simply, worried about Branagh as an action director and whether he can render a seriously entertaining mainstream movie. (To be fair, we had the same concerns about Jon Favreau several years ago, and he at least got his franchise off to a great start initially.) Okay, so what I'm really indicating is that I hope this doesn't play like a Julie Taymor superhero film.

Something Borrowed (2011)

112 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 36/100 (30 reviews)
Friendships are tested and secrets come to the surface when terminally single Rachel falls for Dex, her best friend Darcy's fiancé.
Director:
Luke Greenfield
Stars:
Ginnifer Goodwin, Kate Hudson, Colin Egglesfield, John Krasinski
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Other things you should know regarding all this ... drama: Ginnifer Goodwin's character is a way successful attorney, and John Krasinski's Ethan her bestie and passively in love with her. In Emily Griffin's novel, there's Marcus, a raunchy comedic foil to all the interlaced tension, and here he's played by Steve Howey, who seems to enjoy being fifth billed in Kate Hudson's wedding-themed movies. Also, director Luke Greenfield hasn't been on the big-screen since 2004 (!) with The Girl Next Door (2004).

Jumping the Broom (2011)

112 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 56/100 (26 reviews)
Two very different families converge on Martha's Vineyard one weekend for a wedding.
Director:
Salim Akil
Stars:
Paula Patton, Laz Alonso, Angela Bassett, Loretta Devine
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: While we stand by, hoping that some filmmaker figures out how to make the talented and beautiful Paula Patton a household name, she leads this wedding party guided by director Salim Akil (TV's "Girlfriends" and its suddenly hot again spin-off "The Game") and writer Elizabeth Hunter ("Charmed" and "The L Word"). Working his faith-based magic is T.D. Jakes in producer mode, who scored with the romantic drama Not Easily Broken (2009) back in '09. To deny this movie is to deny an opportunity to see Angela Bassett on the big screen, and we try to never miss her. Something tells us this story could be continued as a TV show, too.

The Beaver (2011)

91 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 60/100 (40 reviews)
A troubled husband and executive adopts a beaver hand-puppet as his sole means of communicating.
Director:
Jodie Foster
Stars:
Mel Gibson, Jodie Foster, Anton Yelchin, Cherry Jones
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: For argument's sake: If Mel Gibson were completely stable, had never even raised his voice in his lifetime, and spent most of his off-camera time raising money for women's shelters, Jewish charities, and GLAAD, The Beaver would still be a near-impossible film to promote. And somehow, the trailer makes it look like a TV movie that got crossed with a puppet show and therapy session somewhere during filming. We'd rather watch the "Wonder Showzen" version of this screenplay, while we lament that Annette Bening wasn't considered for the Gibson role.

Last Night (2010)

93 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 50/100 (15 reviews)
The story follows a married couple, apart for a night while the husband takes a business trip with a colleague to whom he's attracted to. While he's resisting temptation, his wife encounters her past love.
Director:
Massy Tadjedin
Stars:
Keira Knightley, Sam Worthington, Eva Mendes, Guillaume Canet
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Massy Tadjedin's drama faced a lengthy delay as the picture was handed over from the new brass at Miramax to Tribeca Film's fledgling distribution arm. Story-wise, this seems like familiar terrain to us; granted, the cast is quite nice to look at. But really we'd root for a Worthington-Mendes hook-up, so perhaps tangly moral narratives are lost on us, unless there's lots of dark revelations about human nature, a la Closer (2004).

Hobo with a Shotgun (2011)

86 min   -   Action | Comedy | Thriller

Metascore: 55/100 (22 reviews)
A homeless vigilante blows away crooked cops, pedophile Santas, and other scumbags with his trusty pump-action shotgun.
Director:
Jason Eisener
Stars:
Rutger Hauer, Pasha Ebrahimi, Robb Wells, Brian Downey
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We're big fans of Jason Eisener's short Treevenge (2008) and are primed for his feature debut, which we missed at Sundance because screenings were packed and the reviews were great (though this does look like a perfect movie to see for free). Grindhouse tributes don't seem to fare well in theaters, but they rake it in online (VOD, downloads, etc.). We imagine this will play well at rep houses for years to come, too.

Passion Play (2010)

94 min   -   Drama | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 22/100 (11 reviews)
An angel under the thumb of a ruthless gangster is saved by a trumpet player down on his luck.
Director:
Mitch Glazer
Stars:
Mickey Rourke, Megan Fox, Bill Murray, Kelly Lynch
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Widely reported as the worst mainstream movie in quite some time, Passion Play has been granted a limited theatrical release (before it's end-of-May DVD debut). We're sure the release will satisfy select contractual obligations while helping earn back a fraction of the unspecified 7-figure sum that Image Entertainment shelled out an for rights to screenwriter Mitch Glazer's directorial debut. Personally, I think they should have opened the movie against Transformers 3 for hysteria's sake.

There Be Dragons (2011)

122 min   -   Biography | Drama | War

Metascore: 33/100 (19 reviews)
Arising out of the horror of the Spanish Civil War, a candidate for canonization is investigated by a journalist who discovers his own estranged father had a deep, dark and devastating connection to the saint's life.
Director:
Roland Joffé
Stars:
Charlie Cox, Wes Bentley, Dougray Scott, Rodrigo Santoro
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With an unwieldy historical narrative that touches upon themes of war, faith, betrayal, father-son intrigue, and death, Roland Joffé's latest -- which forces Wes Bentley into a fat suit during its contemporary setting -- sounds like it could make for a decent novel. Or a computer game? (PC only)

May 13 

Bridesmaids (2011)

125 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 75/100 (39 reviews)
Competition between the maid of honor and a bridesmaid, over who is the bride's best friend, threatens to upend the life of an out-of-work pastry chef.
Director:
Paul Feig
Stars:
Kristen Wiig, Maya Rudolph, Rose Byrne, Terry Crews
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This trailer is funnier than anything we've seen for The Hangover Part II (2011). Put on your worst dress, stow those Bride Wars (2009) comparisons, and prepare yourselves for the Judd Apatow version of the standard chick flick. As Kristen Wiig finally gets the chance to lead a cast, it's about time that Apatow used his producing powers to push female characters to the front; he's also working with Tiny Furniture (2010) creator/star Lena Dunham on an HBO project.

Priest (2011)

87 min   -   Action | Fantasy | Horror | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 41/100 (13 reviews)
A priest disobeys church law to track down the vampires who kidnapped his niece.
Director:
Scott Stewart
Stars:
Paul Bettany, Cam Gigandet, Maggie Q, Karl Urban
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Scott Stewart reunites with his Legion (2010) star for a second dooms-y horror-thriller. Sony has invested in the duo as well, putting out both movies via their gritty and genre-centric Screen Gems label, so I get the feeling modest box-office for either film will suffice. Scratch that. With the news that Priest is getting that 3D treatment, something tells me the studio is looking for a bigger return on Legion's admirable success (yes, we know it was trashed, but the thing made money).

Hesher (2010)

106 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 45/100 (26 reviews)
A young boy has lost his mother and is losing touch with his father and the world around him. Then he meets Hesher who manages to make his life even more chaotic.
Director:
Spencer Susser
Stars:
Joseph Gordon-Levitt, Devin Brochu, Natalie Portman, Rainn Wilson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Despite its layers of cool, from the ace cast to the presence of co-writer David Michod (he directed the excellent Animal Kingdom (2010)), Hesher represents just how tough it is to sell an unconventional story these days. And the mixed reviews -- some people have said director Spencer Susser has created a cinematic opera, while others say it's rife with indie clichés -- haven't helped the film's long road to its release. Personally, we're excited to watch it again, and again. Rainn Wilson plays against character as a depressed husband dealing with the loss of his wife, while Natalie Portman somehow manages to look plain and every day -- quite a feat given her beauty. And of course, Joseph G-L is, like, beyond perfect with his greasy hair and homemade tattoos.

Everything Must Go (2010)

97 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 65/100 (37 reviews)
When an alcoholic relapses, causing him to lose his wife and his job, he holds a yard sale on his front lawn in an attempt to start over. A new neighbor might be the key to his return to form.
Director:
Dan Rush
Stars:
Will Ferrell, Rebecca Hall, Christopher Jordan Wallace, Michael Peña
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Will Ferrell's gone indie in the past (Stranger Than Fiction (2006), Winter Passing (2005), etc.) but never with a role that feels so vital. A Raymond Carver short story in the hands of a first-time writer-director gives us pause, sure, but we're thinking the film might have more to do with Dan Rush's future career than Ferrell's. Besides, Notorious B.I.G.'s son co-stars alongside Rebecca Hall (suddenly, one of our favorite actresses), and Laura Dern shows up on the lawn for a bit, too.

Go for It! (2011)

105 min   -   Drama | Musical

Metascore: 44/100 (4 reviews)
A student must decide between follwing her dream to become a dancer, or to follow the wishes of her family.
Director:
Carmen Marron
Stars:
Aimee Garcia, Al Bandiero, Jossara Jinaro, Gina Rodriguez
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Que pasa, niños? Pantelion Films has stepped up 2 the niche marketing streets with films that court, more than anything, young people. Sure, their movies feature Latinos and Latinas, but their themes are universal right down to their latest release, a total teen dance fantasy that recalls everything made between "America's Best Dance Crew" and Flashdance.

Skateland (2010)

Director:
Anthony Burns
Stars:

Meanwhile, director Anthony Burns has received praise for finding a harmony between John Hughes themes of Richard Linklater listlessness. Plus we hear the cinematography is gorgeous.

Nanjing! Nanjing! (2009)

133 min   -   Drama | History | War

In 1937, Japan occupied Nanjing, the Chinese capital. There was a battle and subsequent atrocities against the inhabitants, especially those who took refuge in the International Security Zone.
Director:

Stars:

May 20 

Pirates of the Caribbean: On Stranger Tides (2011)

136 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 45/100 (39 reviews)
Jack Sparrow and Barbossa embark on a quest to find the elusive fountain of youth, only to discover that Blackbeard and his daughter are after it too.
Director:
Rob Marshall
Stars:
Johnny Depp, Penélope Cruz, Ian McShane, Geoffrey Rush
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: While we never thought the fourth chapter would happen with both Johnny Depp and director Gore Verbinski, we're happy they're still working together (who imagined that Rango (2011) would be both a critical and commercial hit?). We're also interested to see what Rob Marshall will do with a butch cast of characters -- after seeing his box-office fortunes diminish in the wake of Chicago (2002)'s success. Speaking of the cast, no offense to Will Turner or Elizabeth Swann, but could you ask for better new faces than Ian McShane and Penelope Cruz? Ditto Stephen Graham and Gemma Ward! Do the tepid critical reviews even matter here?

Midnight in Paris (2011)

94 min   -   Comedy | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 81/100 (40 reviews)
While on a trip to Paris with his fiancée's family, a nostalgic screenwriter finds himself mysteriously going back to the 1920s every day at midnight.
Director:
Woody Allen
Stars:
Owen Wilson, Rachel McAdams, Kathy Bates, Kurt Fuller
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Only in Woody Allen's movie world does an entire family travel to Paris on a business trip. But it appears that his latest European jaunt has a series of welcome surprises; mainly, the historic figures encountered by Owen Wilson's Gil. Bummer that Rachel McAdams is playing an unlikable character -- someone needs a breezy adult comedy, stat!

Louder Than a Bomb (2010)

99 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 77/100 (10 reviews)
By turns hopeful and heartbreaking, Louder Than a Bomb follows the fortunes of four Chicago-area high school poetry teams as they prepare for and compete in the world's largest youth slam.
Directors:
Greg Jacobs | Jon Siskel
Stars:
Kevin Coval, Adam Gottlieb, Elizabeth Graf, Kevin Harris
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: An audience favorite from the festival circuit, Greg Jacobs and Jon Siskel could have a word-of-mouth hit on their hands. The project's origin is worth sharing: Jacobs, citing how segregated Chicago is, noticed the line waiting for entry to the 2005 poetry slam finals outside a city rock club and the association with the Louder Than a Bomb organization was quickly formed. There really hasn't been a rousing, populist documentary in some time; we're rooting for this one alongside Oprah.

May 27 

The Hangover Part II (2011)

102 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 44/100 (40 reviews)
Two years after the bachelor party in Las Vegas, Phil, Stu, Alan, and Doug jet to Thailand for Stu's wedding. Stu's plan for a subdued pre-wedding brunch, however, goes seriously awry.
Director:
Todd Phillips
Stars:
Bradley Cooper, Zach Galifianakis, Ed Helms, Justin Bartha
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The man who once made a documentary about underground punk rock legend G.G. Allin now faces the pressure of following up the most successful R-rated comedy of all time. Meanwhile, Zach Galifianakis is a mainstream movie star who has retained his indie-comedy cred. We often joke that Hollywood isn't hip and has run out of ideas, but here's a bit of evidence to the contrary. If part two is anywhere near as good as the original -- and/or if it pulls a Terminator II (1989) and surpasses its predecessor -- we will eagerly await the proposed third and final chapter to complete this epic, drug-fueled, boyz-II-men trilogy.

Kung Fu Panda 2 (2011)

91 min   -   Animation | Action | Adventure | Comedy | Drama | Family

Metascore: 67/100 (31 reviews)
Po and his friends fight to stop a peacock villain from conquering China with a deadly new weapon, but first the Dragon Warrior must come to terms with his past.
Director:
Jennifer Yuh
Stars:
Jack Black, Angelina Jolie, Jackie Chan, Dustin Hoffman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: In all seriousness, isn't this the most welcome sequel of the year, next to The Hangover 2? DreamWorks Animation found their post-Shrek future with Po & co., and the fact that genre vet Jennifer Yuh gets to make her directorial debut here is one of the best Hollywood moves we've seen in a while, kung-fu or otherwise.

The Tree of Life (2011)

139 min   -   Drama | Fantasy

Metascore: 85/100 (43 reviews)
The story of a family in Waco, Texas in 1956. The eldest son witnesses the loss of innocence and struggles with his parents' conflicting teachings.
Director:
Terrence Malick
Stars:
Brad Pitt, Sean Penn, Jessica Chastain, Hunter McCracken
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We haven't seen Terrence Malick since his cameras tracked Colin Farrell and Q'orianka Kilcher around the Virginia coastline. While Cannes audiences either worshiped or derided the auteur's latest -- before ultimately laureling it with the Palme d'Or -- the Lars von Trier press conference drama/ugliness eclipsed the victory. Truth be known, Malick's heady style typically makes us swoon, and we're happy knowing he's busy shooting his next project already.
