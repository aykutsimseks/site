June 3 

X-Men: First Class (2011)

132 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 65/100 (38 reviews)
In 1962, the United States government enlists the help of Mutants with superhuman abilities to stop a malicious dictator who is determined to start World War III.
Director:
Matthew Vaughn
Stars:
James McAvoy, Michael Fassbender, Jennifer Lawrence, Kevin Bacon
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Just over a decade after X-Men (2000) comes this rather welcome prequel, with Bryan Singer in active producer mode and director Matthew Vaughn finally in the Marvel Universe. Ten years ago, your typical cab driver lacked the power to start a casting rumor, and we never would have thought Brett Ratner would help bring a franchise crashing to the ground. May this decade bring hope for a continuation of the original series and a good Wolverine sequel.

Beginners (2010)

105 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 81/100 (36 reviews)
A young man is rocked by two announcements from his elderly father: that he has terminal cancer, and that he has a young male lover.
Director:
Mike Mills
Stars:
Ewan McGregor, Christopher Plummer, Mélanie Laurent, Goran Visnjic
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Thumbsucker (2005) was almost a great movie, and now writer-director Mike Mills is back with an attention-getting premise and wildly anticipated performances from Christopher Plummer, Ewan McGregor, and Melanie Laurent. Despite being teased at the Toronto Film Festival, most of the advance word here compliments the film's visual style and realistic portrayal of familial and romantic relationships. Definitely all part of a big year for Mr. Plummer.

Submarine (2010)

97 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 76/100 (37 reviews)
15-year-old Oliver Tate has two objectives: To lose his virginity before his next birthday, and to extinguish the flame between his mother and an ex-lover who has resurfaced in her life.
Director:
Richard Ayoade
Stars:
Craig Roberts, Sally Hawkins, Paddy Considine, Noah Taylor
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Attention-getting since its debut at Toronto, this oddball coming-of-age comedy from director Richard Ayoade -- who already has a cult audience for his work in front of the TV camera ("The IT Crowd") and behind it ("The Mighty Boosh") -- caught good air at Sundance even with people who lazily tagged it as an homage to Wes Anderson/Hal Ashby. Despite cribbing from a number of overt influences, Submarine`s focus on a transitional month in the life of adolescent is nevertheless unique, trust me. And his girlfriend is totally bonkers; I'm obsessed with her character.

Beautiful Boy (2010)

100 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 62/100 (25 reviews)
A married couple on the verge of separation are leveled by the news their 18-year-old son committed a mass shooting at his college, then took his own life.
Director:
Shawn Ku
Stars:
Michael Sheen, Maria Bello, Kyle Gallner, Logan South
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Shawn Ku's script was inspired by an unfortunate personal event (a friend passed away in his home and he was the person to tell his parents) as well as the shootings at Virginia Tech (where his parents met) in April 2007. Recent dramas Rabbit Hole (2010) and Trust (2010) have received a lot of press attention but failed to draw in audiences due to their heavy subject matter, and, sadly, we're thinking Ku's feature debut will suffer the same fate during the crowded summer season. But we're thinking the film might chart with Independent Spirit Awards voters?

Rejoice and Shout (2010)

115 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 72/100 (17 reviews)
A documentary on Gospel music's 200-year history.
Director:
Don McGlynn
Stars:
Smokey Robinson, Mavis Staples, Willa Ward, Bill Carpenter
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With so many of today's singers referencing and namechecking their Gospel roots, it's only fitting to have a documentary made available for devotees and neophytes alike. We do understand, though, that Don McGlynn's work is more of an insider's companion than a primer for those who aren't well-versed in the musical genre.

June 10 

Super 8 (2011)

112 min   -   Mystery | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 72/100 (41 reviews)
During the summer of 1979, a group of friends witness a train crash and investigate subsequent unexplained events in their small town.
Director:
J.J. Abrams
Stars:
Elle Fanning, AJ Michalka, Kyle Chandler, Joel Courtney
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From his cult following and mainstream appeal, to his secretive ways and dedication to the art of storytelling, J.J. Abrams could be the best commercial film (and TV) maker working today. Digging into his personal nostalgia -- he was a kid raised on the movies of Steven Spielberg, and by now the tale of how teenage Abrams repaired three of Spielberg's early 8 mm films really is one of the best stories out of Hollywood in ages -- the man who rebooted Star Trek and gave us Felicity Porter might just land the best film of the summer (though X-Men: First Class is excellent).



Judy Moody and the Not Bummer Summer (2011)

91 min   -   Comedy | Family

Metascore: 37/100 (23 reviews)
Third grader Judy Moody sets out to have the most thrilling summer of her life.
Director:
John Schultz
Stars:
Jordana Beatty, Heather Graham, Parris Mosteller, Janet Varney
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Confined to the pages of best-selling books for the last decade, the plucky elementary school adventurer from Megan McDonald's kid-lit series gets the big-screen treatment. Boys now have two Diary of a Wimpy Kid movies, so it's only fair that girls get their own heroine -- especially since Ramona Quimby didn't live up to expectations. Great weekend counterprogramming, too.

Trollhunter (2010)

103 min   -   Comedy | Fantasy | Horror

Metascore: 61/100 (25 reviews)
A group of students investigates a series of mysterious bear killings, but learns that there are much more dangerous things going on. They start to follow a mysterious hunter, learning that he is actually a troll hunter.
Director:
André Øvredal
Stars:
Otto Jespersen, Robert Stoltenberg, Knut Nærum, Glenn Erland Tosterud
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: May more Nordic horror films make it to the U.S. This has been a favorite with genre fans, geeks, and people who have just happened to catch it at film festivals. With much love for Jim Henson and DIY special effects, André Øvredal's monster movie/disaster film also gives the shaky-cam subgenre a much-needed polish.

The Trip (2010)

107 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Steve Coogan has been asked by The Observer to tour the country's finest restaurants, but after his girlfriend backs out on him he must take his best friend and source of eternal aggravation, Rob Brydon.
Director:
Michael Winterbottom
Stars:
Steve Coogan, Rob Brydon, Paul Popplewell, Margo Stilley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The Tristram Shandy: A Cock and Bull Story (2005) trio reunite for an improvised road comedy in which Coogan and Brydon play exaggerated versions of themselves and, we sense, everything from Hollywood to food culture to maleness is skewered. Sounds delightful, though we hear it truly works better as a TV show (director Michael Winterbottom edited together the film from the BBC series).

Bride Flight (2008)

130 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 60/100 (19 reviews)
A romantic drama that charts the lives of three women from different backgrounds, forever changed when they emigrate to New Zealand as war brides.
Director:
Ben Sombogaart
Stars:
Karina Smulders, Anna Drijver, Elise Schaap, Pleuni Touw
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: First released back in 2008, De tweeling (2002) director Ben Sombogaart's decades-spanning melodrama earns a U.S. release from Music Box Films. Stateside reviewers have noted its post-feminist themes and graphic sex scenes, but we haven't seen any raves for this one -- though we understand older audiences are embracing it. Rutger Hauer cameos, too.

Viva Riva! (2010)

98 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 65/100 (16 reviews)
Riva is an operator, a man with charm and ambition in equal measure. Kinshasa is an inviting place. With petrol in short supply in DRC's capital, he and his sidekick pursue a plot to get hold of a secret cache - barrels of fuel they can sell for a huge profit. Of course they're not the only ones who want the stuff. Cesar is a ruthless, sharply dressed foreigner thriving in Kinshasa's lawless streets. A female military officer joins the fray. Even the church will betray its tenets for a piece of the action. But Riva's main nemesis is Azor, a crime boss in the classic style: big, decadent and brutal. He's not a man to mess with, but his girlfriend, Nora, may just be the most seductive woman in all of DRC. Riva catches sight of her dancing at a nightclub and it's not long before Nora matches the fuel cache as a coveted object of his lust.
Director:
Djo Munga
Stars:
Patsha Bay, Manie Malone, Diplome Amekindra, Hoji Fortuna
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This gangster tale has been championed as the first film to come out of the Democratic Republic of the Congo in roughly 25 years. The republic's former anti-art government has been documented in the press, as director Djo Tunda Wa Munga has spoken on his voyage out of and back to his native country (short story: he studied film in Belgium for years, then return to the Congo as part of a BBC documentary crew). As he's made the rounds with his debut film, Munga has elegantly spoken about his country's artistic legacy as well as the international talent who helped make his feature.

June 17 

Green Lantern (2011)

114 min   -   Action | Adventure | Romance | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 39/100 (39 reviews)
Reckless test pilot Hal Jordan is granted an alien ring that bestows him with otherworldly powers that inducts him into an intergalactic police force, the Green Lantern Corps.
Director:
Martin Campbell
Stars:
Ryan Reynolds, Blake Lively, Peter Sarsgaard, Mark Strong
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: At one point, this was the most anticipated superhero film of the year, but after the entirely welcome X-Men: First Class (2011) and the surprisingly effective Thor (2011), Hal Jordan and company are facing a crash landing at the box office since the early reviews are reinforcing what fans have thought all along: this is one misguided, CGI-gooey super-mess. It's kind of sad, really, since the project got off the ground with such promise at Comic-Con last year. Let's see how it does worldwide, though.

Mr. Popper's Penguins (2011)

94 min   -   Comedy | Family | Fantasy

Metascore: 53/100 (30 reviews)
The life of a businessman begins to change after he inherits six penguins, and as he transforms his apartment into a winter wonderland, his professional side starts to unravel.
Director:
Mark Waters
Stars:
Jim Carrey, Carla Gugino, Angela Lansbury, Ophelia Lovibond
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I ♥ this cast almost as much as I loved the book as a kid. In the hands of Jim Carrey and director Mark Waters, the contemporary retelling should translate with style. And a smart move on downsizing the original 12 penguins by half for the movie -- no person or animal can take up Carrey's frame! Did you know that Ben Stiller and Noah Baumbach almost teamed up for this project? But no one wanted to see a penguin have a mid-life crisis.

The Art of Getting By (2011)

83 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 36/100 (28 reviews)
George, a lonely and fatalistic teen who has made it all the way to his senior year without ever having done a real day of work, is befriended by Sally, a popular but complicated girl who recognizes in him a kindred spirit.
Director:
Gavin Wiesen
Stars:
Freddie Highmore, Emma Roberts, Michael Angarano, Sasha Spielberg
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The prep-school mindset of writer-director Gavin Wiesen asserts that nothing comes after high school if you don't immediately go to college. That is Art's lesser flaw; the movie's most noticeable pimple is: it plays like a primer of every indie romantic comedy that's preceded it. Like, the best thing Wiesen did refraining from calling Emma Roberts's character "Zooey". Kind of a waste of a fun cast, and personally, once Alicia Silverstone arrived as Freddie Highmore's frustrated English teacher, all I thought about is how is still one of the best high-school comedies ever made.

Buck (2011)

88 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 76/100 (31 reviews)
An examination of the life of acclaimed 'horse whisperer' Buck Brannaman, who recovered from years of child abuse to become a well-known expert in the interactions between horses and people.
Director:
Cindy Meehl
Stars:
Buck Brannaman, Gary Myers, Bibb Frazier, Betty Staley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Buck Brannaman is a study in Cowboy Zen and anyone fortunate to catch Cindy Meehl's documentary on the blue-collar human trainer -- like the Sundance audiences, who lauded it -- might consider applying his philosophies and practices to their interactions with animals and people alike. Something tells me this will get a second look once Project Nim (2011) is released.

Page One: Inside the New York Times (2011)

92 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 68/100 (31 reviews)
Unprecedented access to the New York Times newsroom yields a complex view of the transformation of a media landscape fraught with both peril and opportunity.
Director:
Andrew Rossi
Stars:
David Carr, Sarah Ellison, Larry Ingrassia, Dennis Crowley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With news-room pacing and rousing accounts from reporter David Carr (he's the Anthony Bourdain of the the Times), this window into journalism's fight for thee Rs (readers, revenue, relevancy) in the age of Facebook and Daily Kos is a perfect jumping off point for a wonderfully nerdy conversation about how the Internet is shaping/destroying/reimagining the medium.

June 24 

Cars 2 (2011)

106 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Sport

Metascore: 57/100 (38 reviews)
Star race car Lightning McQueen and his pal Mater head overseas to compete in the World Grand Prix race. But the road to the championship becomes rocky as Mater gets caught up in an intriguing adventure of his own: international espionage.
Directors:
John Lasseter | Brad Lewis
Stars:
Owen Wilson, Larry the Cable Guy, Michael Caine, Emily Mortimer
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Race fans have been quick to call out Pixar and Disney for selling out, but who were all those people who snapped up Cars merchandise -- a major factor in the greenlighting of this sequel? Though this is arguably the first bonafide family movie of the season, we're curious as to why Pixar felt the need to announce a new, mysterious project this week ... Then again, box-office predictions are up in the air now that Green Lantern underperformed and Bridesmaids has become the season's most enduring release.

Bad Teacher (2011)

92 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 47/100 (38 reviews)
A lazy, incompetent middle school teacher who hates her job and her students is forced to return to her job to make enough money for a boob job after her rich fiancé dumps her.
Director:
Jake Kasdan
Stars:
Cameron Diaz, Jason Segel, Justin Timberlake, Lucy Punch
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Personally, after Bridesmaids and The Hangover Part II, a break from hard-R comedy wouldn't be so bad. However, I expect a torrent of nasty one-liners will serve as Cameron D's best-received on-screen role since ... yikes ... let's just say I'm looking forward to her next project, the Gambit (2012) remake penned by the Coen Bros. Jake Kasdan has the experience (he's directed Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story (2007) and Orange County (2002)) but I don't think he's made a great comedy yet, and screenwriting team Lee Eisenberg and Gene Stupnitsky didn't make the most hilarious transition from the small-screen ("The Office") to the big (Year One (2009)). While I've been waiting for a high-school comedy that focuses more on teachers than students, the redband trailer's humor feels a bit forced and, frankly, Justin Timberlake's character seems piped in from another dimension. Jason Segel to the rescue?

A Better Life (2011)

98 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 64/100 (30 reviews)
A gardener in East L.A. struggles to keep his son away from gangs and immigration agents while trying to give his son the opportunities he never had.
Director:
Chris Weitz
Stars:
Demian Bichir, José Julián, Eddie 'Piolin' Sotelo, Joaquín Cosio
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: After directing The Golden Compass (2007) and The Twilight Saga: New Moon (2009), Chris Weitz looks to be distancing himself from fantasy land with this real-life drama nestled into the middle of the summer blockbuster season. Lead Demian Bichir portrayed Fidel Castro in Steven Soderbergh's Che: Part One (2008) and recently scored a role in Oliver Stone's hopefully awesome next project, Savages (2012).

Conan O'Brien Can't Stop (2011)

89 min   -   Documentary | Comedy | Music

Metascore: 64/100 (25 reviews)
A documentary on Conan O'Brien's comedy tour of the U.S. and Canada after leaving his post at "The Tonight Show" and severing his relationship with NBC.
Director:
Rodman Flender
Stars:
Conan O'Brien, Andy Richter, Stephen Colbert, Jimmy Vivino
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Though we now know that Zach Galifianakis tried to get kicked off the set of "Tru Calling", Conan O'Brien is still the funniest man in recent history to lose his television job. And we can understand why he now has trouble watching this documentary, shot by Rodman Flender, his associate since their Harvard Lampoon days, which began days after O'Brien severed his relationship with NBC.

Año bisiesto (2010)

94 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 71/100 (12 reviews)
Laura's personal life consists of one affair after another. She meets Arturo, and the pair enter into an intense, violent sexual relationship. As days go by, Laura crosses out the days on a calendar, revealing her secret past to her lover.
Director:
Michael Rowe
Stars:
Monica del Carmen, Gustavo Sánchez Parra, Armando Hernández, Diego Chas
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Michael Rowe's debut film won the Camera d'Or at Cannes earlier this year, and star Mónica Del Carmen won a Best Actress Ariel (Mexico's Oscars) for what seems like a psychologically trying performance -- for the actress and the audience alike. Expect gratuitous sex of the not-so-gentle kind, as well as gorgeously composed shots on a minimalist set.

Turtle: The Incredible Journey (2009)

81 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 56/100 (7 reviews)
A little loggerhead turtle follows in the path of her ancestors on one of the most extraordinary journeys in the natural world. Born on a beach in Florida, she rides the Gulf Stream all the way to the frozen north and ultimately swims around the entire North Atlantic to Africa and back to the beach where she was born.
Director:
Nick Stringer
Stars:
Miranda Richardson, Hannelore Elsner, Georgina Verbaan
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Warner Bros. and Disney have some new competition as SeaWorld gets into the nature doc business in association with Hannover House, who retreated from the box office after Twelve (2010) earned distinction as one of 2011's worst performers. It's a smart-about face for Hannover and potentially the beginning of a profitable streak.

The Names of Love (2010)

100 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 62/100 (19 reviews)
A young, extroverted left-wing activist who sleeps with her political opponents to convert them to her cause is successful until she meets her match.
Director:
Michel Leclerc
Stars:
Sara Forestier, Jacques Gamblin, Zinedine Soualem, Carole Franck
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This Caesar-winning comedy showcases Sara Forestier's talents, and we'll see her again in a supporting role in Gainsbourg: A Heroic Life (2010) this fall. Look for everything from older-younger relationship dynamics to Arab-Jewish relations to be explored/riffed upon.
