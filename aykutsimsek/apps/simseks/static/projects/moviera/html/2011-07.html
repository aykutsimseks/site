July 1 

Transformers: Dark of the Moon (2011)

154 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 42/100 (37 reviews)
The Autobots learn of a Cybertronian spacecraft hidden on the moon, and race against the Decepticons to reach it and to learn its secrets.
Director:
Michael Bay
Stars:
Shia LaBeouf, Rosie Huntington-Whiteley, Tyrese Gibson, Josh Duhamel
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Remember when Michael Bay and Roland Emmerich each had some sort of identity crisis and pledged to make smaller movies outside of the action genre? Emmerich managed to break free, at least for one project, and we'll see his curious looking political thriller Anonymous (2011) later this year, but Bay couldn't say no to a third Transformers movie and the promise of worldwide domination it guarantees. The funny thing for us here: This is the first time we're anticipating a Transformers storyline, if only to discover what happens to Mikaela Banes. And let's see if Bay makes good on his promise to delve deeper into the toy line's mythos, while exploring darker emotional realms of his human characters as well.

Watch Trailer

However, we love that bosom bud Peter Scolari is in the cast!

Director:
Michael Rapaport
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Michael Rapaport's documentary is more than a love letter and nowhere near a vanity project; it's a knowing, gritty study of Tribe's rise to the top and their influence on a generation of music lovers -- including Kanye and others who are dedicated to keeping commercial hip-hop an evolving art form. From Q-Tip to D'Angelo, many of the hip-hop/R&B innovators from the '90s are addled in the present, though we will continue to hope for a Native Tongue/neo-soul renaissance.

101 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Director:
Matthew Chapman
Stars:
Charlie Hunnam, Terrence Howard, Patrick Wilson, Liv Tyler
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I'm always intrigued by a film that gets such awful longform reviews instead of mere pithy dismissals; some of the most read sites and writes really want you to scroll down to get the full gist of their dislike for Matthew Chapman's self-described philosophical thriller. Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 2 (2011)

130 min   -   Adventure | Drama | Fantasy | Mystery

Metascore: 87/100 (41 reviews)
Harry, Ron and Hermione search for Voldemort's remaining Horcruxes in their effort to destroy the Dark Lord as the final battle rages on at Hogwarts.
Director:
David Yates
Stars:
Daniel Radcliffe, Emma Watson, Rupert Grint, Michael Gambon
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Yes, it's taken ten years (!) and eight (!!) movies to reach the final climax to the Harry Potter film series, so let's take a moment to reflect upon the unprecendented achievement made by the filmmakers, cast, crew and studio, and the commitment everyone has made for the past decade to get this body of work made. Children have grown into adults, directors have come and gone, and still, the myth and legend remains as potent as ever....



There, are you done reflecting yet? Now onto the real meat of it: director Yates is going to need to pull out the stops on this one to make it the showstopper everyone wants it to be. Most everyone still thinks the series peaked with Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban, so it'll take some doing to get J.K. Rowling's rather, erm, lugubrious ending into fine cinematic form. Bonus points for making it suspenseful when practically the entire world knows how it ends!



P.S. We will indeed miss Harry being a presence in our film lives and part of almost every major feature we've done for the beginning of this young century. And if we're a mite melancholy, imagine how Warner Bros. must feel, their golden boy finally graduating!
