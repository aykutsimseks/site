October 7 

Real Steel (2011)

127 min   -   Action | Drama | Sci-Fi | Sport

Metascore: 56/100 (34 reviews)
In the near future, robot boxing is a top sport. A struggling promoter feels he's found a champion in a discarded robot.
Director:
Shawn Levy
Stars:
Hugh Jackman, Evangeline Lilly, Dakota Goyo, Anthony Mackie
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The funniest thing we've read about this movie is a message board post that proclaims in 20 years, no one will be watching boxing. Quite prophetic in these days where cage fighting rules. Seems like the idea here is to engage audiences with Iron Man/Avatar/Transformers-like creations and the human elements found in your typical sports drama. Or something? I feel like Hugh Jackman is going to have to work hard (again) to sell this idea as something cooler than Rock 'Em Sock 'Em Robots: The Movie, which was in development recently.

Dirty Girl (2010)

90 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 37/100 (13 reviews)

Director:
Abe Sylvia
Stars:
Juno Temple, Jeremy Dozier, Milla Jovovich, Zach Lasry
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I've heard remarkably mixed reviews for Abe Slyvia's late-80s comedy, which sounds like a "Glee" prequel with a filthy mouth and parents you can love (Milla Jovovich and Mary Steenburgen) and hate (Dwight Yoakum, playing Clarke's homophobic dad). Juno Temple is going to be, like, in every movie that comes out in the next 12 months, but we don't think this is her best indie side -- that'll be in Little Birds (2011), which will hopefully be released this fall.

October 14 

The Thing (2011)

103 min   -   Horror | Mystery | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 49/100 (31 reviews)
At an Antarctica research site, the discovery of an alien craft leads to a confrontation between graduate student Kate Lloyd and scientist Dr. Sander Halvorson.
Director:
Matthijs van Heijningen Jr.
Stars:
Mary Elizabeth Winstead, Joel Edgerton, Ulrich Thomsen, Eric Christian Olsen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With all the mystery surrounding the Alien spin-off, I forgot about this origin story that will show what happened on the Norwegian research base in Antarctic right before the opening events of John Carpenter's 1982 sci-fi/horror classic. Early in the project's development, Ronald D. Moore, beloved by genre fans for reviving "Battlestar Galactica" and creating the "Caprica" spin-off, was helping craft the story, but his name is nowhere to be found on the final product -- not the most encouraging sign.

113 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Music | Romance

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Smart career move for Zac Efron to cut loose from starring in this project, though we wish that his first replacement, "Gossip Girl's" Chace Crawford, remained on board to work with writer/director Craig Brewer (Hustle & Flow); we think Crawford's true-life bad boy antics (smoking weed in Texas, getting blotto in Europe with Mr. Schuester from "Glee") is the kind of realness we seek out in certain movies. This could be a modest or breakout hit, though modern day Ren MacCormack doesn't have a follow-up movie gig in place yet ...

Up next: his first English-language project?

The Big Year (2011)

100 min   -   Comedy

Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We're on quirk-alert until we see exactly how Mark Obmascik's book is recreated as a film. And, yes, we think it sounds like a Wes Anderson plot, too, though really its another family friendly union between Owen Wilson and his Marley & Me (2008) director. We're hoping the comedy is on point since the cast is truly something to be treasured. And if you are amazed that Jack Black is starring in another picture for Fox after Gulliver's Travels (2010), consider that the diminutive remake shook off its frosty reception in the States and managed to rack up almost $200 million overseas -- another example of the worldwide box office's strength and influence.

This foursome (Worthington, Morgan, Moretz, and Chastain) should reunite for something more arty.

Director Trent Cooper is back, five years after offering up Larry the Cable Guy: Health Inspector (2006), an unenviable gig.

Fireflies in the Garden (2008)

Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Julia Roberts is all about filmmaker Dennis Lee; aside from starring in Fireflies, she's produced Lee's other feature, Jesus Henry Christ (2012), which has yet to land a distributor. Similarly, this long delayed indie, which probably works best if you're on Xanax, has aged nearly three years between its completion and release. Wait, are we in for a repeat of Margaret (2011)'s epic path toward thoroughly underwhelming box office numbers?

Directors:
Henry Joost | Ariel Schulman
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I love the overt nods to Poltergeist (1982), and the effects and stuntwork are jarring enough to keep me interested in how this story begins. Interesting how directors Henry Joost and Ariel Schulman have quietly handled the project after their Catfish (2010) stunt backfired.

110 min   -   Action | Adventure | Romance

Metascore: 35/100 (15 reviews)

Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: A tip of the cavalier hat to Paul W.S. Totally love this cast for many reasons: Anderson and Milla Jovovich are our favorite husband/wife duo next to Vincent Cassel and Monica Bellucci; Christoph Waltz and Mads Mikkelsen are the kind of import attractions that Hollywood desperately needs; this will test Logan Lerman's mettle as a young leading man -- especially in the action/adventure genre. For industry watchers, this is a litmus test for Summit's soon-to-be-post-Twilight sticking power.

October 28 

Puss in Boots (2011)

90 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family

Metascore: 65/100 (24 reviews)
The events leading up to the sword fighting cat's meeting with Shrek and his friends.
Director:
Chris Miller
Stars:
Antonio Banderas, Salma Hayek, Zach Galifianakis, Billy Bob Thornton
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Are spin-offs more cost effective and less fan-provoking than reboots? With so many franchises at or approaching their respective ends, and no true successors on the horizon (see: The Golden Compass (2007) through The Last Airbender (2010)), spin-offs have come into focus. Soon we will see if Jason Bourne and Ethan Hunt can be replaced (by characters both played by Jeremy Renner); meanwhile, the Twilight saga is (was?) mulling over spin-off ideas. Back to Puss, though: originally conceived for a DVD release, the wily feline will test the Shrek universe's mettle, though the movie was under the radar until recently. The cat is cooler than the ogre, right?

In Time (2011)

109 min   -   Action | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 53/100 (36 reviews)
In a future where people stop aging at 25, but are engineered to live only one more year, having the means to buy your way out of the situation is a shot at immortal youth. Here, Will Salas finds himself accused of murder and on the run with a hostage - a connection that becomes an important part of the way against the system.
Director:
Andrew Niccol
Stars:
Justin Timberlake, Amanda Seyfried, Cillian Murphy, Olivia Wilde
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If there ever were a metaphor for trying to remain famous in Hollywood... I feel like Andrew Niccol is poised to make a classic thriller someday (and yes, I love Gattaca (1997) and even have a fondness for Lord of War (2005)); hopefully he can he fashion this Logan's Run-esque premise into something transcendent, and audiences will buy into a complex storyline where Olivia Wilde is Justin Timberlake's mother. (Also, for what it's worth: one of the reasons we love Justin Timberlake is that he never writes or performs songs for movies he's in.) Is Now really going to be the final title?

The Rum Diary (2011)

120 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Director:
Bruce Robinson
Stars:
Johnny Depp, Giovanni Ribisi, Aaron Eckhart, Michael Rispoli
Watch Trailer

And Pirates logistics were just part of this film's bumpy road to the big screen. An extensive post-production (filming completed over 2 years ago), distribution dilemmas and the unexpected death of its creator, Hunter S. Thompson, make Depp's return to his Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas (1998) form one of the more anticipated performances of the year, and also bittersweet.

Anonymous (2011)

130 min   -   Drama | History | Thriller

Metascore: 50/100 (43 reviews)
The theory that it was in fact Edward De Vere, Earl of Oxford, who penned Shakespeare's plays. Set against the backdrop of the succession of Queen Elizabeth I and the Essex rebellion against her.
Director:
Roland Emmerich
Stars:
Rhys Ifans, Vanessa Redgrave, David Thewlis, Sebastian Armesto
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Easily the most daring major-studio release of the year (and perhaps the most ill-advised?), Anonymous represents Roland Emmerich's brief respite from action filmmaking of diminishing returns, and the first time that Rhys Ifans has toplined a mainstream project. Did Ifans get a new agent or something, because he's about to be everywhere - from The Lizard in the Spider-Man reboot to, allegedly, Catweazle and (in the best bit of indie casting ever) as The Teardrop Explodes singer Julian Cope. Meanwhile, I guess we'll see the play Richard II staged in some way?
