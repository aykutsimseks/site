November 4 

A Very Harold & Kumar 3D Christmas (2011)

90 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 61/100 (29 reviews)
Six years after their Guantanamo Bay adventure, stoner buds Harold Lee and Kumar Patel cause a holiday fracas by inadvertently burning down Harold's father-in-law's prize Christmas tree.
Director:
Todd Strauss-Schulson
Stars:
Kal Penn, John Cho, Neil Patrick Harris, Patton Oswalt
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's been three years in real time but six in Harold/Kumar time, and a lot has changed for Kal Penn, who famously went to work for the Obama Administration, while Jon Cho took on an iconic "Star Trek" character in the J.J. Abrams reboot. Their cult audience has grown in the interim, necessitating this third (and final, we sense) chapter -- a story that sounds a bit like a Greg Focker misadventure.

Tower Heist (2011)

104 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime

Metascore: 59/100 (39 reviews)
When a group of hard-working guys find out they've fallen victim to their wealthy employer's Ponzi scheme, they conspire to rob his high-rise residence.
Director:
Brett Ratner
Stars:
Eddie Murphy, Ben Stiller, Casey Affleck, Alan Alda
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The current Wall Street situation makes Ponzi schemes seem like a smaller yet related matter. First called Trump Heist and described as an "urban Ocean's 11" (uggs), this project has been around since the days when Brett Ratner almost directed the Conan the Barbarian reboot. In the interim, the project went through two handfuls' worth of writers as an eyebrow-raising cast was assembled. One could argue (and we will) that scripts don't matter when Ratner is in the director's chair, so why spend the money on all those rewrites?

The Son of No One (2011)

90 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 36/100 (18 reviews)
A young cop is assigned to a precinct in the working class neighborhood where he grew up, and an old secret threatens to destroy his life and his family.
Director:
Dito Montiel
Stars:
Channing Tatum, Al Pacino, Juliette Binoche, James Ransone
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Dito Montiel and Channing Tatum might want to take a time out and work separately for a while since the union that began so promisingly with A Guide to Recognizing Your Saints (2006) has lost its critical appeal. That's a kind way of saying the No One was the subject of Sundance melodrama when initial reports spread about boos/walkouts upon its debut. I was hoping for a major re-edit followed by a triumphant theatrical run, but the Anchor Bay distribution deal pretty much reduced my low expectations by half.

Killing Bono (2011)

114 min   -   Comedy | Music

Metascore: 46/100 (12 reviews)
Two brothers attempt to become global rock stars but can only look on as old school friends U2 become the biggest band in the world.
Director:
Nick Hamm
Stars:
Ben Barnes, Robert Sheehan, Krysten Ritter, Ralph Brown
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The real U2 were so supportive of this satire, they allowed their music, likenesses, even their early concert fliers to be used in the film. (Imagine the lawsuits filed against the production if this project had been based on, say, Metallica.) Ben Barnes is well known to American audiences as Prince Caspian, and his co-star/fictional brother Robert Sheehan is a rising Euro star, but I think the awareness levels for this rock 'n' roll failure tale are rather low, unfortunately.

Young Goethe in Love (2010)

102 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 55/100 (18 reviews)
After aspiring poet Johann Wolfgang von Goethe fails his law exams, he's sent to a sleepy provincial court to reform. Instead, he falls for Lotte, a young woman who is promised to another man.
Director:
Philipp Stölzl
Stars:
Alexander Fehling, Miriam Stein, Moritz Bleibtreu, Volker Bruch
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This period romance doesn't have the kind of festival laurels that tend to lure art house and older audiences, but perhaps the subject of Goethe himself is enough of an attraction, though, like most men, he was more compelling in his later years. I did like director Philipp Stozl's previous film, North Face (2008).

Elle s'appelait Sarah (2010)

111 min   -   Drama | War

Metascore: 59/100 (30 reviews)
In modern-day Paris, a journalist finds her life becoming entwined with a young girl whose family was torn apart during the notorious Vel' d'Hiv Roundup in 1942.
Director:
Gilles Paquet-Brenner
Stars:
Kristin Scott Thomas, Mélusine Mayance, Niels Arestrup, Frédéric Pierrot
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The Weinstein Company's 2011 roster has whiffed at the box office, except for this adaptation of Tatiana De Rosnay's best-seller, hence the re-release this weekend and a crossing of fingers for a Kristen Scott Thomas Oscar nomination.

November 11 

Immortals (2011)

110 min   -   Action | Drama | Fantasy

Metascore: 46/100 (23 reviews)
Theseus is a mortal man chosen by Zeus to lead the fight against the ruthless King Hyperion, who is on a rampage across Greece to obtain a weapon that can destroy humanity.
Director:
Tarsem Singh
Stars:
Henry Cavill, Mickey Rourke, John Hurt, Stephen Dorff
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We love it when a director has the freedom and bravado to say that his new project will be "Caravaggio (1986) meets Fight Club (1999)" while he outfits his hero with leather trousers and a Coppertone complexion. Reviewers who have taken swipes at the weak storytelling seem to have forgotten, conveniently, that a strong narrative construct is director Tarsem's Achilles' heel.

J. Edgar (2011)

137 min   -   Biography | Crime | Drama

Metascore: 59/100 (42 reviews)
J. Edgar Hoover, powerful head of the F.B.I. for nearly 50 years, looks back on his professional and personal life.
Director:
Clint Eastwood
Stars:
Leonardo DiCaprio, Armie Hammer, Naomi Watts, Josh Hamilton
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The winter movie-going season is made for films such as this, Clint Eastwood's revealing biography on FBI honcho/probable homosexual J. Edgar Hoover starring an A+ list actor who has had to dodge certain rumors his entire career. Co-starring as Clyde Tolson, Hoover's alleged lover, is Armie Hammer, last seen in duplicate in The Social Network (2010); what a handsome suspected couple! Both director Eastwood and screenwriter Dustin Lance Black get the opportunity to rebound from recent stumbles.

Melancholia (2011)

130 min   -   Drama | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 80/100 (40 reviews)
Two sisters find their already strained relationship challenged as a mysterious new planet threatens to collide with Earth.
Director:
Lars von Trier
Stars:
Kirsten Dunst, Charlotte Gainsbourg, Kiefer Sutherland, Alexander Skarsgård
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Good news: Lars von Trier seems to respect women this time around! Bad news: his stumbly Nazi commentary at Cannes saw support for the film and its director waver, then Norwegian extremist/assassin Anders Behring Breivik named Dogville (2003) as his favorite movie. But we don't think this unfortunate circumstance prevent a second wave of audiences and critics from embracing Kirsten Dunst's performance as a troubled newlywed whose stability unravels as a planet ten times the size of Earth threatens to collide with us.

Jack and Jill (2011)

91 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 23/100 (26 reviews)
Family guy Jack Sadelstein prepares for the annual event he dreads: the Thanksgiving visit of his twin sister, the needy and passive-aggressive Jill, who then refuses to leave.
Director:
Dennis Dugan
Stars:
Adam Sandler, Katie Holmes, Al Pacino, Elodie Tougne
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: "Gap Girls"? Still funny today. Sandler in drag in his mid-40s? He better be lit exceptionally well.

London Boulevard (2010)

103 min   -   Crime | Drama

Metascore: 52/100 (13 reviews)
An ex-con with a reputation tries to go straight by working as a handy man for a reclusive actress but this is unfortunately not the wish of London's underground crime lord.
Director:
William Monahan
Stars:
Colin Farrell, Keira Knightley, Ray Winstone, David Thewlis
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Screenwriter William Monahan's directorial debut looks and feels like vintage Guy Ritchie, and I thoroughly support a face off between Colin Farrell and Ray Winstone -- even though they would be the ones to fall in love in my gangster fantasy. Keira's a fox, but the neurotic act is totally tired!

Into the Abyss (2011)

107 min   -   Documentary | Crime

Metascore: 74/100 (30 reviews)
Conversations with death row inmate Michael Perry and those affected by his crime serve as an examination of why people - and the state - kill.
Director:
Werner Herzog
Stars:
Werner Herzog, Richard Lopez, Michael Perry, Damon Hall
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Interestingly, Werner Herzog's most recent documentary, Cave of Forgotten Dreams (2010), was his greatest box office performance to date, and now his death row survey is being positioned for a Best Documentary Oscar after Herzog convinced IFC that his end-of-year distribution plan was better than the studio's blueprint. You might have heard that he'll serve as the villain in Jack Reacher (2012) opposite Tom Cruise as well.

Elite Squad: The Enemy Within (2010)

115 min   -   Action | Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 71/100 (18 reviews)
After a prison riot, former-Captain Nascimento, now a high ranking security officer in Rio de Janeiro, is swept into a bloody political dispute that involves government officials and paramilitary groups.
Director:
José Padilha
Stars:
Wagner Moura, Irandhir Santos, André Ramiro, Milhem Cortaz
Watch Trailer

11-11-11 (2011)

90 min   -   Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 26/100 (5 reviews)
After the death of his wife and child, an author travels to Barcelona to see his estranged brother and dying father, where he learns that his life is plagued by events that occur on 11/11/11.
Director:
Darren Lynn Bousman
Stars:
Timothy Gibbs, Michael Landes, Wendy Glenn, Benjamin Cook
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: What's creepier than the entire Saw series is director Darren Lynn Bousman's production blog for his latest movie, his first true location shoot, in a house in Spain - one full of secret rooms, cult symbology, and remnants from sex parties. As compelling is Bousman's willingness to make truly independent movies these days and release them with upstart distributors. Let's see if this numerological thriller can pull an Insidious (2010).

November 18 

The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn - Part 1 (2011)

117 min   -   Adventure | Drama | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 45/100 (36 reviews)
The Quileutes close in on expecting parents Edward and Bella, whose unborn child poses a threat to the Wolf Pack and the towns people of Forks.
Director:
Bill Condon
Stars:
Kristen Stewart, Robert Pattinson, Taylor Lautner, Gil Birmingham
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Bill Condon, you have to hand it to him: he's professional enough to pull off the rather violent and explicit birth of Bella's child and manage the impressive miracle of keeping the movie PG-13 while conveying the grisly and surreal nature of Renesmee Carlie Cullen's birth. Making fans wait an entire year for the saga's end seems way harsh; I still feel as though a release date switch might be in order.

Happy Feet Two (2011)

100 min   -   Animation | Comedy | Family | Musical

Metascore: 50/100 (25 reviews)
Mumble's son, Erik, is struggling to realize his talents in the Emperor Penguin world. Meanwhile, Mumble and his family and friends discover a new threat their home -- one that will take everyone working together to save them.
Directors:
George Miller | Gary Eck | David Peers
Stars:
Elijah Wood, Robin Williams, Pink, Carlos Alazraqui
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We really miss Elijah Wood, and if we only get to hear his voice this year, so be it. And if director George Miller continues to have trouble getting Mad Max: Fury Road (2015) into production, we'll settle for the continuation of his Oscar-winning, all-singing-and-dancing animated environment (facing peril).

The Descendants (2011)

115 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 84/100 (43 reviews)
A land baron tries to reconnect with his two daughters after his wife is seriously injured in a boating accident.
Director:
Alexander Payne
Stars:
George Clooney, Shailene Woodley, Amara Miller, Nick Krause
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: In the seven years since Sideways (2004), Alexander Payne has mostly been in producer mode (The Savages (2007) and, most recently, Cedar Rapids (2011)) and you might have blocked out the fact that he co-wrote I Now Pronounce You Chuck & Larry (2007). Returning to the big-screen in a big way, he's got lit-world approved material with his adaptation of Kaui Hart Hemmings's debut novel, that ponders living wills, drug-addicted minors, adultery, land development, and other real-world themes. Sounds like a custom fit for Payne, and we're hoping the film serves as a major introduction for Shailene Woodley and Amara Miller, who play Clooney's daughters. We also welcome back Matthew Lillard, who is more talented than the roles he's offered. Elsewhere, one of the more welcome trends this year is the promise of new films from long-absent directors such as Payne (who has the road-tripping Nebraska (2013) waiting in the wings) and Terrence Malick, who has, like, a trio of films in various stages of production.

Tyrannosaur (2011)

92 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 65/100 (18 reviews)
Joseph, a man plagued by violence and a rage that is driving him to self-destruction, earns a chance of redemption that appears in the form of Hannah, a Christian charity shop worker.
Director:
Paddy Considine
Stars:
Peter Mullan, Archie Lal, Jag Sanghera, Mike Fearnley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Paddy Considine heads behind the camera for this extension of his short, Dog Altogether (2007). His brutal working-class drama stunned most festival audiences -- though I'm more aligned with this particular detractor -- and earned two top prizes at Sundance.

The Lie (2011)

80 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 59/100 (12 reviews)
A man's life is altered unexpectedly after telling a lie to get out of work.
Director:
Joshua Leonard
Stars:
Joshua Leonard, Jess Weixler, Kelli Garner, Alia Shawkat
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Similar in ways to Miranda July's The Future (2011), Joshua Leonard's solo directorial debut reaches a welcome peak when his shambling Los Angeles hipster dad first realizes his selfishness, then comes to terms with his lack of creative ability (well, it's more the other way around for Miranda, but ...). The main problem is: the scenario put forth is another problem of luxury, relatively speaking; this is yet another frumpy white person story who rebels at the idea of getting health insurance. The welcome supporting cast includes Jane Adams (Joy from Happiness (1998)) and Warhol superstar Holly Woodlawn.

Another Happy Day (2011)

119 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 46/100 (18 reviews)
A wedding at her parents' Annapolis estate hurls high-strung Lynn into the center of touchy family dynamics.
Director:
Sam Levinson
Stars:
Ellen Barkin, Ezra Miller, Ellen Burstyn, Demi Moore
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Melodramatic to the point of tedium, writer-director Sam Levinson offers up one of the most unpleasant family weddings in recent memory. There's something to be said for Levinson's boldness and I definitely look forward to seeing what he has to say as his gets more filmmaking experience, but with his first effort, it's more like his cast is doing him major favors with no reciprocity.

Tomboy (2011)

82 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 74/100 (20 reviews)
A 10-year old transgender boy moves to a new neighborhood with his family. He introduces himself as a boy for the first time to new friends.
Director:
Céline Sciamma
Stars:
Zoé Héran, Malonn Lévana, Jeanne Disson, Sophie Cattani
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Céline Sciamma is proving herself to be one of the better filmmakers around when it comes to directing children; her last feature, Naissance des pieuvres (2007), focused on the friendship between three teenage girls that never ventured into treacly or voyeuristic territory, so I'm intrigued by how she might handle the tomboy situation, which hasn't been done right Stateside since -- hmm -- Manny & Lo (1996)?

Hugo (2011)

126 min   -   Adventure | Drama | Family | Mystery

Metascore: 83/100 (41 reviews)
Set in 1930s Paris, an orphan who lives in the walls of a train station is wrapped up in a mystery involving his late father and an automaton.
Director:
Martin Scorsese
Stars:
Asa Butterfield, Chloë Grace Moretz, Christopher Lee, Ben Kingsley
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: For those of us who never thought there'd be a day where Martin Scorsese would shoot in 3D, there are a couple generations of people who do not know him from Taxi Driver (1976), Mean Streets (1973) ... So we consider this ace source material and the support system in place behind the scenes (including producer Johnny Depp) as Scorsese embraces both big technology and kid-friendly entertainment for the first time. It could be worse: no chipmunks, Smurfs, or boxing robots here.

The Muppets (2011)

103 min   -   Comedy | Family | Musical

Metascore: 75/100 (37 reviews)
A Muppet fanatic with some help from his two human compatriots must regroup the Muppet gang to stop an avaricious oil mogul from taking down one of their precious life-longing treasures.
Director:
James Bobin
Stars:
Amy Adams, Jason Segel, Chris Cooper, Rashida Jones
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From the onset, actor/nudist/puppeteer Jason Segel struck us as the perfect person to bring the Muppets back to the big screen, and we're glad he won the right to do so. The story idea is subversive, resulting in internal rumblings at Disney about the movie's humor not being faithful to the Muppets' legacy. We're wondering how families will respond upon opening weekend.

A Dangerous Method (2011)

A look at how the intense relationship between Carl Jung and Sigmund Freud gives birth to psychoanalysis.
Director:
David Cronenberg
Stars:
Michael Fassbender, Keira Knightley, Viggo Mortensen, Vincent Cassel
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Body horror fans might not be engaged by Cronenberg going period, but something tells me theaters could be crammed with older audiences for the director's latest -- the same people who shifted uncomfortably while Viggo Mortensen and Maria Bello made rough love on their staircase in A History of Violence (2005).

My Week with Marilyn (2011)

99 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 65/100 (38 reviews)
Colin Clark, an employee of Sir Laurence Olivier's, documents the tense interaction between Olivier and Marilyn Monroe during the production of The Prince and the Showgirl (1957).
Director:
Simon Curtis
Stars:
Michelle Williams, Eddie Redmayne, Kenneth Branagh, Julia Ormond
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The Weinstein Company's awards blitz began with Sarah's Key and continues with the adaptation of Colin Clark's memoir, which first grabbed headlines when the stunning initial image of Williams-as-Monroe was released. We lust for this cast and anticipate the first truly interesting role for Eddie Redmayne. And we hope Hermione Granger herself, Emma Watson, kept a journal of her own while she shot her first post-Potter movie.

Arthur Christmas (2011)

97 min   -   Animation | Comedy | Drama | Family

Santa's clumsy son Arthur gets put on a mission with St. Nick's father to give out a present they misplaced to a young girl in less than 2 hours.

Stars:
James McAvoy, Jim Broadbent, Bill Nighy, Hugh Laurie
Watch Trailer

While we don't think it make a difference most anywhere else, will U.S. audiences accept a British Santa?

The Artist (2011)

100 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 89/100 (41 reviews)
A silent movie star meets a young dancer, but the arrival of talking pictures sends their careers in opposite directions.
Director:
Michel Hazanavicius
Stars:
Jean Dujardin, Bérénice Bejo, John Goodman, James Cromwell
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Every once in a while a film comes along that reminds us of why we love movies (or why our grandparents loved movies?). Super 8 (2011) kinda did that earlier this year for the Goonies crowd. But, for those of us who watch more TCM than AMC, comes this instant classic from French cinephille Hazanavicius.
