January 6 

The Devil Inside (2012)

83 min   -   Horror

Metascore: 18/100 (19 reviews)
In Italy, a woman becomes involved in a series of unauthorized exorcisms during her mission to discover what happened to her mother, who allegedly murdered three people during her own exorcism.
Director:
William Brent Bell
Stars:
Fernanda Andrade, Simon Quarterman, Evan Helmuth, Ionut Grama
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sorry, but I fear the whole microbudget horror craze is going to bring down the genre's mainstream appeal. The latest case is this exorcism-themed project (yes, another) from the guys who turned Stay Alive (2006) into an indie hit for Disney's dark side back in 2006. Paramount has set up this project at their specialty/cheapie label, Insurge, so expect it to mimic your standard Paranormal Activity promotional campaign.

95 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Music

Metascore: 61/100 (10 reviews)
After 20 years on the road with Blue Oyster Cult, Jimmy Testagros returns to his hometown to life with his ailing mother. Complications arise when he falls for an old friend, who is now married to his longtime nemesis.
Director:
Michael Cuesta
Stars:
Ron Eldard, Bobby Cannavale, Jill Hennessy, Lois Smith
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sadly, Michael Cuesta has never managed to live up to the promise we first saw in L.I.E. (2001), but kudos to him for getting his projects made in an increasingly difficult environment. Is it mean to say this comes across like the guy version of Hysterical Blindness (2002)?

Noruwei no mori (2010)

133 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 58/100 (19 reviews)
Toru recalls his life in the 1960s, when his friend Kizuki killed himself and he grew close to Naoko, Kizuki's girlfriend, and another woman, the outgoing, lively Midori.
Director:
Tran Anh Hung
Stars:
Ken'ichi Matsuyama, Rinko Kikuchi, Kiko Mizuhara, Kengo Kôra
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: As long awaited and amazing-on-paper as Anh Hung Tran's adaptation of Haruki Murakami's novel sounds, I haven't read anything that suggests it adds up to anything more than some wonderfully stylish moments. Good news: Tran, who hasn't been seen on U.S. screens in over a decade, is putting together the pieces for his next project, reportedly a translation of an unnamed (for now) French novel.

January 13 

Contraband (2012)

109 min   -   Action | Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 51/100 (38 reviews)
To protect his brother-in-law from a drug lord, a former smuggler heads to Panama to score millions of dollars in counterfeit bills.
Director:
Baltasar Kormákur
Stars:
Mark Wahlberg, Giovanni Ribisi, Kate Beckinsale, Ben Foster
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Hollywood's North Atlantic obsession continues with this remake of Baltasar Kormákur's Euro hit Reykjavík Rotterdam (2008). Kormákur's native films -- especially 101 Reykjavík (2000) and Hafið (2002) -- are culturally rich and universally engaging, and I've long thought that one of his works would be imported by a U.S. studio. Unfortunately, his skills didn't translate with his two previous English-language attempts, A Little Trip to Heaven (2005) and Inhale (2010), and I'm rather certain this incident of smuggling is going to be a blemish on the resumes of all involved.

Brisk pacing and sweeping historical backdrops work well for "Downton Abbey," but not here.

Underworld: Awakening (2012)

88 min   -   Action | Fantasy | Horror

Metascore: 39/100 (17 reviews)
When human forces discover the existence of the Vampire and Lycan clans, a war to eradicate both species commences. The vampire warrior Selene leads the battle against humankind.
Directors:
Måns Mårlind | Björn Stein
Stars:
Kate Beckinsale, Michael Ealy, India Eisley, Stephen Rea
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We feel as though Len Wiseman should have returned to the director's chair, but he earned the potentially dubious honor of handling the Colin Farrell-starring Total Recall (2012) remake at Sony, so the call went out to Måns Mårlind and Björn Stein, the Swedish duo whose thriller 6 Souls (2010) is being held captive by The Weinstein Company (for good reason, we hear). Distributor Screen Gems, home of the more successful Resident Evil series, will undoubtedly milk this vampire/werewolf saga for every last silver coin. Something tells us newcomer India Eisley is being groomed to potentially take over the franchise since it's reported that she's playing Selene's daughter here. She's the Jeremy Renner to Kate Beckinsale's Tom Cruise? But don't feel sorry for Kate since she looks poised to have two back-to-back #1 movies in this new year; hopefully that will lead to a much deserved renaissance.

Better yet: initial screenings caused some of the A-list male stars to reconsider getting a thrashing courtesy of mixed-martial artist (and former American Gladiator) Gina Carano. Insecurities led to reshoots, we understand!

Red Tails (2012)

125 min   -   Action | Adventure | Drama | History | War

Metascore: 46/100 (32 reviews)
A crew of African American pilots in the Tuskegee training program, having faced segregation while kept mostly on the ground during World War II, are called into duty under the guidance of Col. A.J. Bullard.
Director:
Anthony Hemingway
Stars:
Cuba Gooding Jr., Gerald McRaney, David Oyelowo, Andre Royo
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sure, the dialogue is riddled with cliched bullets, but the aerial action sequences are the kind of Lucas-style entertainment that we happily eat up. Entirely financed by George Lucas himself and in development for 20+ years, we're happy to know that other people have handled the screenplay, namely, "Boondocks" creator Aaron McGruder. And with Treme (2010) producer/director Anthony Hemingway behind the camera, we're thinking the melodrama might have a bit more resonance than we first expected. Helping keep the story airborne are the always-welcome Terrence Howard, Bryan Cranston, and Andre Royo.

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Joe Carnahan has spat back at his many critics by saying this Alaska-set survival tale is his best script to date.
