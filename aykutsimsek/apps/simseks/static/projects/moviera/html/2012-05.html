Director:
Joss Whedon
Stars:
Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Scarlett Johansson, Jeremy Renner
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: In a way, wouldn't it be cool if development on all other comic-book movies stopped here? And now that his S.H.I.E.L.D. operatives have conquered the rest of the world as we head into opening weekend in the U.S., we're all wondering how this universe will manifest itself in future Marvel episodes -- and what Whedon's next major creative step will be.

Dark Shadows (2012)

113 min   -   Comedy | Horror

Metascore: 55/100 (42 reviews)
An imprisoned vampire, Barnabas Collins, is set free and returns to his ancestral home, where his dysfunctional descendants are in need of his protection.
Director:
Tim Burton
Stars:
Johnny Depp, Michelle Pfeiffer, Eva Green, Helena Bonham Carter
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: For some of us, this Burton-Depp union carries more weight and causes more anxiety than the sum of their recent string of remakes/reimaginings; Alice Kingsley and Willy Wonka, while treasured, are less sacred than the vampire Barnabus Collins. We never had doubts about the project's respect for the past, and were certain that Burton and screenwriter Seth Grahame-Smith (Pride and Prejudice and Zombies (2016), Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter (2012)) would outfit Collinwood Mansion with welcome eccentricities, but the notion of Barnabus as a full-fledged hero is the sort of shortcut that obscures the history of the long-running TV show. RIP Jonathan Frid.

Oslo, 31. august (2011)

95 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 84/100 (23 reviews)

Director:
Joachim Trier
Stars:
Anders Danielsen Lie, Hans Olav Brenner, Ingrid Olava, Malin Crépin
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With all hyperbole intended: after a stellar debut film, Reprise (2006), writer/director Joachim Trier puts forth one of the best second films with his meditation on the reality one faces after hitting a drug-addicted low. Equal parts somber and dryly humorous, and made relatable by Anders Danielsen Lie's lead performance, this is one of the most effective dramas of the year, foreign or otherwise. Currently, Trier is working on a script called Louder Than Bombs, which is backed by the production company that has made Little Children (2006), Election (1999) and Little Miss Sunshine (2006), so my eye is on what could be his English-language debut.
