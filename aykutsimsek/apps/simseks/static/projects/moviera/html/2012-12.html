December 7 

Hyde Park on Hudson (2012)

94 min   -   Biography | Comedy | Drama | History

Metascore: 55/100 (41 reviews)
The story of the love affair between FDR and his distant cousin Margaret "Daisy" Suckley, centered around the weekend in 1939 when the King and Queen of the United Kingdom visited upstate New York.
Director:
Roger Michell
Stars:
Bill Murray, Laura Linney, Olivia Williams, Samuel West
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Feeling a bit like the American version Downton Abbey (2010)'s second season as we watch some of the sociopolitical maneuvering that preceded America's entry into WWII, Roger Michell's first period foray since his debut, Persuasion (1995), has been discussed as an awards contender before filming even began. After its tepid critical reception, however, most of that talk has dissipated; even Bill Murray's FDR is a long shot, remarkably. Awards aside, there's a strong chance the movie will be embraced by older audiences -- the same crowds who turned Lincoln (2012) into an unexpected smash.

Playing for Keeps (2012)

105 min   -   Comedy | Romance | Sport

Metascore: 27/100 (26 reviews)
A former sports star who's fallen on hard times starts coaching his son's soccer team as a way to get his life together. His attempts to become an adult are met with challenges from the attractive soccer moms who pursue him at every turn.
Director:
Gabriele Muccino
Stars:
Gerard Butler, Jessica Biel, Dennis Quaid, Noah Lomax
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: And the point here is ... to reintroduce Gerard Butler as a virile leading man? We're always going to cringe when Butler opts for romantic comedies, especially when he teams up with heavy-handed director Gabriele Muccino (Seven Pounds (2008), L'ultimo bacio (2001)) for some serious atonement and life lessons. We'd like to see Mavis Gary go off on every single main character.

Cheerful Weather for the Wedding (2012)

93 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 49/100 (13 reviews)
A young woman frets upstairs in her family's country manor on her wedding day, fearful she's about to marry the wrong man. Downstairs, both her fiancé and her former lover grow increasingly anxious.
Director:
Donald Rice
Stars:
Felicity Jones, Luke Treadaway, Elizabeth McGovern, James Norton
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Felicity Jones didn't have her deserved breakout with Like Crazy (2011), but this period film, based on Julia Strachey's novella should benefit from its solid advance reviews. Look for Elizabeth McGovern to be promoted to "Downton Abbey" fans, who could turn this into one of the holiday season's most enduring performers.

Heleno (2011)

116 min   -   Biography | Drama | Sport

Metascore: 52/100 (8 reviews)
A biography of the tragic life of one of Brazil's greatest soccer players, Heleno de Freitas.
Director:
José Henrique Fonseca
Stars:
Rodrigo Santoro, Angie Cepeda, Alinne Moraes, Marcelo Adnet

The Buzz: The talented and underutilized Rodrigo Santoro shouldered the responsibility of illuminating the life of troubled soccer star Heleno de Freitas by starring in and producing the project, which took a couple years to get off the ground for myriad reasons. We imagine the idea of an unflinching, black-and-white look at a sex symbol who ultimately died of syphilis at the age of 39 wasn't the easiest sell. But we are actually surprised that it wasn't picked as Brazil's nomination for the Best Foreign Language Oscar (that went to The Clown (2011)).

In Our Nature (2012)

103 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 51/100 (11 reviews)
A scheduling mistake leads to an estranged father and son sharing a vacation home with their respective girlfriends.
Director:
Brian Savelson
Stars:
Zach Gilford, Jena Malone, John Slattery, Gabrielle Union
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We're wondering what this father-and-son drama might have been like if John Slattery had directed it. Since he's helmed several "Mad Men" episodes at this point, it's a matter of time (and of course the script and the funding) before Slattery makes his first feature. In a completely different film, you could cast Slattery, Gabrielle Union, Zach Gilford, and Jean Malone, then mix and match at will for a series of appealing couples.

Lay the Favorite (2012)

94 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 38/100 (17 reviews)
Ex-private dancer Beth aspires to be a Las Vegas cocktail waitress, when she falls in with Dink, a sports gambler. Sparks fly as she proves to be something of a gambling prodigy--much to the ire of Dink's wife, Tulip.
Director:
Stephen Frears
Stars:
Rebecca Hall, Bruce Willis, Vince Vaughn, Catherine Zeta-Jones
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Stephen Frears hasn't connected with audiences since The Queen (2006), and his take on Beth Raymer's memoir doesn't look to improve on his recent track record, as festival reviews have sighed at the apparent lack of charm or spark. Rebecca Hall + Bruce Willis = no chemistry? Hard to imagine until you watch the initial trailer; between her baby-talk voice and his acting, which recalls The Whole Nine Yards (2000), the best bet here might be to pass. Hopefully Frears will rebound with his Muhammad Ali project while his Freddie Mercury biopic starring Sacha Baron Cohen is about to get off the ground finally.

Neuk-dae-so-nyeon (2012)

122 min   -   Fantasy | Romance

Summoned by an unexpected phone call, an elderly woman visits the country cottage she lived in as a child. Memories of an orphan boy she knew 47 years ago come flooding back to her.
Director:
Sung-hee Jo
Stars:
Joong-ki Song, Bo-yeong Park, Yeong-ran Lee, Young-nam Jang
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This South Korean hit is turning into a phenomenon on its native soil; now that it has bested Breaking Dawn - Part 2's opening weekend, a second version with an alternate ending is being prepped for release.

Deadfall (2012)

95 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 52/100 (24 reviews)
A thriller that follows two siblings who decide to fend for themselves in the wake of a botched casino heist, and their unlikely reunion during another family's Thanksgiving celebration.
Director:
Stefan Ruzowitzky
Stars:
Eric Bana, Olivia Wilde, Charlie Hunnam, Sissy Spacek
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Nearly every review I've encountered confirms my initial reaction to this plot outline: a good movie is hiding within the overworked and convoluted narrative. Looks like another case of a foreign director losing his way with an American production; in this case, it's Stefan Ruzowitzky, whose The Counterfeiters won the Best Foreign Language Oscar.

December 14 

The Hobbit: An Unexpected Journey (2012)

169 min   -   Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 58/100 (40 reviews)
A reluctant hobbit, Bilbo Baggins, sets out to the Lonely Mountain with a spirited group of dwarves to reclaim their mountain home - and the gold within it - from the dragon Smaug.
Director:
Peter Jackson
Stars:
Martin Freeman, Ian McKellen, Richard Armitage, Andy Serkis
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: What was once the most anticipated prequel since The Phantom Menace is facing a divided audience upon its release. First it was Peter Jackson's decision to shoot the two films at 48 frames per second -- a hyper-realistic format that was questioned from the onset -- then the announcement that a third movie would be added. And then the stories of animal deaths on set. This series of events didn't seem to shake the core fans who consider Jackson's adaptations of J. R. R. Tolkien's work to be sacred, but it's hard to predict how the larger, general audience will react after opening weekend. Perhaps original director Guillermo del Toro should have shot first.

Save the Date (2012)

97 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 54/100 (17 reviews)
Sarah begins to confront her shortcomings after she rejects her boyfriend's hasty proposal and soon finds herself in a rebound romance. Meanwhile, her sister Beth is immersed in the details of her wedding.
Director:
Michael Mohan
Stars:
Lizzy Caplan, Alison Brie, Mark Webber, Geoffrey Arend
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I think we've reached a tipping point where indie romantic comedies feel more influenced by Girls (2012) and less by Bridesmaids (2011). "Girls" took advantage of its short but ever-evolving season by revealing the insecurities behind its characters and their narcissism -- something that's harder to do in 100 minutes or fewer. We hear that Mark Webber, the guy on Lizzy Caplan's rebound, is the one performer who knows to look up from his own navel.

Any Day Now (2012)

98 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 60/100 (16 reviews)
In the 1970s, a gay couple fights a biased legal system to keep custody of the abandoned mentally handicapped teenager that comes to live under their roof.
Director:
Travis Fine
Stars:
Alan Cumming, Garret Dillahunt, Isaac Leyva, Frances Fisher
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Earlier this year, Music Box Films released Keep the Lights On (2012), queer filmmaker Ira Sachs's searing, drug-fueled drama based on one of his romantic relationships, and the studio now offers a different gay-themed story - one that has attracted further attention for its period setting.

Let Fury Have the Hour (2012)

100 min   -   Documentary | Biography | History | Music

Metascore: 53/100 (9 reviews)
A documentary that chronicles how a generation of artists, thinkers, and activists used their creativity as a response to the reactionary politics that came to define our culture in the 1980s.
Director:
Antonino D'Ambrosio
Stars:
Chuck D., Ian MacKaye, Shepard Fairey, Tom Morello
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Pretty much anyone over the age of 35 can look back to the Reagan/Thatcher era and recall the hardcore response from the art and music world to their respective, entwined reigns. Antonino D'Ambrosio, who here makes his feature documentary debut, published the book version of this project back in 2004. Maybe he could be convinced via Kickstarter to continue the project with every presidential administration, 7 Plus Seven (1970) style.

December 21 

This Is 40 (2012)

134 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 59/100 (39 reviews)
Pete and Debbie are both about to turn 40, their kids hate each other, both of their businesses are failing, they're on the verge of losing their house, and their relationship is threatening to fall apart.
Director:
Judd Apatow
Stars:
Paul Rudd, Leslie Mann, Maude Apatow, Iris Apatow
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: One of the smartest ideas Judd Apatow has made since he segued into super-producer/occasional director mode was promoting the supporting characters from his best comic environment to their own movie. Pete and Debbie and Jason (Jason Segel) were the true stars of Knocked Up (2007); and I'd make a needless argument that Leslie Mann is funnier than KU and Bridesmaids (2011) combined.

Zero Dark Thirty (2012)

157 min   -   Drama | History | Thriller

Metascore: 95/100 (46 reviews)
A chronicle of the decade-long hunt for al-Qaeda terrorist leader Osama bin Laden after the September 2001 attacks, and his death at the hands of the Navy S.E.A.L.s Team 6 in May 2011.
Director:
Kathryn Bigelow
Stars:
Jessica Chastain, Joel Edgerton, Chris Pratt, Mark Strong
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Kathryn Bigelow's never known the level of controversy her follow-up to The Hurt Locker (2008) has generated. Even before cameras rolled on the recreation of the events leading up to the assassination of Osama bin Laden, a government watchdog group has filed a lawsuit against the CIA and the Department of Defense since details of the reported meeting between the agencies, Bigelow, and writer Mark Boal have not been released via the Freedom of Information Act. Seems like the filmmakers have been privy to classified information. And that situation is old news when compared to the early critical reactions, which range from laudatory to damning. It's the sort of real-world drama the industry and those who write about it could mutually benefit from, a reminder of when American films used to resonate beyond our desire for entertainment.

Jack Reacher (2012)

130 min   -   Action | Crime | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 50/100 (35 reviews)
A homicide investigator digs deeper into a case involving a trained military sniper who shot five random victims.
Director:
Christopher McQuarrie
Stars:
Tom Cruise, Rosamund Pike, Richard Jenkins, Werner Herzog
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sure the title of the movie is akin to the "Tug Toner", but Tom Cruise's reunion with Christopher McQuarrie, who wrote and produced Valkyrie (2008) marks the beginning of creative marriage between star and filmmaker: McQuarrie wrote the Edge of Tomorrow (2014) screenplay and just might write and direct the fifth M:I outing. But here McQuarrie takes a second turn behind the camera as director of the adaptation of Lee Child's novel, which has been on the Paramount lot since 2008. It's a tall order to make a movie as universally entertaining as M:I4, but if the world embraces Cruise as homicide investigator Jack Reacher, there's a bounty of other Reacher adventures from which to choose. But this will be the only one bold enough to cast Werner Herzog as the villain.

The Guilt Trip (2012)

95 min   -   Comedy | Drama

As inventor Andy Brewster is about to embark on the road trip of a lifetime, a quick stop at his mom's house turns into an unexpected cross-country voyage with her along for the ride.
Director:

Stars:
Barbra Streisand, Seth Rogen, Julene Renee, Zabryna Guevara
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Reviews indicate that a subdued Seth Rogen and a hammy Barbra Streisand make for one familiar and unwelcoming road trips in some time. At first we thought it would be a coup for Seth to get his movie mom to take a hit on camera, but now we're more focused on what exactly is going on with his career choices.

In order to power the city, monsters have to scare children so that they scream. However, the children are toxic to the monsters, and after a child gets through, two monsters realize things may not be what they think.

Cirque du Soleil: Worlds Away (2012)

The Impossible (2012)

114 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 73/100 (42 reviews)
The story of a tourist family in Thailand caught in the destruction and chaotic aftermath of the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami.
Director:
J.A. Bayona
Stars:
Naomi Watts, Ewan McGregor, Tom Holland, Oaklee Pendergast
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I've had my eye on Juan Antonio Bayona's English-language debut because I love projects that maintain a low profile throughout their production. While sensitive to the traumatizing real-world event that inspired his story of a family working to survive a natural disaster, it's the scope of the visual effects and the presence of actual-real lead actors that have me stoked for a hopefully thoughtful action/drama.

On the Road (2012)

124 min   -   Adventure | Drama

Metascore: 56/100 (32 reviews)
Young writer Sal Paradise has his life shaken by the arrival of free-spirited Dean Moriarty and his girl, Marylou. As they travel across the country, they encounter a mix of people who each impact their journey indelibly.
Director:
Walter Salles
Stars:
Sam Riley, Garrett Hedlund, Kristen Stewart, Amy Adams
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's been almost ten years since The Motorcycle Diaries (2004) -- fifteen since Central Station (1998)! -- and all the while Walter Salles has spent his non-directing time championing projects by other South American filmmakers. Now comes his take on Jack Kerouac's enduring novel - a reunion for Salles and Diaries screenwriter Jose Rivera - with a cast worthy of a recent Soderbergh movie, a burden of weighty expectations, and Kristen Stewart embroiled in Marylou-adjacent man troubles. Fortunately, in this case, Stewart doesn't need to atone for her behavior with Twilighters.

Set in suburban New Jersey the 1960s, a group of friends form a rock band and try to make it big.
Director:
David Chase
Stars:
John Magaro, Jack Huston, Will Brill, Brahm Vaccarella
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: While the premise doesn't exactly stoke my interest, David Chase's character-writing ability is evident in some of today's best television programs, and his emotional scenarios are never strained or false. The only thing that bums me out is no role for Drea de Matteo.

Georges and Anne are an octogenarian couple. They are cultivated, retired music teachers. Their daughter, also a musician, lives in Britain with her family. One day, Anne has a stroke, and the couple's bond of love is severely tested.

The once beautiful accidents of nature, Jean-Louis Trintignant and Emmanuelle Riva have become exquisite works of art. A measured but gratifying work." -- Keith Simanton

Les Misérables (2012)

158 min   -   Drama | Musical | Romance

Metascore: 63/100 (41 reviews)
In 19th-century France, Jean Valjean, who for decades has been hunted by the ruthless policeman Javert after breaking parole, agrees to care for a factory worker's daughter. The decision changes their lives for ever.
Director:
Tom Hooper
Stars:
Hugh Jackman, Russell Crowe, Anne Hathaway, Amanda Seyfried
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Tom Hooper goes further back in history for his follow up to The King's Speech (2010), opting for the latest cinematic take on Victor Hugo's novel, what could be the second best adaptation after Raymond Bernard's exhaustive 1934 work. Before cameras rolled, the project has been positioned as a seemingly unbeatable Oscar candidate, though I say it's more of a career stabilizer for many, and a launchpad for stage actress and singer Samantha Barks.

Parental Guidance (2012)

105 min   -   Comedy | Family

Metascore: 36/100 (20 reviews)
Artie and Diane agree to look after their three grandkids when their type-A helicopter parents need to leave town for work. Problems arise when the kids' 21st-century behavior collides with Artie and Diane's old-school methods.
Director:
Andy Fickman
Stars:
Billy Crystal, Bette Midler, Marisa Tomei, Tom Everett Scott
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Can a broad, contemporary comedy add any length to Billy Crystal's comeback streak? Audiences ignored Barbra Streisand in The Guilt Trip (2012); does that have any bearing on Bette's big-screen return? It's as hard to answer these questions as it is to pinpoint this movie's demographic -- harried moms and dads, perhaps, and their parents. But children? No.

Promised Land (2012)

106 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 55/100 (36 reviews)
A salesman for a natural gas company experiences life-changing events after arriving in a small town, where his corporation wants to tap into the available resources.
Director:
Gus Van Sant
Stars:
Matt Damon, Frances McDormand, John Krasinski, Hal Holbrook
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We'd love to know exactly what transpired to make Matt Damon bow out of directing this enviroment-minded drama but remain as its star; Damon's industry edge has surfaced since The Bourne Ultimatum (2007) and we have ever-increasing respect for the former Oscar wunderkind who has matured into a talky, opinionated fortysomething who is dedicated to a clutch of filmmaker. A wave of '90s nostalgia finds Damon reuniting with his Good Will Hunting (1997) director Gus Van Sant, though we are out and proud defenders of their other project together, Gerry (2002).

West of Memphis (2012)

147 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 80/100 (27 reviews)
An examination of a failure of justice in the case against the West Memphis Three.
Director:
Amy Berg
Stars:
Jason Baldwin, Damien Wayne Echols, Jessie Misskelley, Michael Baden
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Though Jason Baldwin, Damien Wayne Echols, and Jessie Misskelley are out of prison, their legal saga continues. Amy Berg's documentary wisely summarizes the Paradise Lost trilogy before moving into the present day, as the three men were semi-exonerated in the case of three murdered Arkansas boys from two decades ago and made their first steps to re-enter society. The most challenging aspect of of this work -- aside from the maddening Alford Plea that secured their freedom -- are the opinions of those who so fully believe in the judicial system made no mistakes here.
