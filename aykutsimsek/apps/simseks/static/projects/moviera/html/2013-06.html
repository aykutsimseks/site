June 7 

The Internship (2013)

119 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 42/100 (36 reviews)
Two salesmen whose careers have been torpedoed by the digital age find their way into a coveted internship at Google, where they must compete with a group of young, tech-savvy geniuses for a shot at employment.
Director:
Shawn Levy
Stars:
Vince Vaughn, Owen Wilson, Rose Byrne, Aasif Mandvi
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Eight years ago (yikes), Wedding Crashers (2005) made R-rated comedy bankable and paved the way for Knocked Up (2007), The Hangover (2009), and other industry-changing hits. I can see this pitch exciting marketing execs, but I'm a bit unsure who the target audience is here. As The Hangover Part III recently proved, audiences seem to be a bit disinterested in this summer's comedy offerings thus far, too.

The Purge (2013)

85 min   -   Horror | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 41/100 (33 reviews)
In the future, a wealthy family is held hostage for harboring the target of a murderous syndicate during the Purge, a 12-hour period in which any and all crime is legalized.
Director:
James DeMonaco
Stars:
Ethan Hawke, Lena Headey, Max Burkholder, Adelaide Kane
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Producer Jason Blum has set up projects all over Hollywood since Paranormal Activity proved itself as a franchise instead of a mere one-off success, and here he's pushing the kind of horror story that freaks us out the most: the kind that could actually happen in real life. Meanwhile, Ethan Hawke might pull a semi-McConaughey this year with a trio of films that seem to have respective built-in audiences.

Much Ado About Nothing (2012)

109 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 78/100 (37 reviews)
A modern retelling of Shakespeare's classic comedy about two pairs of lovers with different takes on romance and a way with words.
Director:
Joss Whedon
Stars:
Alexis Denisof, Amy Acker, Fran Kranz, Jillian Morgese
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Shakespeare in the hands of Joss Whedon - what else is there to say? We love that Whedon's insecurity about directing The Avengers (2012) manifested in a such a way (the restless creator shot this project in secret while assembling the Marvel blockbuster), and those of us who reeled from the news that Anthony Head stepped down from playing Leonato were assuaged by the slotting Clark Gregg in his place. I mean, it's clear to some that Gregg's Agent Coulson is made of the same Whedonesque fabric as Head's Rupert Giles from "Buffy", so it is assuring to see him become one of the new Whedon Players.

Violet & Daisy (2011)

88 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 43/100 (16 reviews)
Two teenage assassins accept what they think will be a quick-and-easy job, until an unexpected target throws them off their plan.
Director:
Geoffrey Fletcher
Stars:
Saoirse Ronan, Alexis Bledel, James Gandolfini, Danny Trejo
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I wouldn't be at all surprised if Precious (2009) screenwriter Geoffrey Fletcher's directorial debut becomes a cult film; it's a dark fantasy where teen assassins Saoirse Ronan and Alexis Bledel play patty cake and look up to their pop idol while they await news of their next target. I saw it at the Toronto Festival back in 2011 and remember nodding when a fellow audience member likened it to a Martin McDonagh film but with female leads. And not that anyone needs the reminder, but James Gandolfini takes full advantage of playing a nuanced character who acts as a sort of doomed father figure to the young women.

Dirty Wars (2013)

87 min   -   Documentary | Crime | Drama | Mystery | War

Metascore: 76/100 (18 reviews)
Investigative journalist Jeremy Scahill is pulled into an unexpected journey as he chases down the hidden truth behind America's expanding covert wars.
Director:
Rick Rowley
Stars:
Jeremy Scahill, Nasser Al Aulaqi, Saleha Al Aulaqi, Muqbal Al Kazemi
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Rick Rowley and journalist Jeremy Scahill (who is credited with spotlighting Blackwater operations against members of the Taliban and Al Qaeda) team up for an exposé that seems to be burbling just under the mainstream radar. By all critical indications, their filings on the objectives and targets of the Joint Special Operations Command will bring your beliefs into questions - no matter your political affiliation.

Wish You Were Here (2012)

89 min   -   Drama | Mystery

Metascore: 60/100 (14 reviews)
Four friends lose themselves in a carefree South-East Asian holiday. Only three come back. Dave and Alice return home to their young family desperate for answers about Jeremy's mysterious disappearance. When Alice's sister Steph returns not long after, a nasty secret is revealed about the night her boyfriend went missing. But it is only the first of many. Who amongst them knows what happened on that fateful night when they were dancing under a full moon in Cambodia?
Director:
Kieran Darcy-Smith
Stars:
Joel Edgerton, Teresa Palmer, Felicity Price, Antony Starr
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Seems as though director Kieran Darcy-Smith has co-written a script that has frustrated some and captivated others with its secrets shared between its rather unlikeable characters. To us, it sounds similar to some of the best films that have come out of Darcy-Smith's native Australia of late, so even if it doesn't come close to the excellence of Lore (2012) or The Snowtown Murders (2011), I'm begging to think of the country as a record label whose every release is worth a listen.

Syrup (2013)

90 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

A slacker hatches a million-dollar idea. But, in order to see it through, he has to learn to trust his attractive corporate counterpart. Based on Max Barry's novel.
Director:
Aram Rappaport
Stars:
Amber Heard, Shiloh Fernandez, Kellan Lutz, Brittany Snow
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Max Barry's first novel finds itself turned into a dark comedy starring Shiloh Fernandez (who is having a great year with roles in Evil Dead (2013) and the just-released The East (2013)) and the always-welcome Amber Heard, one of the most popular actresses who never has had a lead role in a hit film. Maybe the Goodreads crowd will show up for opening weekend?

Évocateur: The Morton Downey Jr. Movie (2012)

90 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 70/100 (14 reviews)
Before entire networks were built on populist personalities; before reality morphed into a TV genre; the masses fixated on a single, sociopathic star: controversial talk-show host Morton Downey, Jr.
Directors:
Seth Kramer | Daniel A. Miller | Jeremy Newberger
Stars:
Morton Downey Jr., Glenn Beck, Victoria Jackson, Bob Pittman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Morton Downey, Jr. first ruled the airwaves in the mid-1980s before creating one of the decade's most memorable TV shows - a blueprint for countless imitators that still glut the syndication circuit to this day. I'm unsure if Seth Kramer's documentary looks to humanize its subject, a man who manipulated the media and his audience alike until his final days, but I do hope it traces back to his roots as a songwriter; Downey Jr. had more moments than his controversial 80s heyday.

Metascore: 55/100 (47 reviews)
Clark Kent, one of the last of an extinguished race disguised as an unremarkable human, is forced to reveal his identity when Earth is invaded by an army of survivors who threaten to bring the planet to the brink of destruction.
Director:
Zack Snyder
Stars:
Henry Cavill, Amy Adams, Michael Shannon, Diane Lane
Watch Trailer

But every behind-the-scenes decision here seems to be heading for the ultimate payoff: popular and critical success, worldwide.

This Is the End (2013)

107 min   -   Comedy | Fantasy

Metascore: 67/100 (41 reviews)
While attending a party at James Franco's house, Seth Rogen, Jay Baruchel and many other celebrities are faced with the apocalypse.
Directors:
Evan Goldberg | Seth Rogen
Stars:
James Franco, Jonah Hill, Seth Rogen, Jay Baruchel
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Seth Rogen and Jay Baruchel look to out-meta pal James Franco with a studio-backed expansion of their short, Jay and Seth Versus the Apocalypse (2007), which was shot right after filming on Knocked Up (2007) wrapped. Though Judd Apatow has nothing to do with the production directly, he must be one proud papa; look at what his players have achieved: Ken Miller is now a film director. Competitor studios shuffled their releases to get out of the way here, though we imagine the pressure's on at Sony/Columbia in the wake of After Earth (2013)'s highly criticized crash-landing at the U.S. box office.

Fukrey (2013)

139 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

The paths of four dream-chasing college friends cross with an array of colourful characters, from a tough-talking Punjabi female don to a Jugaad Baaz college watchman. Mayhem ensues.
Director:
Mrigdeep Singh Lamba
Stars:
Pulkit Samrat, Manjot Singh, Ali Fazal, Varun Sharma

The Buzz: With a youth-baiting premise and a push to turn Pulkit Samrat into a global heartthrob, director Mrigdeep Singh Lamba's second movie could be the next Bollywood success story in the U.S. after Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani (2013)'s recent top 10 box-office placement.

Twenty Feet from Stardom (2013)

91 min   -   Documentary | History | Music

Metascore: 83/100 (25 reviews)
Backup singers live in a world that lies just beyond the spotlight. Their voices bring harmony to the biggest bands in popular music, but we've had no idea who these singers are or what lives they lead, until now.
Director:
Morgan Neville
Stars:
Darlene Love, Merry Clayton, Lisa Fischer, Judith Hill
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Veteran documentarian Morgan Neville worked with famed record producer Gil Friesen (who passed away last December) on this project, which is coming into focus with Darlene Love at its center - the storied back-up singer who worked with Marvin Gaye and countless others enjoyed a 4-page spread in a recent issue of Rolling Stone to cap a long overdue amount of mainstream attention.

Berberian Sound Studio (2012)

Director:
Peter Strickland
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: You set a film within the realm of Italian horror movie-making, you have a built-in audience. You cast Toby Jones as the lead, and you have a chance of creating the kind of slow building word-of-mouth that equates writer-director Peter Strickland's to the best films of the aforementioned genre. We can't vouch for the picture just yet since we have yet to see it, but it had one of the best trailers in some time.

June 21 

World War Z (2013)

116 min   -   Action | Adventure | Horror | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 63/100 (46 reviews)
United Nations employee Gerry Lane traverses the world in a race against time to stop the Zombie pandemic that is toppling armies and governments, and threatening to destroy humanity itself.
Director:
Marc Forster
Stars:
Brad Pitt, Mireille Enos, Daniella Kertesz, James Badge Dale
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Marc Foster always seemed like a curious pick to helm an action-heavy adaptation of the novel by Max Brooks, and details on the out-of-focus production emerged over the summer of 2012 as to its myriad problems, from indecision as to what the zombies should look like to major crew members asking to be let go from the production. Never mind the skyrocketing budget. What first appeared to be the best franchise starter since Rise of the Planet of the Apes (2011) has a target on its back; I'm sensing this is the Men in Black 3 (2012) of Summer 2013 - meaning it will under-perform in the U.S. and make its money elsewhere. An opening under $50 million dollars has to be a major disappointment for all involved - especially for a project that was developed to be a trilogy.

Monsters University (2013)

104 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Fantasy

Metascore: 65/100 (41 reviews)
A look at the relationship between Mike and Sulley during their days at Monsters University -- when they weren't necessarily the best of friends.
Director:
Dan Scanlon
Stars:
Billy Crystal, John Goodman, Steve Buscemi, Helen Mirren
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Initially, we were a bit disheartened to hear that Pixar was using its 2013 slot for a Monsters, Inc. (2001) prequel: we already know that Mike and Sulley wind up being best friends, so the story of how they met could feel redundant. We got a look at some Monsters University (2013) concept art at Disney's D23 event last summer, though, and now we're definitely interested to see how Pixar goes about "monsterizing" the college experience. Here's hoping that in the pantheon of Pixar sequels/prequels, this is more Toy Story 3 (2010) and less Cars 2 (2011).

But, I mean, it's Terence Stamp, and you don't waste an opportunity to see him perform.

Downloaded (2013)

106 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 56/100 (6 reviews)
A documentary that explores the downloading revolution; the kids that created it, the bands and the businesses that were affected by it, and its impact on the world at large.
Director:
Alex Winter
Stars:
Mike D, Henry Rollins, Noel Gallagher, Billy Corgan
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If there were ever a documentary that could release further updates and installments, it's Alex Winter's look at the first wave of the downloading revolution that forever altered the music industry. Since the music business has found ways in which to recover -- by refocusing attention on the power of the hit single; by the rebirth of vinyl LP sales; by widening the definition of what makes a pop star -- Downloaded might be a many things, including a study guide for the sagging film industry.

The Bling Ring (2013)

90 min   -   Biography | Crime | Drama

Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

This past weekend, the ring owned the box office in Los Angeles and New York, slinking away with Coppola's best performance since Lost in Translation (2003). Breakout star Katie Chang leads her crew into the big leagues this coming weekend with a wide expansion.

The Heat (2013)

117 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime

An uptight FBI Special Agent is paired with a foul-mouthed Boston cop to take down a ruthless drug lord.
Director:
Paul Feig
Stars:
Sandra Bullock, Michael McDonald, Melissa McCarthy, Demian Bichir
Watch Trailer

With a sequel already in development -- and a lot of press dedicated to a theatrical poster featuring a heavily airbrushed Melissa McMarthy -- the stage is set for opening weekend.

I'm So Excited! (2013)

90 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 55/100 (36 reviews)
When it appears as though the end is in sight, the pilots, flight crew, and passengers of a plane heading to Mexico City look to forget the anguish of the moment and face the greatest danger, which we carry within ourselves.
Director:
Pedro Almodóvar
Stars:
Javier Cámara, Pepa Charro, Cecilia Roth, Lola Dueñas
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Pedro Almodovar himself let it be known that he is revisiting the tone of his early comedies with his latest film, which features cameos from Antonio Banderas and Penelope Cruz, and plenty of fey delights, apparently. We've loved the dark detour he's taken since Volver (2006) and look forward to a summery change of tone. It's also worth noting that in a creative climate where money is hard to come by, Excited! was financed entirely by worldwide pre-sales.

Byzantium (2012)

118 min   -   Drama | Fantasy | Horror | Thriller

Metascore: 66/100 (22 reviews)
Residents of a coastal town learn, with deathly consequences, the secret shared by the two mysterious women who have sought shelter at a local resort.
Director:
Neil Jordan
Stars:
Saoirse Ronan, Gemma Arterton, Sam Riley, Caleb Landry Jones
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Few directors handle myth like Neil Jordan. Look up detailed reviews of his questionable films -- In Dreams (1999), Ondine (2009), The Brave One (2007) are my nominees -- and you'll sense the architecture of his entire, curious filmography. Fans have breathlessly awaited his return to the world of vampires, hoping he will breathe new eternal life into the genre, which has been victimized by many unworthy entries over the last few years. One dividing factor here could be the lack of gore, because this is a mood piece from beginning to end.

Some Girl(s) (2013)
