300: Rise of an Empire (2014)

102 min   -   Action | Drama | Fantasy | War

Metascore: 48/100 (34 reviews)
Greek general Themistokles leads the charge against invading Persian forces led by mortal-turned-god Xerxes and Artemisia, vengeful commander of the Persian navy.
Director:
Noam Murro
Stars:
Sullivan Stapleton, Eva Green, Lena Headey, Hans Matheson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Is it a prequel? A sequel? Yes it's both. And also a Greekquel. A lot has changed since 300 was released: Gerard Butler's career has waxed and waned and neither Lena Headey nor Michael Fassbender were well known (crazy that this was Fassenbender's first role) -- the iPhone hadn't even been introduced yet, remarkably. So what is the expectation in 2014 for this movie ... high fives and a huge collective sigh of relief if it makes over $100 million in the U.S. and surpassing the overseas grosses of the original? For the fans: Eva Green is looking more than fit to become the villain you wind up rooting for.

Journey to the West (2013)

110 min   -   Adventure | Comedy | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 68/100 (13 reviews)
Tang Sanzang, an aspiring Buddhist hero tries to protect a village from three demons. He develops complex feelings for Miss Duan, the demon hunter who repeatedly helps him, and finally quests to meet the legendary Monkey King.
Directors:
Stephen Chow | Chi-kin Kwok
Stars:
Zhang Wen, Qi Shu, Bo Huang, Show Luo
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: When you make films on your own terms and they break box-office records on your home turf, who needs Hollywood ultimately, right? It's been a decade since the U.S. movie business tried to domesticate Stephen Chow, who built a cult audience with The God of Cookery, Shaolin Soccer, and his "breakthrough" Kung Fu Hustle before slowing down his output a bit. Now, with all eyes on the Chinese box office, Chow's latest has become the current hometown champion with over $160 million in receipts.

Veronica Mars (2014)

107 min   -   Crime | Drama | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 62/100 (35 reviews)
Years after walking away from her past as a teenage private eye, Veronica Mars gets pulled back to her hometown - just in time for her high school reunion - in order to help her old flame Logan Echolls, who's embroiled in a murder mystery.
Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

As we enthusiastically prepare to return to Neptune, California, we're thinking back to Veronica and her zesty dialogue, as well as Logan Echolls and the dark matter that surrounds him, which is as irresistible as a black kitten.
