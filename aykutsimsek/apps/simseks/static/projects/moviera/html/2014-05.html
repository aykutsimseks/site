May 2 

The Amazing Spider-Man 2 (2014)

142 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 53/100 (49 reviews)
When New York is put under siege by Oscorp, it is up to Spider-Man to save the city he swore to protect as well as his loved ones.
Director:
Marc Webb
Stars:
Andrew Garfield, Emma Stone, Jamie Foxx, Paul Giamatti
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Even though this second chapter of the rebooted Spider-Man franchise looks like lite-Marvel entertainment akin to Thor, we fear the movie won’t be able to wriggle out from the waves of scrutiny slung at it. There has long been talk of character fatigue since its predecessor didn’t earn more than any of the releases from the Sam Raimi/Tobey Maguire trilogy. Before a frame of the film was shown, there was negative reaction to Mary Jane (Shailene Woodley) being cut from the storyline; after the first few looks, everyone with an Internet connection and passing interest in the project began to wonder if there were too many villains wedged into a single movie. And on and on. We admire Sony’s all-in bet on Peter Parker by giving the green light to two additional sequels and a couple spin-off films (hey,Venom). And if this movie is best remembered as the lead in to The Sinister Six, we’re just fine with that.

Belle (2013)

104 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 64/100 (35 reviews)
The mixed race daughter of a Royal Navy Admiral is raised by her aristocratic great-uncle in 18th century England.
Director:
Amma Asante
Stars:
Gugu Mbatha-Raw, Matthew Goode, Emily Watson, Miranda Richardson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Gugu Mbatha-Raw could find herself in a series of contemporary gowns if her first starring role is as well received as people are expecting; we hear whispers about Amma Asante's historical biography, saying it could be destined for an awards push this year. It appears as though Fox Searchlight is going to let word of mouth do the talking as the studio hasn’t pushed hard on the release.

Walk of Shame (2014)

95 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 25/100 (13 reviews)
A reporter's dream of becoming a news anchor is compromised after a one-night stand leaves her stranded in downtown L.A. without a phone, car, ID or money - and only 8 hours to make it to the most important job interview of her life.
Director:
Steven Brill
Stars:
Elizabeth Banks, James Marsden, Gillian Jacobs, Sarah Wright
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Elizabeth Banks lands her first starring role in what should be a busy year for one of our favorite actresses: we're expecting some sort of Effie Trinket-ness in the two-part finale of The Hunger Games (in the novels, her appearances are diminished), she has at least four projects that could earn a release date in 2014, and she was named the director of Pitch Perfect 2 in January. And while we like the sound of this premise, we can't help but wonder what it would have been like if a woman had been allowed to write the screenplay - and/or direct the project outright. The industry keeps talking about the Bridesmaids effect, but here the decision-makers went with the guy who directed a couple Adam Sandler movies.

Ida (2013)

82 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 91/100 (35 reviews)
Anna, a young novitiate nun in 1960s Poland, is on the verge of taking her vows when she discovers a dark family secret dating back to the years of the Nazi occupation.
Director:
Pawel Pawlikowski
Stars:
Agata Kulesza, Agata Trzebuchowska, Dawid Ogrodnik, Jerzy Trela
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Pawel Pawlikowski makes a hard-right turn from his last movie, oddball thriller The Woman in the Fifth, though the character of Wanda was influenced by a real-life mystery woman from the director's Oxford days. Music, particularly jazz and pop, help shape the mood of the story, embodied by actor Dawid Ogrodnik, who plays young saxophonist Lis.

When the owner of a major elephant camp is murdered, Kham finds himself the number one suspect and on the run from both the police and the deceased's vengeful twin nieces. But luck is on Kham's side when he runs into an Interpol agent sent to Thailand on a secret mission.
Director:

Tony Jaa, RZA, Petchtai Wongkamlao, JeeJa Yanin

Palo Alto (2013)

100 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 69/100 (34 reviews)
Shy, sensitive April is the class virgin, torn between an illicit flirtation with her soccer coach Mr. B and an unrequited crush on sweet stoner Teddy. Emily, meanwhile, offers sexual favors to every boy to cross her path - including both Teddy and his best friend Fred, a live wire without filters or boundaries. As one high school party bleeds into the next - and April and Teddy struggle to admit their mutual affection - Fred's escalating recklessness starts to spiral into chaos.
Director:
Gia Coppola
Stars:
Emma Roberts, James Franco, Jack Kilmer, Zoe Levin
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Gia Coppola, the latest filmmaker from the family, makes her directorial debut with her adaptation of James Franco's short-stories novel, and the source material seems to run parallel to Franco's real-life Instagram exploits, where of late he's flirted openly with a 17-year-old New York City tourist and taken boudoir selfies with Keegan Allen from "Pretty Little Liars". We often wonder if his antics overshadow his films -- even the good ones, which this latest appears to be from the advanced reviews.

132 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi | Thriller

The X-Men send Wolverine to the past in a desperate effort to change history and prevent an event that results in doom for both humans and mutants.
Director:
Bryan Singer
Stars:
Patrick Stewart, Ian McKellen, Hugh Jackman, James McAvoy
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We're pretty into the idea of Wolverine pulling a John Connor by traveling back in time with some harsh news for people who think he's thoroughly gonzo, but we will freely admit that the increasingly jumpy X-timeline has us feeling more lightly slapped than engaged. This is probably a farewell to the original cast too, right -- assuming that Hugh Jackman is inextricably linked to Apocalypse and a third and final Wolverine outing?

Blended (2014)

117 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 31/100 (33 reviews)
After a bad blind date, a man and woman find themselves stuck together at a resort for families, where their attraction grows as their respective kids benefit from the burgeoning relationship.
Director:
Frank Coraci
Stars:
Adam Sandler, Drew Barrymore, Wendi McLendon-Covey, Kevin Nealon
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Hard to blame anyone for thinking the time was right for another Adam Sandler/Drew Barrymore reunion; it's been a decade since 50 First Dates, after all. The idea of lily-white Americans running amuck in Africa feels touchy to us, and we're obsessed with how bad all of the children's haircuts are on both sides of this family. Reports say the tracking has been soft, but the movie-going family is a moving target, and we imagine the new formula of terrible critical reviews/above-average opening weekend will apply here.

Cold in July (2014)

109 min   -   Crime | Thriller

Metascore: 73/100 (30 reviews)
When a protective father meets a murderous ex-con, both need to deviate from the path they are on as they soon find themselves entangled in a downwards spiral of lies and violence while having to confront their own inner psyche.
Director:

Stars:
Michael C. Hall, Sam Shepard, Don Johnson, Vinessa Shaw
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Filmmaker Jim Mickle has flirted with breakthrough success before; both Stake Land and his remake of We Are What We Are pleased genre fans while ekeing out a bit of mainstream success, but its this Texas-set thriller that has earned distinct praise. It's the kind of starring role we'd hope Michael C. Hall would earn as "Dexter" wound down, and we can't wait to see how he handles being an unexpected hero who finds himself pulled into the underworld of his small town.

Words and Pictures (2013)

And shouldn't Wallace Shawn be in the movie?

May 30 

Maleficent (2014)

97 min   -   Action | Adventure | Family | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 56/100 (44 reviews)
A vengeful fairy is driven to curse an infant princess, only to discover that the child may be the one person who can restore peace to their troubled land.
Director:
Robert Stromberg
Stars:
Angelina Jolie, Elle Fanning, Sharlto Copley, Lesley Manville
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Remember when Angelina Jolie almost starred as the evil queen in Snow White and the Huntsman? (It was around the time she passed on Gravity). Well, she was attracted to this project back when Tim Burton was developing it, and she remained on board during the myriad director/co-star shuffles. And we love that she considers this story to be the kind of movie she can watch with her children. Reviews have shown that Jolie is one of the last true movie stars in that even critics are immune to their own neutral reviews and instead favor of her on-screen powers.

A Million Ways to Die in the West (2014)

116 min   -   Comedy | Western

Metascore: 44/100 (43 reviews)
As a cowardly farmer begins to fall for the mysterious new woman in town, he must put his new-found courage to the test when her husband, a notorious gun-slinger, announces his arrival.
Director:
Seth MacFarlane
Stars:
Seth MacFarlane, Charlize Theron, Liam Neeson, Amanda Seyfried
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From the look of things, it's now questionable whether Seth MacFarlane will break the Western-comedy curse with his follow-up to Ted. We once thought it could be the most subversive and beloved addition to the subgenre since Blazing Saddles, but the reviews are near universal in their decree that live action is just not the man's forte.

Night Moves (2013)

112 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Three radical environmentalists look to execute the protest of their lives: the explosion of a hydroelectric dam.
Director:
Kelly Reichardt
Stars:
Jesse Eisenberg, Dakota Fanning, Peter Sarsgaard, Alia Shawkat
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We can't wait to see director Kelly Reichardt's interpretation of the word "thriller" since she makes some of the most measured and observant films we've seen. Love that Jesse Eisenberg is having an indie-movie year with this role as well as his highly praised dual-character work in The Double.

Filth (2013)

97 min   -   Comedy | Crime | Drama

Metascore: 56/100 (24 reviews)
A corrupt, junkie cop with Borderline Personality Disorder attempts to manipulate his way through a promotion in order to win back his wife and daughter while also fighting his own borderline-fueled inner demons.
Director:
Jon S. Baird
Stars:
James McAvoy, Jamie Bell, Eddie Marsan, Imogen Poots
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This art-house release has been timed to coincide with star James McAvoy's X-momentum, but to us it's a litmus test for audience interest in the source material's creator, Trainspotting author Irvine Welsh. It's not a stretch to say the story looks like what might have happened if Renton enrolled in the police academy instead of apartment rentals.

Vi är bäst! (2013)

102 min   -   Drama | Music

Metascore: 87/100 (29 reviews)
Three girls in 1980s Stockholm decide to form a punk band -- despite not having any instruments and being told by everyone that punk is dead.
Director:
Lukas Moodysson
Stars:
Mira Barkhammar, Mira Grosin, Liv LeMoyne, Johan Liljemark
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Lukas Moodysson's debut, Show Me Love was a breakthrough, and he followed up with another hit, Together. Since then his Stateside profile diminished a bit as he went down a darker and worthwhile road with films such as Lilya 4-Ever. But his latest project and its lighter-heartedness has been generating interest and awards around the world with its testimony to the unstoppable power of youth.

Korengal (2014)

84 min   -   Documentary | History | News | War

Metascore: 67/100 (20 reviews)
Korengal picks up where Restrepo left off; the same men, the same valley, the same commanders, but a very different look at the experience of war.
Director:
Sebastian Junger
Stars:
LaMonta Caldwell, Miguel Cortez, Stephen Gillespie, Aron Hijar

The Buzz: Sebastian Junger continues the project he created with Tim Hetherington, his colleague who was killed in Libya just over a year after their film Restrepo debuted at Sundance and went on to earn an Oscar nomination. Korengal is comprised of unused footage from that work and continues the stories of the same men in the same valley of war.

The Grand Seduction (2013)

113 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 57/100 (20 reviews)
To survive, a dying Newfoundland fishing village must convince a young doctor to take up residence by any means necessary.
Director:
Don McKellar
Stars:
Taylor Kitsch, Brendan Gleeson, Liane Balaban, Gordon Pinsent
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Don McKellar has one of the greatest indie filmographies around, and maybe his remake of the Quebecois dramedy Seducing Doctor Lewis will help introduce Taylor Kitsch as a potential leading man in art-house circles. What's not being shown in the movie's marketing is the fact that Kitsch's doctor gets busted for cocaine possession at the beginning of the story.
