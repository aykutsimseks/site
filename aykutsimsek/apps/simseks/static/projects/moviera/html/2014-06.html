June 6 

Edge of Tomorrow (2014)

113 min   -   Action | Adventure | Mystery | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 71/100 (43 reviews)
A military officer is brought into an alien war against an extraterrestrial enemy who can reset the day and know the future. When this officer is enabled with the same power, he teams up with a Special Forces warrior to try and end the war.
Director:
Doug Liman
Stars:
Tom Cruise, Emily Blunt, Bill Paxton, Brendan Gleeson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Doug Liman gets to toy with visual effects for the first time since Jumper (which has become a cult movie), and Tom Cruise knows the genre well by now - it has become his place of assured career longevity. This particular sci-fi Groundhog Day has more expectations that Source Code had on its shoulders, and the great reviews bode well for a huge worldwide embrace. More than anything we're happy for Emily Blunt, who is pretty much our favorite Hollywooder.

The Fault in Our Stars (2014)

126 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 69/100 (45 reviews)
Two teens, both who have different cancer conditions, fall in love after meeting at a cancer support group.
Director:
Josh Boone
Stars:
Shailene Woodley, Ansel Elgort, Nat Wolff, Laura Dern
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: While studios still mine young-adult literature offerings for potential movie franchises, it's worth noting the breathless anticipation for this adaptation of YouTube hero John Green's novel is, like The Perks of Being a Wallflower, set in the real world - miles away from effects-heavy dystopian societies. Of course it's a bit odd to say this as Shailene Woodley has just started filming the sequel to Divergent, but we're happy to see that she already has career options. We're curious as to director Josh Boone's next move; his earlier movie, Stuck in Love, is still finding its audience, too.

Obvious Child (2014)

84 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 76/100 (35 reviews)
A twenty-something comedienne's unplanned pregnancy forces her to confront the realities of independent womanhood for the first time.
Director:
Gillian Robespierre
Stars:
Jenny Slate, Jake Lacy, Gaby Hoffmann, Gabe Liedman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We really got into "Kroll Show" because of the PubLIZity skits featuring Jenny Slate, who presents a series of on-screen characters with whom we want to be friends. In her first starring role, she fearlessly shows how to not beat yourself up over a lapse in judgment - a lesson most of us could use, right?

Borgman (2013)

113 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 66/100 (22 reviews)
A vagrant enters the lives of an arrogant upper-class family, turning their lives into a psychological nightmare in the process.
Director:
Alex van Warmerdam
Stars:
Jan Bijvoet, Hadewych Minis, Jeroen Perceval, Alex van Warmerdam
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Alex van Warmerdam makes statements that are intriguing as his film: "I'm a little disappointed about how nasty this film became. I think it has something to do with the fact that I'm getting old. Some people are getting milder when they get older, but I feel I get more and more nasty." Seek out this work before it is inevitably turned into an English-language thriller.

Supermensch: The Legend of Shep Gordon (2013)

85 min   -   Documentary | Biography

Metascore: 64/100 (26 reviews)
The life and career of the legendary Hollywood insider, Shep Gordon.
Directors:
Beth Aala | Mike Myers
Stars:
Shep Gordon, Sylvester Stallone, Michael Douglas, Alice Cooper
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Most of the pre-release attention here has been focused on co-director's Mike Meyers's professional whereabouts, but his subject matter's life is heaps more fascinating if you're a fan of Hollywood insider tales - especially ones that show how some players, like Shep Gordon, prefer to remain behind the scenes with their ethics in tact.

The Sacrament (2013)

99 min   -   Horror | Thriller

Metascore: 49/100 (21 reviews)
A news team trails a man as he travels into the world of Eden Parish to find his missing sister, where it becomes apparent that this paradise may not be as it seems.
Director:
Ti West
Stars:
Joe Swanberg, AJ Bowen, Kentucker Audley, Amy Seimetz
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ti West is a young master of suspense filmmaking, and though his partnership with VICE Media reeking of corporate-media synergy (this fictional news crew works for that real-world organization), we're down for this documented voyage into a fringe community where character actor Gene Jones is the spiritual leader.

Ping Pong Summer (2014)

92 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 52/100 (18 reviews)
A family vacation during the summer of 1985 changes everything for a teenage boy obsessed with ping pong.
Director:
Michael Tully
Stars:
Marcello Conte, Judah Friedlander, Amy Sedaris, Lea Thompson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Perhaps if you cribbed bits from Adventureland, Napoleon Dynamite, and Wild Style you might have the components of Michael Tully's latest indie outing, which seems to be miles apart thematically from his earlier features: Septien and Cocaine Angel.

Trust Me (2013)

90 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 51/100 (14 reviews)
In an attempt to sign a Hollywood starlet, struggling talent agent and former child star Howard Holloway must contend with her volatile father, a scheming long-time rival, and a producer and casting director who despise him.
Director:
Clark Gregg
Stars:
Clark Gregg, Felicity Huffman, Allison Janney, William H. Macy
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This mission seems like nothing compared to what Agent Coulson has seen.

Anna (2013)

99 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 42/100 (7 reviews)
A man with the ability to enter peoples' memories takes on the case of a brilliant, troubled sixteen-year-old girl to determine whether she is a sociopath or a victim of trauma.
Director:
Jorge Dorado
Stars:
Mark Strong, Taissa Farmiga, Brian Cox, Indira Varma
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Jorge Dorado has worked closely with Guillermo del Toro, Pedro Almodovar, and others as an assistant director, so we're interested in his take on the otherwise familiar story of gift detective and a troubled young woman who may/may not be a murderess. It feels like a paycheck movie for the cast, but at least you get Mark Strong as the detective instead of Nicolas Cage (or even John Cusack)

June 13 

22 Jump Street (2014)

112 min   -   Action | Comedy | Crime

Metascore: 71/100 (46 reviews)
After making their way through high school (twice), big changes are in store for officers Schmidt and Jenko when they go deep undercover at a local college.
Directors:
Phil Lord | Christopher Miller
Stars:
Channing Tatum, Jonah Hill, Ice Cube, Nick Offerman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: So, it turns out that for all of Charming Tatum's talent and musculature, his career stabilizer will be the sequel to an unexpected hit (The Lego Movie doesn't count - voice roles never do). And not to compare them directly, but it's cool to live in a moment where Jonah Hill is the bigger movie star. More than anything, we're curious as to how you come close to matching the surprise factor and luring an audience for a second go-round, even with all the key players back in place.

How to Train Your Dragon 2 (2014)

102 min   -   Animation | Action | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Fantasy

Metascore: 76/100 (39 reviews)
When Hiccup and Toothless discover an ice cave that is home to hundreds of new wild dragons and the mysterious Dragon Rider, the two friends find themselves at the center of a battle to protect the peace.
Director:
Dean DeBlois
Stars:
Jay Baruchel, Cate Blanchett, Gerard Butler, Craig Ferguson
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: There's a surprising lack of family entertainment this summer, so look for Hiccup and Toothless to rule the skies until Planes: Fire and Rescue arrives later next month. Where are the other family movies this summer?

Hellion (2014)

99 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 55/100 (24 reviews)
When motocross and heavy metal obsessed thirteen-year-old Jacob's increasing delinquent behavior forces CPS to place his little brother, Wes, with his aunt, Jacob and his emotionally absent father, Hollis, must finally take responsibility for their actions and for each other in order to bring Wes home.
Director:
Kat Candler
Stars:
Aaron Paul, Juliette Lewis, Josh Wiggins, Deke Garner
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Themes of wayward youth and damaged men tend to play well with festival audiences, so it's no surprise that director Kat Candler's drama, which took shape as a short film nurtured by the Sundance Film Festival, played well there this past January. And we all want Aaron Paul to thrive in the indie-film world, right? We imagine he'll take the Jennifer Lawrence/James Franco approach of balancing major-studio work with smaller projects like this one.

The Rover (2014)

103 min   -   Crime | Drama

Metascore: 64/100 (38 reviews)
10 years after a global economic collapse, a hardened loner pursues the men who stole his only possession, his car. Along the way, he captures one of the thieves' brother, and the duo form an uneasy bond during the dangerous journey.
Director:
David Michôd
Stars:
Guy Pearce, Robert Pattinson, Scoot McNairy, David Field
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: "10 years after a global economic collapse," ... so David Michod's follow-up to the excellent Animal Kingdom is set in the very, very near future. If that's too upsetting to ponder, focus on the cool fact that Kingdom star Joel Edgerton co-wrote this screenplay with Michod, and people who spent obsessive amounts of time hating on Edward Cullen might find a way to love Edward Pattinson. And not that Oscars are everything, but can Guy Pearce finally get a nomination?

The Signal (2014)

97 min   -   Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 53/100 (33 reviews)
On a road trip, Nic and two friends are drawn to an isolated area by a computer genius. When everything suddenly goes dark, Nic regains consciousness - only to find himself in a waking nightmare.
Director:
William Eubank
Stars:
Brenton Thwaites, Olivia Cooke, Beau Knapp, Laurence Fishburne
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From the sound of things, what William Eubank's sci-fi oddity makes up for any lacking narrative form with heaps of style and mood, a refreshing break from the half-baked intellectualism that hamstrung Transcendence and alienated genre fans. At the very least we root for this movie to achieve cult status on par with last year's Europa Report.

Think Like a Man Too (2014)

106 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 38/100 (30 reviews)
All the couples are back for a wedding in Las Vegas, but plans for a romantic weekend go awry when their various misadventures get them into some compromising situations that threaten to derail the big event.
Director:
Tim Story
Stars:
Kevin Hart, Gabrielle Union, Wendi McLendon-Covey, La La Anthony
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Everyone's back in place for the sequel to the breakout hit that outperformed most Tyler Perry movies almost 2:1. While Kevin Hart might be the most famous cast member, can we take a moment to celebrate Gabrielle Union and Meagan Good's respective career longevity? Both women have been working steadily since the early 90s.

Jersey Boys (2014)

134 min   -   Biography | Drama | Music

Metascore: 54/100 (44 reviews)
The story of four young men from the wrong side of the tracks in New Jersey who came together to form the iconic 1960s rock group The Four Seasons.
Director:
Clint Eastwood
Stars:
John Lloyd Young, Erich Bergen, Michael Lomenda, Vincent Piazza
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's always a risk to pull a musical off the stage in attempt to make filmed entertainment, but it's also a no-brainer to hand over this particular story - essentially a four-character biography - to an established director. Does that mean we think Clint Eastwood was the right choice to helm the project? No. Do we think it's near impossible to market a musical with no stars? Yes. And if we're at all correct, we think this explains the lack of a push for this summer release, which might have done better in the fall.

Third Person (2013)

137 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 38/100 (33 reviews)
Three interlocking love stories involving three couples in three cities: Rome, Paris, and New York.
Director:
Paul Haggis
Stars:
Liam Neeson, Mila Kunis, Adrien Brody, Olivia Wilde
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Paul Haggis tried to interlock with his writing style that initially worked for Crash (though what is that movie's true legacy?), but the results this time around have underwhelmed critics. Sony Pictures Classics only puts out a stinker every now and then, and we wouldn't be surprised one bit if Haggis's next project is about a disillusioned filmmaker.

An actress attempts to convince a director how she's perfect for a role in his upcoming production.

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Roman Polanski's latest has been embraced by critics who love seeing him back in psychosexual territory with a stripped down two-hander that has no awards ambition and able to wipe away memory of Polanski's shockingly mechanical reproduction of the play Carnage.

Le Chef (2012)

84 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 43/100 (13 reviews)
A veteran chef faces off against his restaurant group's new CEO, who wants to the establishment to lose a star from its rating in order to bring in a younger chef who specializes in molecular gastronomy.
Director:
Daniel Cohen
Stars:
Jean Reno, Michaël Youn, Raphaëlle Agogué, Julien Boisselier
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: You could bide your time while waiting for the next installment of the "Top Chef" franchise with this slightly aged French comedy (it bowed there in early 2012) that certainly is banking on the appeal of star Jean Reno and the topical nature of its story.

Dom över död man (2012)

126 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 60/100 (15 reviews)
A story based on the life of journalist Torgny Segerstedt, who alerted the Swedish public to the threat of Fascism in the 1930s.
Director:
Jan Troell
Stars:
Jesper Christensen, Pernilla August, Ulla Skoog, Björn Granath
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Film buffs remember when director Jan Troell's The Emigrants earned 5 Oscar nominations at the 1973 ceremonies (including Best Picture); more recently he earned considerable attention for Everlasting Moments. Really, though, he's been working the art-house scene since the 60s and as a contemporary of ours noted, that era's black-and-white aesthetic is enjoying a resurgence with this film as well as the recent indie triumph, Pawel Pawlikowski's Ida.

A Summer's Tale (1996)

113 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 91/100 (14 reviews)
A shy maths graduate takes a holiday in Dinard before starting his first job. He hopes his sort-of girlfriend will join him, but soon strikes up a friendship with another girl working in town. She in turn introduces him to a further young lady who fancies him. Thus the quiet young lad finds he is having to do some tricky juggling in territory new to him.
Director:
Eric Rohmer
Stars:
Melvil Poupaud, Amanda Langlet, Gwenaëlle Simon, Aurelia Nolin
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Eric Rohmer's third movement in his Four Seasons film cycle was the only one to never receive a theatrical release in the US, but here it is some 18 years after its initial run. (Here are the other three for you potential completists.) How fortunate are we to be able to watch star Melvil Poupaud is this transitional starring role so soon after his beautiful performance in Xavier Dolan's Laurence Anyways.

Norte, hangganan ng kasaysayan (2013)

250 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 81/100 (10 reviews)
An embittered law student commits a brutal double murder; a family man takes the fall and is forced into a harsh prison sentence; a mother and her two children wander the countryside looking for some kind of redemption.
Director:
Lav Diaz
Stars:
Sid Lucero, Angeli Bayani, Archie Alemania, Soliman Cruz
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This Cannes-approved film is a re-imagining of Dostoevsky's Crime and Punishment set on the Filipino island of Luzon. Considering for a moment the state Dostoyevsky was in when he wrote this novel (broke from gambling, as all great writers should be when they're young) it will be exceedingly worthwhile to watch his story in the hands of writer/director Lav Diaz, who is in the prime of his life.

165 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 32/100 (38 reviews)
Autobots must escape sight from a bounty hunter who has taken control of the human serendipity: Unexpectedly, Optimus Prime and his remaining gang turn to a mechanic and his daughter for help.

Watch Trailer

Michael Bay shuffled his cast and restyled the vehicles for the fourth chapter of his global juggernaut, which will give new leading man Mark Wahlberg a second taste of international success after Ted's record-shattering run.

Begin Again (2013)

104 min   -   Drama | Music

Metascore: 62/100 (39 reviews)
A chance encounter between a disgraced music-business executive and a young singer-songwriter new to Manhattan turns into a promising collaboration between the two talents.
Director:
John Carney
Stars:
Keira Knightley, Mark Ruffalo, Adam Levine, Hailee Steinfeld
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We'd love to see someone take this genre back to, say, the 1970s and make a more uncompromising and stylish picture like All That Jazz instead of watching hearts break and mend in standard time during the age of digital downloads. We hear that Once director John Carney missed an opportunity to show more of the disintegrating marriage of Mark Ruffalo and Catherine Keener's characters -- which sounds like it could have been its own film -- and Keira Knightley doesn't quite sell the songs, but there's comfort in this formula.

Snowpiercer (2013)

126 min   -   Action | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 84/100 (37 reviews)
Set in a future where a failed climate-change experiment kills all life on the planet except for a lucky few who boarded the Snowpiercer, a train that travels around the globe, where a class system emerges.
Director:
Joon-ho Bong
Stars:
Chris Evans, Jamie Bell, Tilda Swinton, Ed Harris
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Grr, yes, we know that Harvey Weinstein has diced up Bong Joon-ho's artistic vision, but what do you expect when a filmmaker goes into business with that particular studio? It's a hot-blooded union that sometimes results in successful Oscar campaigns and other times, unforgivable tragedies. And don't forget the number of projects that get a lot of pre-release attention and then are quietly pushed out to theaters. Or worse: just shelved. Genre fans will grab ahold of this one, but we fear its chances of crossover success - even with Captain American facing off against Social Services - are minimal.

They Came Together (2014)

83 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 60/100 (27 reviews)
A small business owner is about to lose her shop to a major corporate development.
Director:
David Wain
Stars:
Paul Rudd, Amy Poehler, Bill Hader, Cobie Smulders
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: After two big-studio comedies (Role Models and the less successful Wanderlust), director David Wain returns to familiar indie territory with an all-star cast from his on-call roster. Wain has been ahead of his contemporaries -- he created the popular web series "Wainy Days" back in 2007 -- so we imagine he's going to gauge whether or not his latest is a success by its VOD/streaming tally, not its very limited theatrical release. We hope more than anything that he's able to proceed with his idea of making a Wet Hot American Summer prequel with his original cast playing even younger versions of their characters.

Whitey: United States of America v. James J. Bulger (2014)

107 min   -   Documentary | Biography | Crime

Metascore: 74/100 (18 reviews)
A look at James 'Whitey' Bulger, one of the most infamous criminals in American history.
Director:
Joe Berlinger
Stars:
Stephen Rakes, James 'Whitey' Bulger, Tommy Donahue, David Boeri
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We hear Joel Berlinger's documentary on Whitey Bulger is quite clinical - no big surprise after you take in the project's mouthful of a title. So this is an opportunity for the uninitiated to share a row with crime buffs and dig into the myriad ways in which police forces and federal agencies work with organized crime operatives. Then you can see the American Hustle version next year when Black Mass, starring a Johnny Depp and a partially bald cap as Bulger, is released next fall.

105 min   -   Documentary | Biography | Crime | News

The story of programming prodigy and information activist Aaron Swartz, who took his own life at the age of 26.
Director:
Brian Knappenberger
Stars:
Aaron Swartz, Tim Berners-Lee, Cindy Cohn, Gabriella Coleman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: One of the most forward-thinking minds of his generation was quieted much too soon, and please please please Hollywood do not take the biopic route with Mr. Swartz just yet.

Yves Saint Laurent (2014)

106 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 51/100 (25 reviews)
A look at the life of French designer Yves Saint Laurent from the beginning of his career in 1958 when he met his lover and business partner, Pierre Berge.
Director:
Jalil Lespert
Stars:
Pierre Niney, Guillaume Gallienne, Charlotte Le Bon, Laura Smet
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We love to watch fashion-world biographies for their art direction and the opportunity to watch a performer try to mimic a real-life eccentric, but when it comes to the actual narrative, we'd typically rather watch a documentary over a feature. Consider the case of Yves Saint Laurent, whose career began when Christian Dior died unexpected at the age of 52, positioning the untested designer as the heir to the house of Dior  only to find himself dropped into a life-altering stint in the French army three years later. And that's just the beginning of his adult life, which is worthy of a detailed, sprawling documentary - not merely two competing biopics in the same year (Saint Laurent screened at Cannes back in May).
