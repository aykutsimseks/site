July 4 

Deliver Us from Evil (2014)

118 min   -   Crime | Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 40/100 (32 reviews)
New York police officer Ralph Sarchie investigates a series of crimes. He joins forces with an unconventional priest, schooled in the rites of exorcism, to combat the possessions that are terrorizing their city.
Director:
Scott Derrickson
Stars:
Eric Bana, Édgar Ramírez, Olivia Munn, Chris Coy
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Scott Derrickson has directed two similarly themed hits already: The Exorcism of Emily Rose and Sinister, and the studio behind this thriller has effectively message the based-on-true-events nature of the story - something that tends to sway audiences. Look for Eric Bana to get the Ethan Hawke career bump if Deliver does just that, and we think there's something to be seen in him pairing up with Edgar Ramirez, even if the latter cannot help being ridiculously sexy when playing a priest.

Tammy (2014)

97 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 39/100 (36 reviews)
After losing her job and learning that her husband has been unfaithful, a woman hits the road with her profane, hard-drinking grandmother.
Director:
Ben Falcone
Stars:
Melissa McCarthy, Susan Sarandon, Kathy Bates, Allison Janney
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Weird to realize it's been 23 years since Susan Sarandon was behind the wheel ... It's remarkable to see Melissa McCarthy opening her own film over Fourth of July weekend with nary a superhero or sequel in direct competition (though even with a massive drop T4 should top the box office in its second outing). We are wondering if the marketing campaign has focused a bit too much on the fast-food joint robbery and showing Tammy as a crook/vigilante instead of spotlighting the fact that her character is facing a crisis -- a much more relatable and cathartic story idea. This road comedy is also a major relationship tester since her McCarthy's husband, Ben Falcone, is making his debut as director.

Earth to Echo (2014)

91 min   -   Adventure | Family | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 53/100 (31 reviews)
After receiving a bizarre series of encrypted messages, a group of kids embark on an adventure with an alien who needs their help.
Director:
Dave Green
Stars:
Teo Halm, Astro, Reese Hartwig, Ella Wahlestedt
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If Super 8 was a love letter to 1980s-style filmmaking, this adventure story looks to pay a similar form of tribute with its tech-savvy teens and their bicycle-based campaign to help the alien life form they discover. There's even a character named "Munch" - a direct nod to The Goonies. Relativity Media isn't established enough as a distributor to create a blockbuster hit, but Munch and his pals could still perform well if their real-life peers turn up on opening weekend.

America: Imagine the World Without Her (2014)

105 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 15/100 (11 reviews)
A story that questions the shaming of the US through revisionist history, lies and omissions by educational institutions, political organizations, Alinsky, Barack Obama, Hillary Clinton and other progressives to destroy America.
Directors:
Dinesh D'Souza | John Sullivan
Stars:
Dinesh D'Souza, Barack Obama, Josh Bonzie, Rich Bentz
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Two indisputable facts about filmmaker Dinesh D'Souza: his last project, 2016: Obama's America, is the second-highest grossing political documentary in its titular country; he was found guilty of campaign finance law violation earlier this year. And a general fact: as a felon, his films have replaced his right to vote or hold political office.

Land Ho! (2014)

95 min   -   Adventure | Comedy

Metascore: 68/100 (31 reviews)
A pair of former brothers-in-law embark on a road trip through Iceland.
Directors:
Aaron Katz | Martha Stephens
Stars:
Earl Lynn Nelson, Paul Eenhoorn, Daníel Gylfason, Þrúður Kristjánsdóttir
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Co-writers/directors Aaron Katz and Martha Stephens have earned acclaim for crafting a story about getting older that allows their adventure-seeking duo to share thoughtful, sober, and existential moments -- as well as some vulgar times, too. No wonder we keep thinking it's a documentary. Note that co-star Earl Lynn Nelson is related to co-director Stephens, who put the story idea in motion when she thought of what it would be like to travel to Iceland with Nelson.

96 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Director:
Pascal Chaumeil
Stars:
Pierce Brosnan, Toni Collette, Imogen Poots, Aaron Paul
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Save the U.S. remake of Fever Pitch, Nick Hornby novels usually make for successful adaptations, but this suicide-pact dramedy from director Pascal Chaumeil (whose Heartbreaker is a delight) might join the shallow pile of failures. When you look at the cast, it's hard to rectify how savage the reviews have been.

Sex Tape (2014)

94 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 36/100 (36 reviews)

Director:
Jake Kasdan
Stars:
Jason Segel, Cameron Diaz, Rob Corddry, Ellie Kemper
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It makes total mainstream comedy sense to reunite Bad Teacher, the gym teacher who ultimately wooed her, and their director in a new movie that steers an R-rated course through married life and a couple's attempt to spice it up. We don't think the movie will reach the same box-office heights as Neighbors, but Cameron Diaz just worked The Other Woman into a huge success and the big screen has been surprisingly Segel-free for the most part, so we think word-of-mouth will keep the movie going after a successful opening weekend.

Hercules (2014)

98 min   -   Action | Adventure

Metascore: 47/100 (25 reviews)
Having endured his legendary twelve labors, Hercules, the Greek demigod, has his life as a sword-for-hire tested when the King of Thrace and his daughter seek his aid in defeating a tyrannical warlord.
Director:
Brett Ratner
Stars:
Dwayne Johnson, John Hurt, Ian McShane, Joseph Fiennes
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The Brett Ratner factor is the alienating element here, but Dwayne Johnson leading an army that looks similar to Daenerys Targaryen's might be enough to defeat hack filmmaking. The Rock is the closest thing Hollywood has to a male movie star (his movies grossed $1.3 billion last year) so the biggest labor here is surpassing box-office expectations, which is always a challenge with the sword-and-sandals genre. Johnson and company might look overseas for their greatest victories.

94 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Romance

Would it be against his beliefs to really earn a PG-13 rating at this point in his career? Remember that Stand by Me was rated R.
