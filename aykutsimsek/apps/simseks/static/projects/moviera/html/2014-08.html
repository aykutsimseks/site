August 1 

Guardians of the Galaxy (2014)

121 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 76/100 (46 reviews)
A group of intergalactic criminals are forced to work together to stop a fanatical warrior from taking control of the universe.
Director:
James Gunn
Stars:
Chris Pratt, Vin Diesel, Bradley Cooper, Zoe Saldana
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: An entity from a far corner of the Marvel universe has found itself positioned as the unlikely hero of the flailing summer box office; even the people behind Honest Trailers seemed to think Guardians might be Marvel's Green Lantern. Thinking of Guardians as a champion in this way seems like a classic case of Internet goodwill, but we see the movie more as Marvel's potential cult classic. Troma veteran James Gunn is the director after all, and we love the his unexpected path through Hollywood has brought him to this point. Let's remember that Guardians is a standalone Marvel movie with no ties to its previous films or more recognizable characters. That's a tough sell to a wide audience. In fact, our colleague Ray Subers at Box Office Mojo has already wondered if Guardians is this year's Pacific Rim - an instance of online hype outweighing general interest. Personally, GOTG looks like a Marvel-take on a TV show we miss quite a bit: "Farscape." And that's more than enough to get us to see the movie in theaters.

Director:

Stars:
Brendan Gleeson, Chris O'Dowd, Kelly Reilly, Aidan Gillen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Brendan Gleeson also starred in writer John Michael McDonagh directorial debut, The Guard, a rare instance of a deftly handled crime/comedy that earned Gleeson a Golden Globe nomination and proved that some screenwriters really can get behind the camera and direct, too. Calvary might seem a bit serious and grave on its surface but I understand McDonagh has layered in plenty of black comedy as well, though critical response has been a bit more divided than it was for The Guard. It's also worth knowing that Gleeson was drinking with McDonagh when he conceived this story at a pub in Galway.

The Buzz: This adaptation of Lowis Lowry's celebrated children's novel (which belongs to a loose collection of four books) takes certain liberties with its source material: Jonas is aged up by several years; Rosemary, more of a conceptual character in the novel, was fleshed-out and red-lip lined by Taylor Swift for the movie. Sin City: A Dame to Kill For (2014)

102 min   -   Crime | Thriller

Metascore: 46/100 (38 reviews)
Some of Sin City's most hard-boiled citizens cross paths with a few of its more reviled inhabitants.
Directors:
Frank Miller | Robert Rodriguez
Stars:
Mickey Rourke, Jessica Alba, Josh Brolin, Joseph Gordon-Levitt
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Back in the mid-2000s, Sin City and 300 exposed the world of non-superhero comics to sunlight and became so successful that people like David Fincher looked to turn material such as Charles Burns's Black Hole into movies. Outsiders including Preacher, Sandman , Y: The Last Man were also candidates ... A decade later, it's a different landscape - one clearly dominated by the Marvel Cinematic Universe and DC Comics' tentative first steps into franchise-dom. How does Sin City's sequel fit into the frame? I'm not quite sure. The original didn't have the worldwide reach that 300 achieved, but it did earn just shy of $75 million in the U.S. (and $85 million overseas) but not only was that a different time - it was a different time of year (an early-summer release vs. late in the season here). I just don't see Marv, Nancy, and the others carving out the same territory, and it's always hard to gauge the behavior of a cult audience when viewing options abound. Looked what happened with Kick-Ass 2 - maybe the best direct comparison.
