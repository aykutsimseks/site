December 5 

Wild (2014)

115 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 76/100 (47 reviews)
A chronicle of one woman's 1,100-mile solo hike undertaken as a way to recover from a recent catastrophe.
Director:
Jean-Marc Vallée
Stars:
Reese Witherspoon, Laura Dern, Gaby Hoffmann, Michiel Huisman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Wild has been on my must-see list since it premiered to rave reviews at the Toronto International Film Festival. When I saw the first trailer, I couldn't help but notice the similarities to Sean Penn's Into the Wild, the story of Christopher McCandless's journey to Alaska, one of my favorite films. But this film is no copycat. Based on a true story, the film documents Cheryl Strayed's 1,100-mile solo hike on the Pacific Crest trail. While the hike itself is enough to make me want to watch, the biggest draw for me is the fact that she takes this journey as a way to recover from the loss of her mother. Critics are already calling this Reese Witherspoon's best role of her career and I can't wait to see it. -- Michelle Nelson

Director:

Stars:

We really hope Dennis O'Hare plays a creep, and the movie manages to strike a tone that feels like The Descent meets Indiana Jones.

It's a pity that Meester's character seems so self-hating in the trailer.

December 12 

Exodus: Gods and Kings (2014)

150 min   -   Action | Adventure | Drama

Metascore: 52/100 (42 reviews)
The defiant leader Moses rises up against the Egyptian Pharaoh Ramses, setting 600,000 slaves on a monumental journey of escape from Egypt and its terrifying cycle of deadly plagues.
Director:
Ridley Scott
Stars:
Christian Bale, Joel Edgerton, Ben Kingsley, Sigourney Weaver
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ridley Scott's don't-care attitude toward the uproar over his casting white actors as Egyptians has received more attention than any aspect of this movie, but we don't think the people who have taken offense are also the target audience here. Remember that Noah experienced similar backlash before its release, and consider the liberties that story took and its considerable success. At least Christian Bale's Moses won't have to deal with rock giants and fallen angels.

Top Five (2014)

102 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 81/100 (37 reviews)
A comedian tries to make it as a serious actor when his reality-TV star fiancée talks him into broadcasting their wedding on her TV show.
Director:
Chris Rock
Stars:
Chris Rock, Rosario Dawson, Gabrielle Union, Kevin Hart
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The industry loves Chris Rock's celebrity satire, but we're interested to see if the movie-going public embraces the comedy. Rock has hazed the entertainment industry for its racial stereotypes; way more importantly, he has commented on the Ferguson verdict, Obama's legacy, the allegations against Bill Cosby ... He's openly criticized Paramount for changing the release plan for the movie in favor of going wide on opening weekend. Is there any other actor-filmmaker like him in Hollywood? No. And now it seems he's put a movie where his mouth is.

Inherent Vice (2014)

148 min   -   Crime | Drama | Mystery

Metascore: 81/100 (43 reviews)
In 1970, drug-fueled Los Angeles private investigator Larry "Doc" Sportello investigates the disappearance of a former girlfriend.
Director:
Paul Thomas Anderson
Stars:
Joaquin Phoenix, Josh Brolin, Owen Wilson, Katherine Waterston
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Every Paul Thomas Anderson movie since Boogie Nights has been wildly anticipated and if anyone can turn a Thomas Pynchon novel into a successful film, it's Anderson - especially with a free-ranging cast you want to root for. And it seems as though Joaquin Phoenix is done with the self-satire bit and is back to mainly being a fine actor.

Free the Nipple (2014)

84 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 33/100 (8 reviews)
An army of passionate women launch a revolution to "Free the Nipple" and decriminalize the female body. Based on a true story, this mass movement of topless women, armed with First Amendment lawyers, graffiti installations and national publicity stunts, invade New York City to protest the backwards censorship laws in the USA.
Director:
Lina Esco
Stars:
Lina Esco, Lola Kirke, Monique Coleman, Zach Grenier
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: With Pussy Riot's imprisonment, the Hobby Lobby decision, and Riot Girl bands reforming, it cool to see how this movie, which sprung from a real-world activist campaign that was backed by Michelle Rodriguez, Keira Knightley, Lena Dunham and others, fits into the larger conversation about women's rights and social justice. Speaking of Ms. Dunham, Nipple director/star Linda Esco just joined the cast of "Open", Ryan Murphy's sexually provocative pilot at HBO.

The Color of Time (2012)

73 min   -   Biography | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 34/100 (12 reviews)
A poetic road trip through Pulitzer prize-winning CK Williams' life.
Directors:
Edna Luise Biesold | Sarah-Violet Bliss | Gabrielle Demeestere | Alexis Gambis | Brooke Goldfinch | Shripriya Mahesh | Pamela Romanowsky | Bruce Thierry Cheung | Tine Thomasen | Virginia Urreiztieta | Omar Zúñiga Hidalgo
Stars:
James Franco, Mila Kunis, Jessica Chastain, Zach Braff
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: James Franco's love for poetry and film reaches a zenith with this, his impressionistic take on the life and work of poet CK Williams that was made with a handful of actor friends (Kunis, Chastain, Braff, others) and a production crew comprised of NYU film students -- including 12 different directors from the University. Titled Tar upon its debut in 2012, the two-year period in which the project went unreleased synchs with the underwhelming reviews; it's a smart time, though, to release something with either Franco or Chastain in it since they are both making headlines.

Director:
Peter Jackson
Stars:
Ian McKellen, Martin Freeman, Richard Armitage, Cate Blanchett
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Confessions of a former Unexpected Journey hater: It was The Desolation of Smaug that helped me to re-engage with Bilbo and the Dwarves and remember that, with the release of Five Armies, it will soon be possible to watch the entire Hobbit/LOTR saga in proper sequential order.

And if "Regal Armor" Thorin is made available as a toy, I'll buy two (one to keep in its packaging, the other to cast love spells upon). The same goes for Bard in any form.

118 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Family | Musical

Metascore: 33/100 (38 reviews)
A foster kid, who lives with her mean foster mom, sees her life change when business tycoon and New York mayoral candidate Will Stacks makes a thinly-veiled campaign move and takes her in.
Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: When was the last time a family movie found itself caught up in a real-world drama involving computer hackers, embarrassing leaked corporate documents, and an alarming note that a "big surprise" is coming from the aforementioned hackers on Christmas Day? It's enough to overshadow this particular movie's good intentions and the welcome return of Quvenzhané Wallis to a starring role, though we can't help but wonder what direction the story might've taken if Willow Smith hadn't quit the production (picture the PR nightmare Sony would have found itself in, however, given the mean-spirited internal emails about Willow and her brother).

Goodbye to All That (2014)

87 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 62/100 (14 reviews)

Director:
Angus MacLachlan
Stars:

Watch Trailer

Since American movies so clumsily handled the idea of friends-with-benefits and have tended to chastise the notion of having a trusty one night stand, we're open to following Paul Schneider on this particular path to sexual and romantic enlightenment.

Unbroken (2014)

137 min   -   Biography | Drama | Sport | War

Metascore: 59/100 (48 reviews)
After a near-fatal plane crash in WWII, Olympian Louis Zamperini spends a harrowing 47 days in a raft with two fellow crewmen before he's caught by the Japanese navy and sent to a prisoner-of-war camp.
Director:
Angelina Jolie
Stars:
Jack O'Connell, Miyavi, Domhnall Gleeson, Garrett Hedlund
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From its memorable opening scene forward, Unbroken is a gorgeously shot, well-acted, and assuredly directed WWII prestige picture. Perhaps its main shortcoming is its screenplay, which never fully animates the the movie despite being based on a remarkable life story and directly tied to its source material, Laura Hillenbrand's biography of Louis Zamperini. Everyone involved with the physical production has greatness in them, including Angelina Jolie as a director; I know she will get her hands on an ace screenplay and soon make her true mark as a filmmaker. As Zamperini, Jack O'Connell shows his extraordinary value as a leading man; as good are Miyavi and Garrett Helund in their supporting roles.

Director:

Aside from Marshall, the biggest winner here could be Chris Pine, who has received excellent reviews as Cinderella's preening Prince.

The Gambler (2014)

111 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Lit professor and gambler Jim Bennett's debt causes him to borrow money from his mother and a loan shark. Further complicating his situation is his relationship with one of his students. Will Bennett risk his life for a second chance?
Director:
Rupert Wyatt
Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Interview (2014)

112 min   -   Action | Comedy

Metascore: 52/100 (33 reviews)
Dave Skylark and producer Aaron Rapoport run the celebrity tabloid show "Skylark Tonight." When they land an interview with a surprise fan, North Korean dictator Kim Jong-un, they are recruited by the CIA to turn their trip to Pyongyang into an assassination mission.
Directors:
Evan Goldberg | Seth Rogen
Stars:
James Franco, Seth Rogen, Randall Park, Lizzy Caplan
Watch Trailer

The movie that brought Sony to its knees at hackers had a field day with the company's data, media, and emails will not be denied as several hundred independent theaters stepped up to ensure a limited release on Christmas Day despite threats to those who made, marketed, and will potentially see it. Team America, truly.

Selma (2014)

128 min   -   Biography | Drama | History

Metascore: 89/100 (46 reviews)
A chronicle of Martin Luther King's campaign to secure equal voting rights via an epic march from Selma to Montgomery, Alabama in 1965.
Director:
Ava DuVernay
Stars:
David Oyelowo, Carmen Ejogo, Tim Roth, Lorraine Toussaint
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ava DuVernay bowled over the film-festival circuit with her 2012 project, Middle of Nowhere, and she's arrived as a Hollywood filmmaker with direction of Paul Webb's script, which previously had been in development with Lee Butler. David Oyelowo had a supporting role in Nowhere and it is excellent to see him arrive simultaneously with DuVernay; both the director and her star have earned high praise for the intimate portrait of MLK that is framed against his history-making march to Montgomery, while being unafraid to show the man's human, less than heroic attributes as well.

132 min   -   Action | Biography | Thriller | War

Metascore: 72/100 (48 reviews)
Navy SEAL sniper Chris Kyle's pinpoint accuracy saves countless lives on the battlefield and turns him into a legend. Back home to his wife and kids after four tours of duty, however, Chris finds that it is the war he can't leave behind.
Director:
Clint Eastwood
Stars:
Bradley Cooper, Sienna Miller, Kyle Gallner, Cole Konis
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: From the advanced reviews, it sounds as though Clint Eastwood has directed one of the best movies of his career and completely abandoned his typical moral-forward approach to making war pictures in favor of a more quiet, meditative style reminiscent of Unforgiven.

Big Eyes (2014)

106 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 62/100 (40 reviews)
A drama about the awakening of the painter Margaret Keane, her phenomenal success in the 1950s, and the subsequent legal difficulties she had with her husband, who claimed credit for her works in the 1960s.
Director:
Tim Burton
Stars:
Amy Adams, Christoph Waltz, Danny Huston, Krysten Ritter
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: When Tim Burton went back to the Frankenweenie world, some of us wondered -- and hoped -- his career might experience a sea change and the masterful storyteller would look to more original and peculiar stories for his new projects. So here we are with Burton's latest, an attempt to correct a chapter of art history that saw Walter Keane take credit for his wife Margaret's influential paintings, and his first indie studio project since ... since ... forever?

Everyone wins here: Burton, weary Burton loyalists, leads Amy Adams and Christoph Waltz, the supporting cast led by Krysten Ritter, and Margaret Keane's legacy. Though perhaps her Wikipedia page says it all: "Margaret D. H. Keane ... is an American artist ..." while her former husband's reads: "Walter Stanley Keane ... was an American plagiarist who became famous in the 1950s for claiming he had painted a series of widely-reproduced paintings depicting vulnerable waifs with enormous eyes."

Cotillard is definitely in the dark-horse Oscar pool for Best Actress too. Is it leading to say that this is the kind of story that would never be made in the U.S.?
