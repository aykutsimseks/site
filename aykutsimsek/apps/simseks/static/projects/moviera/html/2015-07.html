Terminator Genisys (2015)

126 min   -   Action | Adventure | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 38/100 (41 reviews)
When John Connor, leader of the human resistance, sends Sgt. Kyle Reese back to 1984 to protect Sarah Connor and safeguard the future, an unexpected turn of events creates a fractured timeline.
Director:
Alan Taylor
Stars:
Arnold Schwarzenegger, Jason Clarke, Emilia Clarke, Jai Courtney
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Once we learned what Skynet has morphed into in this fifth movie in the Terminator franchise, we let go of the tentative optimism we had reserved for the return of Arnold Schwarzenegger in T-800 form. Gone is our excitement to see Emilia Clarke reunite with her one-time "Game of Thrones" director Alan Taylor. Baffled we are by the news that Jai Courtney gets more screentime than Jason Clarke. We are equally confused by the barrage of timelines, new robots, and apparent loose ends that exist to be threaded together future movies. Perhaps all we need to do is look at the TV show "Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles" and realize: if that quite-good program couldn't make it beyond two seasons, perhaps the franchise as we know it is done?

Magic Mike XXL (2015)

115 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Music

Metascore: 60/100 (41 reviews)
Three years after Mike bowed out of the stripper life at the top of his game, he and the remaining Kings of Tampa hit the road to Myrtle Beach to put on one last blow-out performance.
Director:
Gregory Jacobs
Stars:
Channing Tatum, Joe Manganiello, Matt Bomer, Adam Rodriguez
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: I know a few people who dig the first Magic Mike as a 'Steven Soderbergh movie about the economy, with more than one opportunity to see Channing Tatum's rump', but MMXXL is the sequel the majority of the movie-going public wants. It has undertones of Mike's blue-collar struggles while most of the other Knights of Tampa are afflicted with Peter Pan Syndrome (except for Matt Bomer - his character is quite namaste in an expanded role), but the story makes a few pit stops en route to Myrtle Beach in order to strip, thrust, gyrate, and even spend some time in a gay bar. And we counted three bisexual characters, too. While the finale is a spectacle to be sure, Mike's performance in the all-male pleasure palace run by Rome (Jada Pinkett Smith's character) is the most over-the-top set piece.

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: This well-timed documentary began its life soon after directors Antonio Santini and Dan Sickles and cinematographer Adam Uhl visited a drag club while they were at a film festival with Santini's first short.
