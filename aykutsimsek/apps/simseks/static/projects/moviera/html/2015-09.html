The Transporter Refueled (2015)

96 min   -   Action | Crime | Thriller

In the south of France, former special-ops mercenary Frank Martin enters into a game of chess with a femme-fatale and her three sidekicks who are looking for revenge against a sinister Russian kingpin.
Director:
Camille Delamarre
Stars:
Ed Skrein, Loan Chabanol, Ray Stevenson, Lenn Kudrjawizki
Watch Trailer Get Tickets

The Buzz: Hitman: Agent 47's abysmal box-office performance doesn't bode well for this action-movie reboot. At least Ed Skrein oozes a similar kind of sexuality as Jason Statham (who has moved on to bigger franchises, thank you very much) and the storyline's woman power might help bring in a new audience. Nice touch with Ray Stevenson playing Frank Sr., too.

A Walk in the Woods (2015)

104 min   -   Adventure | Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 51/100 (24 reviews)
After spending two decades in England, Bill Bryson returns to the U.S., where he decides the best way to connect with his homeland is to hike the Appalachian Trail with one of his oldest friends.
Director:
Ken Kwapis
Stars:
Robert Redford, Nick Nolte, Emma Thompson, Mary Steenburgen
Watch Trailer Get Tickets

The Buzz: This broad comedy feels like it could have been made any time between the mid-1980s and today. This sort of zero-surprises storytelling either really works for you or it doesn't. We've noticed that Little Miss Sunshine writer Michael Arndt helped with this screenplay ... but he used an alias, Rick Kerb, for his credit. We can't help but wonder what this movie might have been like if J.C. Chandor directed it.

Director:
Chris Evans
Stars:
Chris Evans, Alice Eve, Emma Fitzpatrick, Scott Evans
Watch Trailer

September 11 

The Visit (2015)

94 min   -   Comedy | Horror

A single mother finds that things in her family's life go very wrong after her two young children visit their grandparents.
Director:
M. Night Shyamalan
Stars:
Olivia DeJonge, Ed Oxenbould, Deanna Dunagan, Peter McRobbie
Watch Trailer Get Tickets

The Buzz: We left the theater smiling after a Comic-Con screening of M. Night Shyamalan's low-budget/high-return mystery. Or is it a campy thriller? Really, it's both. Shyamalan seemed to be enjoying the return to his filmmaking roots, and we have to say we think the story's twist is sublime. Furthermore, it makes real world sense, so we're excited to see how audiences respond to his mea culpa.

90 Minutes in Heaven (2015)

Drama

A man involved in a horrific car crash is pronounced dead, only to come back to life an hour and a half later, claiming to have seen Heaven.
Director:
Michael Polish
Stars:
Kate Bosworth, Hayden Christensen, Hudson Meek, Bobby Batson
Watch Trailer Get Tickets

The Buzz: Michael Polish taking them helm of a Faith-based movie makes sense to us since the world of Christian film production is one of the most successful non-Hollywood-based businesses, and Polish has made a stack of decidedly independent films with his brother, Mark. Gui lai (2014)

109 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 69/100 (5 reviews)
Lu and Feng are a devoted couple forced to separate when Lu is arrested and sent to a labor camp as a political prisoner during the Cultural Revolution. He finally returns home only to find that his beloved wife no longer remembers him.
Director:
Yimou Zhang
Stars:
Li Gong, Daoming Chen, Huiwen Zhang, Tao Guo
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Zhang Yimou and Gong Li's newest collaboration has been described as their most traditional picture yet. At Cannes, some critics meant this as a compliment; others politely dismissed it and moved on as the film screened outside of competition. Chinese audiences weren't particularly moved by the story either. Yimou wasted no time mounting his next project, The Great Wall, which stars a ponytailed Matt Damon and puts a sci-fi spin on the reason why the Wall went up.

Wolf Totem (2015)

121 min   -   Adventure

In 1967, a young Beijing student, Chen Zhen, is sent to live among the nomadic herdsmen of Inner Mongolia. Caught between the advance of civilization from the south and the nomads' traditional enemies - the marauding wolves - to the north; humans and animals, residents and invaders alike, struggle to find their true place in the world.
Director:
Jean-Jacques Annaud
Stars:
Shaofeng Feng, Shawn Dou, Ankhnyam Ragchaa, Yin Zhusheng
Watch Trailer Get Tickets

The Buzz: Jean-Jacques Annaud's new film, which is based on the semi-autobiographical novel by Lu Jiami, is expected to be China's submission for this year's foreign language Academy Award. Already certified a blockbuster in China earlier this year, it took seven years to make Wolf Totem. Part of the reason? The production required two packs of wolves to be trained for use in the film.

Sleeping with Other People (2015)

95 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 77/100 (4 reviews)
A good-natured womanizer and a serial cheater form a platonic relationship that helps reform them in ways, while a mutual attraction sets in.
Director:
Leslye Headland
Stars:
Jason Sudeikis, Alison Brie, Jordan Carlos, Margarita Levieva
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Bachelorette director Leslye Headland returns with another comedy that seems likely to polarize viewers with its crass, realistic humor. We certainly love these two leads; we'd also love to see Headland write a story that instantly seems original and set in uncharted interpersonal territory.

Time Out of Mind (2014)

120 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 73/100 (5 reviews)
George seeks refuge at Bellevue Hospital, an Manhattan intake center for homeless men, where his friendship with a fellow client helps him try to repair his relationship with his estranged daughter.
Director:
Oren Moverman
Stars:
Richard Gere, Ben Vereen, Jena Malone, Steve Buscemi
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Oren Moverman's body of work (Rampart and The Messenger are both his movies) has dealt with men in crisis, and Time Out of Mind represents his artiest approach to narrative filmmaking thus far. Richard Gere has received strong reviews for his performance, but Moverman's techniques have been criticized by some for getting in the way of telling an effective story. We tend to be attracted to movies that get mixed reviews; in truth, this one might have flown under our radar otherwise.
