Source Code (2011)

93 min   -   Mystery | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 74/100 (41 reviews)
A soldier wakes up in someone else's body and discovers he's part of an experimental government program to find the bomber of a commuter train. A mission he has only 8 minutes to complete.
Director:
Duncan Jones
Stars:
Jake Gyllenhaal, Michelle Monaghan, Vera Farmiga, Jeffrey Wright
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Duncan Jones keeps the action on Earth for his follow-up to his beloved, near-perfect debut, Moon (2009). Though he's partnered with a major studio and visions of Unstoppable (2010) flicker here and there, we sense the story itself will be as focused as Moon's, and perhaps a second exploration of a man working against a manipulated environment. Jake Gyl traveling through time again (a la Donnie Darko (2001)'s best bits), is definitely not a bad idea after his recent miscues, which haven't turned him into the leading man we were all expecting. Perhaps during one of the attempts to prevent this incident, Michelle Monaghan will appear as a giant rabbit.

Insidious (2010)

103 min   -   Drama | Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 52/100 (30 reviews)
A family looks to prevent evil spirits from trapping their comatose child in a realm called The Further.
Director:
James Wan
Stars:
Patrick Wilson, Rose Byrne, Ty Simpkins, Lin Shaye
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Yes, it's true, it's the newest non-Saw film from James Wan and Leigh Whannell, and the buzz from the picky, informed Toronto International Film Festival midnight-movie scene has been strong. Paranormal Activity director Oren Peli is one of the producers, and his influence can be felt in terms of the mood and atmosphere, but really everyone works hard here to pay homage to Poltergeist, Amityville Horror, and other faves. Barbara Hershey is back with another creepy performance, but we hear genre vet Lin Shaye owns the third act. This is the beginning of an interesting year for upstart distributor FilmDistrict, former Apparition co-chief Bob Berney's new company.

Meek's Cutoff (2010)

104 min   -   Drama | Western

Metascore: 85/100 (36 reviews)
Settlers traveling through the Oregon desert in 1845 find themselves stranded in harsh conditions.
Director:
Kelly Reichardt
Stars:
Michelle Williams, Bruce Greenwood, Paul Dano, Will Patton
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Anyone who loves Kelly Reichardt's works (River of Grass (1994), Old Joy (2006), and Wendy and Lucy (2008)) should (quietly) revel in the director's decision to make a period piece with her emerging signatures: a handmade aesthetic, long takes, longer periods of silence, and a love of open, unresolved endings. It's the kind of filmmaking that makes people give up halfway through, while others laugh or stare at one another blankly as the credits roll. Personally, it's the exact sort of film I crave and happily devour when it's in front of me. Michelle Williams ostensibly is the star, though since Meek's mostly is a man's world, her character emerges in the third act. Naturally, her performance is vital.

Ceremony (2010)

89 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 40/100 (13 reviews)
Two friends are off on a weekend outing. Marshall thinks the trip is to re-establish their friendship, but Sam has ulterior motives in mind as he crashes the wedding of a woman he still loves.
Director:
Max Winkler
Stars:
Michael Angarano, Uma Thurman, Reece Thompson, Lee Pace
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Max Winkler will forever be referred to as the son of The Fonz, but I think he'll emerge as a successful comedy filmmaker in time. He's directed bits of two very funny web series, Wainy Days (2007) and Clark and Michael (2006) and his bookish sense of humor informs the character Sam Davis, played by Michael Angarano in The Ceremony, who brings to mind the determined young men seen in the films of Wes Anderson. Sam also has a Sorkinesque gift for rapid-fire delivery. Personally, I wished for more chemistry between Angarano and Uma Thurman, but the story becomes more about two young men and their rites of passage, and, frankly, I lost interest in the narrative and its themes. I miss Uma Thurman: movie star.
