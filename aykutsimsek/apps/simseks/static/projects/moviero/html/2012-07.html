July 6 

The Amazing Spider-Man (2012)

136 min   -   Action | Adventure | Fantasy

Metascore: 66/100 (42 reviews)
After Peter Parker is bitten by a genetically altered spider, he gains newfound, spider-like powers and ventures out to solve the mystery of his parent's mysterious death.
Director:
Marc Webb
Stars:
Andrew Garfield, Emma Stone, Rhys Ifans, Irrfan Khan
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Thanks to a smart marketing campaign that lured in both men and women over the Fourth of July holiday, Sony and Marvel have proved it's possible to reboot a successful franchise after a five-year period. No doubt some credit is due to The Avengers (2012), thus far the best example of how a superhero flick can appeal to most anyone who goes to the movies. Still, I'm interested to see what happens in week two and beyond, and to what degree the Andrew Garfield, Emma Stone, and company ultimately perform overseas.

Savages (2012)

131 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 59/100 (41 reviews)
Pot growers Ben and Chon face off against the Mexican drug cartel who kidnapped their shared girlfriend.
Director:
Oliver Stone
Stars:
Aaron Taylor-Johnson, Taylor Kitsch, Blake Lively, Benicio Del Toro
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Oliver Stone reunites Uma Thurman and John Travolta in this fictional take on a Mexican drug cartel's agenda and the less reported but equally frightening realities of California's pot wars. Suddenly, I'm re-interested in Stone as a narrative filmmaker since these days he's most effective as a documentarian. Don Wilson's bestseller is the basis of the upcoming movie; a prequel novel, The Kings of Cool, will be published to coincide with the film's release.

Katy Perry: Part of Me (2012)

93 min   -   Documentary | Music

Metascore: 57/100 (24 reviews)
The life, career and music of singer Katy Perry as we follow her on the California Dreams tour.
Directors:
Dan Cutforth | Jane Lipsitz
Stars:
Katy Perry, Adam Marcello, Casey Hooper, Patrick Matera
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: What a friend said about Justin Bieber: Never Say Never (2011) applies here: This is "50% concert film and 50% hagiographic promotional video and 0% documentary." As orchestrated as Bieber's movie was, his innocence shone through, whereas Ms. Perry's active role in the shaping of her celebrity story makes us feel as though there's no chance of swaying the opinions of non-believers. Never Say Never converted me, if only for a short while, but it was effective nonetheless. Young ladies, consider checking out what Gwen Stacy has to say in The Amazing Spider-Man (2012) this weekend instead.

China Heavyweight (2012)

94 min   -   Documentary | Sport

Metascore: 77/100 (11 reviews)
A documentary on rural teenagers in southwestern China who are recruited as their country's next Olympic hopefuls, with a focus on the coach Qi Moxiang.
Director:
Yung Chang
Stars:
Moxiang Qi, Yunfei Miao, Zongli He, Ye Xinchun
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Documentarian Yung Chang's follows up the excellent Up the Yangtze (2007) with a layered sports documentary with roots to Chairman Mao's founding of the People's Republic of China.

Collaborator (2011)

87 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 56/100 (9 reviews)
A playwright whose marriage and career are in a free fall has an explosive run-in with his former neighbor, a right-wing ex-con.
Director:
Martin Donovan
Stars:
Martin Donovan, David Morse, Olivia Williams, Melissa Auf der Maur
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Independent film icon Martin Donovan might forever be known in small circles as Hal Hartley's leading man, but here he makes his debut as a writer/director in his own right, with an appealing cast that includes a first acting role by former Hole bassist and thoroughly awesome lady, Melissa Auf der Maur. Donovan, also quite memorable from his run on "Weeds," was able to connect with co-star David Morse through Mary-Louise Parker.

The Do-Deca-Pentathlon (2012)

76 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 60/100 (15 reviews)
Two brothers compete in their own private 25-event Olympics.
Directors:
Jay Duplass | Mark Duplass
Stars:
Mark Kelly, Steve Zissis, Jennifer Lafleur, Julie Vorus
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If you're thinking this looks like old-school Duplass Brothers, you're right on. Filmed back in 2008, the competitive-brother comedy had to be set aside while Jay and Mark made Cyrus (2010), and that film's success resulted in a quick green light for Jeff, Who Lives at Home (2011). Mark Duplass, who is making strides into the mainstream, calls the movie an unofficial cap to the trilogy of The Puffy Chair (2005) and Baghead (2008), their low-fi productions that, for better or otherwise, trafficked in the Mumblecore movement -- which is still going strong in the world of film festivals and self-distribution.

The Magic of Belle Isle (2012)

109 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 46/100 (14 reviews)
Monty Wildhorn, an alcoholic novelist of Westerns, has lost his drive. His nephew pushes him to summer in quiet Belle Isle. He begrudgingly befriends a newly single mom and her 3 girls who help him find the inspiration to write again.
Director:
Rob Reiner
Stars:
Morgan Freeman, Virginia Madsen, Madeline Carroll, Emma Fuhrmann
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Word has it this redemptive dramedy is bolstered by Morgan Freeman's performance, , but director Rob Reiner adds little to the proceedings -- which may be why he's taking a right turn into thriller mode for his recently announced next project.

Crazy Eyes (2012)

95 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 31/100 (12 reviews)
Zach is guy for whom the party never ends. But when he meets the girl he nicknames "Crazy Eyes," the inability to have her, combined with family matters, are signs that his idle life might be due for a change.
Director:
Adam Sherman
Stars:
Lukas Haas, Madeline Zima, Jake Busey, Tania Raymonde
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Adam Sherman is an indie filmmaker akin to memoirist Augusten Burroughs; both writers recreate their darker personal experiences for the masses - though Sherman, who here follows up his under-achieving commune drama Happiness Runs (2010), hasn't unlocked a formula for mainstream success yet. Seems like a tough box-office environment in which to promote the sobering-up of a scruffy Los Angeles playboy, even with the mostly interesting Lukas Haas in the lead.

The Pact (2012)

89 min   -   Horror | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 54/100 (10 reviews)
As a woman struggles to come to grips with her past in the wake of her mother's death and the disappearance of her sister, an unsettling presence emerges in her childhood home.
Director:
Nicholas McCarthy
Stars:
Caity Lotz, Casper Van Dien, Mark Steger, Agnes Bruckner
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Most reviews of Nicholas McCarthy's haunted-house thriller say the exact same things: McCarthy does little to augment or re-invent the well-worn sub-genre, but the cinematography makes the film look like a major-studio production.

88 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family

Metascore: 49/100 (29 reviews)

Directors:
Steve Martino | Mike Thurmeier
Stars:
Ray Romano, Denis Leary, John Leguizamo, Aziz Ansari
Watch Trailer

And Aziz Ansari, whose character will probably be annoying.

Snabba cash (2010)

124 min   -   Action | Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 75/100 (27 reviews)
When JW becomes a drug runner in order to maintain his double life, his fate becomes tied to two other men: Jorge, a fugitive on the run from both the Serbian mafia and the police, and mafia enforcer Mrado, who is on the hunt for Jorge.
Director:
Daniel Espinosa
Stars:
Joel Kinnaman, Matias Varela, Dragomir Mrsic, Lisa Henni
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Finally we get to enter the criminal underworld that launched the careers of star Joel Kinnaman and director Daniel Espinosa. And know (or remember) that Zac Efron is looking to diversify his celebrity by starring as the young drug runner in the proposed remake. European audiences will get a look at the sequel late this coming summer.

Red Lights (2012)

114 min   -   Drama | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 36/100 (22 reviews)
Psychologist Margaret Matheson and her assistant study paranormal activity, which leads them to investigate a world-renowned psychic who has resurfaced years after his toughest critic mysteriously passed away.
Director:
Rodrigo Cortés
Stars:
Sigourney Weaver, Robert De Niro, Cillian Murphy, Elizabeth Olsen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: If you feel as though the term "paranormal" has been tainted, the thought of the director of Buried (2010) taking a less-expected approach to the genre might be worth your interest. Reportedly more of a cat-and-mouse thriller between Sigourney Weaver a Robery De Niro, it could be older audiences who gravitate to the mysteries herein. Meanwhile, it's good to see that Elizabeth Olsen has turned the initial Sundance hype into an ever-diversifying career.

The Imposter (2012)

99 min   -   Documentary | Biography | Thriller

Metascore: 77/100 (27 reviews)
A documentary centered on a young man in Spain who claims to a grieving Texas family that he is their 16-year-old son who has been missing for 3 years.
Director:
Bart Layton
Stars:
Adam O'Brian, Anna Ruben, Cathy Dresbach, Alan Teichman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Frédéric Bourdin's incredible real-life exploits were fictionalized in the Canadian film The Chameleon (2010), but Bart Layton's docudrama (Bourdin consulted on the projected by is portrayed by an actor who bares an eerie resemblance) focuses on the fateful set of circumstances that took him from a French children's home to San Antonio, Texas, where he was accepted by a family as their son, Nicholas, who went missing as a boy. Without revealing much more, it can be safe that Bourdin isn't the only member of this family who is misrepresenting themselves.

Alpeis (2011)

93 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 69/100 (16 reviews)
A group of people start a business where they impersonate the recently deceased in order to help their clients through the grieving process.
Director:
Yorgos Lanthimos
Stars:
Stavros Psyllakis, Aris Servetalis, Johnny Vekris, Ariane Labed
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Might we see some sentimentality from Giorgos Lanthimos in his follow-up to the Oscar-nominated Kynodontas (2009)? The subject matter, another unique narrative from the writer/director, hints at an opportunity for more humor than we've seen from Lanthimos thus far; it was present in Dogtooth, but folded into a thoroughly unnerving family psychodrama that kept me off balance throughout.

Trishna (2011)

117 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 57/100 (22 reviews)
The story of the tragic relationship between the son of a property developer and the daughter of an auto rickshaw owner.
Director:
Michael Winterbottom
Stars:
Freida Pinto, Riz Ahmed, Mita Vasisht, Harish Khanna
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Someone once said the best thing about seeing a bad Michael Winterbottom film is the fact that you don't have to wait long for him to release another effort. And to be fair, Winterbottom is usually more On than otherwise. But his interpretation of the novel Tess of the d'Urbervilles is thick through the middle and comes to an unsatisfying melodramatic end. But, hey, already in a short career, Freida Pinto has worked with some of the best established and emerging directors out there.

The Dark Knight Rises (2012)

165 min   -   Action | Thriller

Metascore: 78/100 (45 reviews)
Eight years after the Joker's reign of anarchy, the Dark Knight is forced to return from his imposed exile to save Gotham City from the brutal guerrilla terrorist Bane with the help of the enigmatic Catwoman.
Director:
Christopher Nolan
Stars:
Christian Bale, Tom Hardy, Anne Hathaway, Gary Oldman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Bane, one of the most empathy earning villains in comic book lore, is the ideal final opponent for Christopher Nolan's Batman trilogy; Tom Hardy's portrayal of the Venom-addicted backbreaker should rank up there with The Joker, Hannibal Lecter, Anton Chigurh ... Comics types should be able to piece together the main parts of the narrative given the connection between Bane and Ra's Al Ghul, though how Catwoman fits in remains a mystery.

The Queen of Versailles (2012)

100 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 80/100 (35 reviews)
A documentary that follows a billionaire couple as they begin construction on a mansion inspired by Versailles. During the next two years, their empire, fueled by the real estate bubble and cheap money, falters due to the economic crisis.
Director:
Lauren Greenfield
Stars:
Lorraine Barrett, June Downs, Phillip Froehlich, Marissa Gaspay
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Controversial even before its Sundance debut (subject David Siegel sued the festival for their use of the phrase "rags to riches to rags" to describe their fortune losses), Lauren Greenfield's documentary captivated the Park City crowd and took home the directing award in its category. Magnolia Pictures has had a few major documentary successes -- Food, Inc. (2008), Gonzo: The Life and Work of Dr. Hunter S. Thompson (2008), and most recently Jiro Dreams of Sushi (2011) -- so this insider look at the 1% should have no trouble reaching its audiences and stirring their respective pots. But the true reason Greenfield has earned accolades for her work here is for her humanism.

Shut Up and Play the Hits (2012)

108 min   -   Documentary | Music

Metascore: 72/100 (8 reviews)
On April 2nd 2011, LCD Soundsystem played its final show at Madison Square Garden. Documenting this once in a life time performance and an intimate portrait of James Murphy as he navigates the lead-up to the show, the day after, and the personal and professional ramifications of his decision.
Directors:
Will Lovelace | Dylan Southern
Stars:
Chuck Klosterman, James Murphy, Keith Wood, Nancy Whang
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: LCD Soundsystem leader James Murphy has always come across like a fantastic man on record and in interviews, so imagine how crush-worthy he is as he reflects on his music career to date as LCDSS's final show approaches. Exuding the kind of cool that comes from humble self-consciousness, Murphy's answers to writer Chuck Klosterman`s guiding questions touch on his love of art and community as he illuminates some of the feelings behind his decision to end LCD at a creative zenith. The footage from the Madison Square Garden show is emotional, and you will probably tear up for the 1,000th time during a live rendition of "All My Friends".

La fille du puisatier (2011)

107 min   -   Drama | Romance

In 1930s southern France, a father is torn between his sense of honor and his deep love for his daughter when she gets in trouble with the wealthy son of a shopkeeper.
Director:
Daniel Auteuil
Stars:
Daniel Auteuil, Astrid Bergès-Frisbey, Nicolas Duvauchelle, Jean-Pierre Darroussin

The Buzz: Cuddly French actor Daniel Auteuil makes his directorial debut with an apparently sexless and airy drama that might be a letdown for people who are accustomed to his performances in movies such as La séparation (1994), Caché (Hidden) (2005), or Un Coeur en Hiver (1992).

The Watch (2012)

102 min   -   Action | Comedy | Sci-Fi

Metascore: 36/100 (35 reviews)
Four men who form a neighborhood watch group as a way to get out of their day-to-day family routines find themselves defending the Earth from an alien invasion.
Director:
Akiva Schaffer
Stars:
Ben Stiller, Vince Vaughn, Jonah Hill, Billy Crudup
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Sight unseen, this emits a vibe that's one part Adam Sandler buddy comedy, one part Paul (2011). Or maybe E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982) starring middle-aged dudes. The latest ensemble comedy from Ben Stiller and second directorial outing for Lonely Island's Akiva Schaffer hit a PR hurdle after the killing of Trayvon Martin; the studio and filmmakers responded gracefully by adjusting the movie's title and its marketing, which was a bit off in the beginning anyway. Perhaps the aliens vs. neighborhood guys comedy will be crushed by Batman in his second weekend, or it could be the best inadvertent counterprogramming in years.

Step Up Revolution (2012)

99 min   -   Drama | Music | Romance

Metascore: 43/100 (22 reviews)
Emily arrives in Miami with aspirations to become a professional dancer. She sparks with Sean, the leader of a dance crew whose neighborhood is threatened by Emily's father's development plans.
Director:
Scott Speer
Stars:
Kathryn McCormick, Ryan Guzman, Cleopatra Coleman, Misha Gabriel Hamilton
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: As long as overseas audiences keep buying tickets and there are former "So You Think You Can Dance" contestants to poach, we will always be treated to the Step Up movies, which fetishize poverty and feature undeniably rad choreography. The franchise that launched Channing Tatum's career moves over to Summit Entertainment (home of Twilight) from Disney in its fourth outing.

Ruby Sparks (2012)

104 min   -   Comedy | Drama | Fantasy | Romance

Metascore: 67/100 (40 reviews)
A novelist struggling with writer's block finds romance in a most unusual way: by creating a female character he thinks will love him, then willing her into existence.
Directors:
Jonathan Dayton | Valerie Faris
Stars:
Paul Dano, Zoe Kazan, Annette Bening, Antonio Banderas
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We've seen so many indie-minded filmmaking duos come and go, I was getting antsy about the absence of Little Miss Sunshine (2006) directors Valerie Faris and Jonathan Dayton; not to be rude, but look what happened to Jared + Jerusha Hess after Napoleon Dynamite (2004), or Robert Pulcini + Shari Springer Berman after American Splendor (2003). The other thing worth noting here is the presence of Zoe Kazan in front of the camera in the titular role, and behind the scenes as the screenwriter of this romantic comedy that sounds like a classic Fox Searchlight formula, and I mean that in a good way. And lest we forget that Kazan and Paul Dano have been hitched before, albeit in a tenuous way in Meek's Cutoff (2010).

Killer Joe (2011)

102 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 62/100 (38 reviews)
When a debt puts a young man's life in danger, he turns to putting a hit out on his evil mother in order to collect the insurance.
Director:
William Friedkin
Stars:
Matthew McConaughey, Emile Hirsch, Juno Temple, Thomas Haden Church
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Matthew McConaughey, suddenly one of every critic's favorite actors, has earned serious raves for his work in the latest film from William Friedkin, a reportedly bleak and hyperviolent thriller that has earned an NC-17 rating (apparently the end sequence is extreme). Unless the Aurora, Colorado event serves as a deterrent, we expect the film will open to huge numbers in an limited release -- a la Bernie (2011), McConaughey's recent collaboration with Richard Linklater -- with word-of-mouth and talk of award nominations keeping it in theaters for most of the summer. After several recent indie attempts, Juno Temple, referred to as a "retainer" in the trailer, might finally have a hit on her hands.

Searching for Sugar Man (2012)

86 min   -   Documentary | Biography | Music

Metascore: 79/100 (32 reviews)
Two South Africans set out to discover what happened to their unlikely musical hero, the mysterious 1970s rock 'n' roller, Rodriguez.
Director:
Malik Bendjelloul
Stars:
Rodriguez, Stephen 'Sugar' Segerman, Dennis Coffey, Mike Theodore
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: One great thing about the LCD Soundsystem documentary Shut Up and Play the Hits (2012) is the presence of essayist and all-culture champion Chuck Klosterman, who gently guides frontman James Murphy through a series of memories and experiences as his next step in life remains unknown. If director Malik Bendjelloul had maintained the same level of intimacy and respect for his childhood musical hero, Sixto Rodriguez, who disappeared from the singer-songwriter scene after releasing a few critically acclaimed records, his documentary would be the equivalent of a platinum record. As it stands, with its invasive approach to an otherwise captivating story of a musician's life and a man's choices, it's gold-certified by us.

Unfamiliar with his saga? Here's a good background article.

Klown (2010)

93 min   -   Comedy

Indie critics have responded enthusiastically to the movie's deadpan comedy and episodic structure, but we'll see if it connects with general audiences.
