October 4 

Gravity (2013)

91 min   -   Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 96/100 (49 reviews)
A medical engineer and an astronaut work together to survive after a catastrophe destroys their shuttle and leaves them adrift in orbit.
Director:
Alfonso Cuarón
Stars:
Sandra Bullock, George Clooney, Ed Harris, Orto Ignatiussen
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: A who's who of actresses were attached or considered for this leading role. Angelina Jolie was set to star -- on two separate occasions -- and then it was Natalie Portman's next big project in the post-Black Swan (2010)/pre-pregnancy days. Ultimately, it will serve as Sandra Bullock's first science fiction role, and her fanbase should swell even more under the direction of treasured director Alfonso Cuarón, who somehow hasn't directed a project since the absolutely perfect Children of Men (2006). It's been widely noted that Clooney's screen time will be minimal, but pivotal (clearly).

Runner Runner (2013)

91 min   -   Crime | Thriller

Metascore: 36/100 (31 reviews)
When a poor college student who cracks an online poker game goes bust, he arranges a face-to-face with the man he thinks cheated him, a sly offshore entrepreneur.
Director:
Brad Furman
Stars:
Ben Affleck, Justin Timberlake, Gemma Arterton, Anthony Mackie
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ben Affleck has entrusted his acting-but-not-directing career to the right people: first up was Terrence Malick in To the Wonder (2012), and now he stars in Brad Furman's follow-up to sleeper hit The Lincoln Lawyer (2011). Could also be a boon for Justin Timberlake's acting career, which has hit the skids somewhat.

Pulling Strings (2013)

111 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 57/100 (7 reviews)
After a night of drinking, Rachel, a diplomat working in Mexico City finds her world turned upside down after she's saved by Alejandro, a Mariachi singer whose visa was rejected the day before - by Rachel.
Director:
Pedro Pablo Ibarra
Stars:
Laura Ramsey, Jaime Camil, Omar Chaparro, Stockard Channing
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Right now, the movie industry's eyes are on Pantelion Films, the Latino-focused studio that has a distribution deal with Lionsgate and whose recent release, Instructions Not Included (2013), has become one of the most successful foreign-language hits ever. We're interested to see how this crossover romantic comedy performs in comparison. Co-star Laura Ramsey will also be seen in Are You Here (2013), "Mad Men" creator Matthew Weiner's feature-film debut.

Besharam (2013)

143 min   -   Comedy | Romance

A car thief falls in love with a woman whose car he has stolen.
Director:
Abhinav Kashyap
Stars:
Ranbir Kapoor, Pallavi Sharda, Rishi Kapoor, Neetu Singh
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Filmmaker Abhinav Kashyap's follow-up to Dabangg (2010) sets a new record as the widest opening ever for a Bollywood film in North America. It's been a big international year for star Ranbir Kapoor, who just had a hit with Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani (2013).

Grace Unplugged (2013)

102 min   -   Drama | Music

Metascore: 39/100 (5 reviews)
Grace Trey is the ideal Christian teen who is also a phenomenal singer. But at the tender age of eighteen, after she gets the music break of a lifetime and is thrust into the "real world" - her faith is put to the test.
Director:
Brad J. Silverman
Stars:
AJ Michalka, Kelly Thiebaud, James Denton, Kevin Pollak
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Director Brad Silverman's coming-of-age story could be a strong performer in limited release thanks to star AJ Michalka's legion of fans, the Faith-based audience at large, and a heavy TV-focused ad campaign. We're still wondering if her planned TV project with sister Aly -- which is backed by Adam Sandler's production company -- will ever be made.

A.C.O.D. (2013)

88 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 50/100 (23 reviews)
A grown man caught in the crossfire of his parents' 15-year divorce discovers he was unknowingly part of a study on divorced children and is enlisted in a follow-up years later, which wreaks new havoc on his family.
Director:
Stu Zicherman
Stars:
Adam Scott, Richard Jenkins, Catherine O'Hara, Jane Lynch
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It would probably work the best to watch this talky-therapy comedy in the same manner as you enjoy Adam Scott on "Parks and Recreation"; from the comfort of your home, you can watch Scott and Amy Poehler repel one another, or put yourself in his shoes as he makes out with a tattooed Jessica Alba. There are many ways to distract yourself from the notion that the script just isn't that good.

Concussion (2013)

96 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 56/100 (19 reviews)
After a blow to the head, Abby decides she can't do it anymore. Her life just can't be only about the house, the kids and the wife. She needs more: she needs to be Eleanor.
Director:
Stacie Passon
Stars:
Robin Weigert, Maggie Siff, Johnathan Tchaikovsky, Ben Shenkman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Writer/director Stacie Passon was mentored here by Rose Troche of "L Word"/Go Fish (1994) indie fame, and their collaboration earned creative and technical accolades on the festival circuit for its frank depiction of a queer married woman's sexuality that satisfies its audiences desire for thoughtful and honest emotions, and super-hot sex scenes.

Parkland (2013)

93 min   -   Drama | History | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 51/100 (35 reviews)

Director:
Peter Landesman
Stars:
Zac Efron, Tom Welling, Billy Bob Thornton, Paul Giamatti
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Former New York Times reporter Peter Landesman has a handful of film projects in the works, and his docudrama on JFK's assassination wasn't immediately welcomed (at Venice) though it has been embraced (and reworked, we think) a bit more closely in North America, as the critics were much less harsh at the Toronto International Film Festival recently. We hear the opening scenes, as Kennedy is brought into the Parkland hospital, are a bit tough to watch.

Linsanity (2013)

89 min   -   Documentary | Biography | Sport

Metascore: 52/100 (13 reviews)
The life story of basketball sensation, Jeremy Lin.
Director:
Evan Leong
Stars:
Jeremy Lin, Daniel Dae Kim, Ming Yao, Landry Fields
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The open secret to the Sundance's success is in the festival's documentary programming, which is absolutely more reliable than its annual feature-film rosters. Director Evan Leong's work here has been praised for its heart (no surprise) and its capturing of a phenomenon as it is happening. Similar to the wonderful The Other Dream Team (2012), you need not be a sports enthusiast to be hooked in.

All Is Bright (2013)

107 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 54/100 (15 reviews)
While out on parole, Dennis reluctantly takes a job selling Christmas trees with his old buddy Rene in order to make enough money to buy his estranged daughter the piano she's always wanted.
Director:
Phil Morrison
Stars:
Paul Giamatti, Paul Rudd, Sally Hawkins, Amy Landecker
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Phil Morrison has a compact, impressive resume; aside from his breakout directorial debut, Junebug (2005), he's produced two projects for Kelly Reichardt, worked on the canceled-too-soon series Enlightened (2011), and directed some of the Upright Citizens Brigade's TV show. Here he's working with a script by one of the writers from The Americans (2013), so there's some definite promise for a nuanced take on the holiday comedy. In the trailer, however, neither Paul (Rudd or Giamatti) makes us laugh one bit. (Better is Sally Hawkins's Russian accent.)

Director:
Jason Osder
Stars:
Birdie Africa, Ramona Africa, Wilson Goode, William Brown III
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: New York-based indie Zeitgeist Films knows how to promote a political documentary, and this look at an under-reported event from the Reagan Era seems to be registering with people on Facebook and elsewhere.

The Summit (2012)

Bad Milo (2013)

Director:
Jacob Vaughan
Stars:

Watch Trailer

And now he's created what we hope is a loving, potent, and totally ill tribute to filmmakers such as Frank Henenlotter (Basket Case (1982)/Brain Damage (1988)).

Director:
Paul Greengrass
Stars:
Tom Hanks, Barkhad Abdi, Barkhad Abdirahman, Catherine Keener
Watch Trailer

The political thriller should also serve as a return to form for director Paul Greengrass. Catherine Kenner takes her second sidelined role of the season.

Director:

Stars:

Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Robert Rodriguez has long been a micro-budget filmmaker, so no matter what happens to the theatrical movie-going experience over the next few years, chances are Rodriguez will always be a step ahead, cranking out small-scale cult hits such as this second installment of the planned Machete trilogy. This time around, it's a chance for several former A-listers to rehabilitate their public perception - no matter which side of the law they're on in relation to Danny Trejo's ex-Federale.

Escape from Tomorrow (2013)

Watch Trailer

It will be interesting to see how general audiences react to the release, where Moore won't be present for any Q&A. But this is from the distributor who has released Exit Through the Gift Shop (2010) and Senna (2010), so we trust their taste implicitly.

The Inevitable Defeat of Mister & Pete (2013)

All the Boys Love Mandy Lane (2006)

Next up: Bottle Shock (2008) director Randall Miller's latest film casts some of the tamest performers around to recreate an era when Lower Manhattan didn't look like a giant outdoor shopping mall.

Ah, right, it must be the fact that star Kelen Coleman's profile has been raised thanks to "The Mindy Project" and "The Newsroom". Also, there's an Oscar winner amongst the cast ...

Carrie (2013)

100 min   -   Drama | Horror

Metascore: 53/100 (34 reviews)
A reimagining of the classic horror tale about Carrie White, a shy girl outcast by her peers and sheltered by her deeply religious mother, who unleashes telekinetic terror on her small town after being pushed too far at her senior prom.
Director:
Kimberly Peirce
Stars:
Chloë Grace Moretz, Julianne Moore, Gabriella Wilde, Portia Doubleday
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's unsurprising to see Brian De Palma's creation applied to modern times, the age of heightened awareness of peer-to-peer bullying. I'll spare you any commentary on the remake trend and say: at least the casting choices are ace. Though it will be completely different in tone, I will use Chlöe Moretz's performance as Carolyn Stoddard in Dark Shadows (2012) as the litmus test for her stepping into a character immortalized by Sissy Spacek.

The Fifth Estate (2013)

128 min   -   Biography | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 49/100 (42 reviews)
A dramatic thriller based on real events that reveals the quest to expose the deceptions and corruptions of power that turned an Internet upstart into the 21st century's most fiercely debated organization.
Director:
Bill Condon
Stars:
Benedict Cumberbatch, Daniel Brühl, Carice van Houten, Alicia Vikander
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: The first Internet-maverick profile since The Social Network worked hard to create a a Zero Dark Thirty-level controversy/conversation with its marketing campaign. Julian Assange himself denounced an early version of the script, saying its structure was built on a series of lies - though it's hard to know which screenplay he saw, as DreamWorks used two different books as the basis of the filmed story. Don't expect his voice to be silent at any stage unless he wants it to be. In lighter news, director Bill Condon might find himself back in Oscar contention for the first time since his Chicago screenplay, and we will take our Benedict Cumberbatch starring roles in any form.

12 Years a Slave (2013)

Director:
Steve McQueen
Stars:
Chiwetel Ejiofor, Michael Kenneth Williams, Michael Fassbender, Brad Pitt
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: While it won't be an easy watch, Chiwetel Ejiofor finally gets a leading role in a project that should artfully exhibit his range as an actor. Steve McQueen reunites with Fox Searchlight, who distributed his last work, Shame (2011), for another brave outing from the writer/director. We're interested to see how McQueen works on a larger scale given the depth of this cast and the period setting.
