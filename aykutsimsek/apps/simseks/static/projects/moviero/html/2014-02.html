February 7 

The Lego Movie (2014)

100 min   -   Animation | Adventure | Comedy | Family | Fantasy

Metascore: 83/100 (43 reviews)
An ordinary Lego construction worker, thought to be the prophesied 'Special', is recruited to join a quest to stop an evil tyrant from gluing the Lego universe into eternal stasis.
Directors:
Phil Lord | Christopher Miller
Stars:
Chris Pratt, Will Ferrell, Elizabeth Banks, Will Arnett
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Perhaps one lesson learned from Battleship (2012): audiences don't necessarily want classic board games and toys turned into overly serious action-adventure tales. So we were relieved when Warner Bros. announced the project as an animated movie to be co-directed by the guys from Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs (2009). That said: Candyland really would make for a freaky Gothic horror story.

The Monuments Men (2014)

118 min   -   Drama | War

Metascore: 52/100 (43 reviews)
An unlikely World War II platoon is tasked to rescue art masterpieces from Nazi thieves and return them to their owners.
Director:
George Clooney
Stars:
George Clooney, Matt Damon, Bill Murray, Cate Blanchett
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: George Clooney's latest directorial effort has been called "this year's Argo" for its true-life political thrills and ensemble cast, and we also think it will play like a realer-world Avengers -- just replace superheroes with Oscar winners, nominees, etc. Savvy/ingenious casting should guarantee solid box-office performance on both sides of the Atlantic despite being pushed onto the 2014 calendar from its initial awards berth.

Vampire Academy (2014)

104 min   -   Action | Comedy | Fantasy | Horror | Mystery

Metascore: 30/100 (14 reviews)
Rose Hathaway is a Dhampir, half human-half vampire, a guardian of the Moroi, peaceful, mortal vampires living discreetly within our world. Her calling is to protect the Moroi from bloodthirsty, immortal Vampires, the Strigoi.
Director:
Mark Waters
Stars:
Zoey Deutch, Lucy Fry, Danila Kozlovsky, Gabriel Byrne
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Mark Waters hasn't had the best luck since Mean Girls, but the director's underrated take on The Spiderwick Chronicles proved he can handle the fantasy genre. Here, he partners with his brother, Heathers (1988) screenwriter Daniel Waters, to mount the first installment of a hopeful franchise based on Richelle Mead's 6-volume series of best-selling novels that sound a bit like Twilight meets X-Men meets, well, Mean Girls. A potential advantage this contender has over its competition: two of the six books have been turned into graphic novels thus far.

A Field in England (2013)

90 min   -   Drama | Horror

Metascore: 73/100 (19 reviews)
Amid the Civil War in 17th-century England, a group of deserters flee from battle through an overgrown field. Captured by an alchemist, the men are forced to help him search to find a hidden treasure that he believes is buried in the field.
Director:
Ben Wheatley
Stars:
Julian Barratt, Peter Ferdinando, Richard Glover, Ryan Pope
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: In the U.S., Ben Wheatley is still a film-critic's director since his previous works -- Sightseers, Kill List and Down Terrace -- have faired quite well on the festival circuit but barely made a dent in theaters (though we hope and imagine his at-home audience has grown). His latest, an experimental period piece, has earned some of his strongest reviews to date and the backing of one of our favorite studios, Drafthouse Films.

A Fantastic Fear of Everything (2012)

100 min   -   Comedy | Horror

Metascore: 31/100 (14 reviews)
A crime novelist whose research on Victorian serial killers has turned him into a paranoid wreck must confront his worst fears when a film executive takes a sudden interest in his movie script.
Directors:
Crispian Mills | Chris Hopewell
Stars:
Simon Pegg, Paul Freeman, Amara Karan, Clare Higgins
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We haven't seen writer-director Crispian Mill's feature film debut, but plenty of reviewers have questioned whether a horror comedy with Victorian tones will resonate with U.S. audiences, to which I say: Since people stuck with "Downton Abbey" through its most recent season, genre audiences might just take to this Simon Pegg-starring curio.

The Pretty One (2013)

90 min   -   Comedy | Drama

Metascore: 53/100 (14 reviews)
A tragedy presents Laurel with the chance to reinvent herself as her idolized twin sister, Audrey. As she eases into the life she has always wanted, she must decide between continuing the lie or revealing herself as the perfect fraud.
Director:
Jenée LaMarque
Stars:
Zoe Kazan, Jake Johnson, Ron Livingston, John Carroll Lynch
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Definitely interested in what's next for writer-director Jenee LaMarque since critics have come out in support of her narrative, which moves the often-creepy take on identical twins into romantic comedy territory, and supplies yet another left-of-center role for Zoe Kazan, one of the more underrated actresses around.

February 14 

About Last Night (2014)

100 min   -   Comedy | Romance

Metascore: 62/100 (30 reviews)
Follow two couples as they journey from the bar to the bedroom and are eventually put to the test in the real world.
Director:
Steve Pink
Stars:
Kevin Hart, Michael Ealy, Regina Hall, Joy Bryant
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Scrappy director Steve Pink is aboard this contemporary version of the 1986 romantic comedy, and I'm guessing the update with have a lot to say about trying to build a relationship in an era where privacy is hard to come by. Bachelorette filmmaker Leslye Headland handled the screenplay, so the scenarios and dialogue could get filthy -- perhaps an open field for Kevin Hart to let loose like he does on stage.

RoboCop (2014)

117 min   -   Action | Sci-Fi | Thriller

Metascore: 52/100 (41 reviews)
In 2028 Detroit, when Alex Murphy - a loving husband, father and good cop - is critically injured in the line of duty, the multinational conglomerate OmniCorp sees their chance for a part-man, part-robot police officer.
Director:
José Padilha
Stars:
Joel Kinnaman, Gary Oldman, Michael Keaton, Abbie Cornish
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: For his English-language debut, Elite Squad director Jose Padilha has the dubious honor of handling this long-in-development remake that had trouble holding onto its stars before filming began. Even with this enviable cast, in the post-Total Recall, post-Dredd era, the studio and most everyone involved with this project must be a bit nervous. Trying to reboot a classic sci-fi film is as difficult as it is to launch the next Twilight. And we love that the movie has garnered good reviews, but the gap between critical consensus and the general movie-going audience has never been wider.

Winter's Tale (2014)

118 min   -   Drama | Fantasy | Mystery | Romance

Metascore: 31/100 (35 reviews)
A burglar falls for an heiress as she dies in his arms. When he learns that he has the gift of reincarnation, he sets out to save her.
Director:
Akiva Goldsman
Stars:
Colin Farrell, Jessica Brown Findlay, Russell Crowe, Jennifer Connelly
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's easy to see why novelist Mark Helprin's New York City fantasy would appeal to screenwriter Akiva Goldsman for his directorial debut, but we have so many questions about this movie's box-office chances. Colin Farrell: we love him as an actor and would love to drink with him as a friend, but audiences don't seem interested in him as a leading man. And the fantasy genre hasn't served many studios that well, unless they're working with an already established franchise. Meanwhile, it's interesting to see Will Smith in a barely there role as he's in the news this week for opting out of the Independence Day sequel.

Endless Love (2014)

104 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 30/100 (32 reviews)
The story of a privileged girl and a charismatic boy whose instant desire sparks a love affair made only more reckless by parents trying to keep them apart.
Director:
Shana Feste
Stars:
Gabriella Wilde, Alex Pettyfer, Bruce Greenwood, Robert Patrick
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We crave an obsessive-love thriller now and then and hope the script by "Gossip Girl" vet Joshua Safran overflows with bad behavior from Alex Pettyfer, who just might have found a starring role worthy of his Hollywood reputation. Shana Feste (Country Strong) directs.

Adult World (2013)

97 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 61/100 (19 reviews)
Amy, a naive college graduate who believes she's destined to be a great poet, begrudgingly accepts a job at a sex shop while she pursues a mentorship with reclusive writer Rat Billings.
Director:
Scott Coffey
Stars:
Emma Roberts, Evan Peters, John Cusack, Cloris Leachman
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Fresh from the success of "American Horror Story"'s recent season, Emma Roberts is on-screen with her real-life lover Evan Peters for actor/occasional director Scott Coffey. The trailer gives off a "Girls"/Ghost World vibe -- if Hannah Horvath were seemingly more naive and less talented, and Enid wasn't as naturally funny and observant? Cloris Leachman playing a transvestite might help the eventual word of mouth here.

Jimmy P. (2013)

117 min   -   Drama

Metascore: 58/100 (16 reviews)
A troubled Native American veteran forms an extraordinary friendship with his maverick French psychoanalyst as they try to find a cure to his suffering.
Director:
Arnaud Desplechin
Stars:
Benicio Del Toro, Mathieu Amalric, Gina McKee, Larry Pine
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Talk therapy rules the narrative of director Arnaud Desplechin's second English-language film which hasn't received strong reviews aside from some of its more subtle touches and refusal to diverge into melodrama. Desplechin's work of late has approximated camp, so perhaps this was too tough a transition for an otherwise crowd-pleasing filmmaker.

February 21 

Pompeii (2014)

105 min   -   Action | Adventure | Drama | History | Romance

Metascore: 39/100 (33 reviews)
A slave-turned-gladiator finds himself in a race against time to save his true love, who has been betrothed to a corrupt Roman Senator. As Mount Vesuvius erupts, he must fight to save his beloved as Pompeii crumbles around him.
Director:
Paul W.S. Anderson
Stars:
Kit Harington, Emily Browning, Kiefer Sutherland, Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's hard to tell if the Resident Evil army will turn up to support Paul W.S. Anderson's disaster tale; American audiences steered clear of his swooshy take on The Three Musketeers, which was was spared complete embarrassment by its performance overseas. And while we love Kit Harington as Jon Snow (from "Game of Thrones"), neither he nor co-star Emily Browning can help carry a movie at this point in their careers. So the real draw is ... Mount Vesuvius?

3 Days to Kill (2014)

117 min   -   Action | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 40/100 (30 reviews)
A dying CIA agent trying to reconnect with his estranged daughter is offered an experimental drug that could save his life in exchange for one last assignment.
Director:
McG
Stars:
Kevin Costner, Hailee Steinfeld, Connie Nielsen, Amber Heard
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Liam Neeson's Taken's success obviously caused handfuls of similar projects to be put in development; kudos to Kevin Costner for already having his career back on track before taking on this one ... last ... assignment.

Kaze tachinu (2013)

126 min   -   Animation | Biography | Drama | Romance | War

Metascore: 83/100 (41 reviews)
A look at the life of Jiro Horikoshi, the man who designed Japanese fighter planes during World War II.
Director:
Hayao Miyazaki
Stars:
Hideaki Anno, Hidetoshi Nishijima, Miori Takimoto, Masahiko Nishimura
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: We are 100% sure this is the last time we will see a work by Hayao Miyazaki even though he's played the retirement card yet again, indicating this is his last film. Remember that he signed off after Princess Mononoke and re-emerged with Spirited Away. Perhaps this is way a small segment of Miyazaki's movie-reviewer disciples are so grumbly.

Omar (2013)

96 min   -   Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 75/100 (28 reviews)
A young Palestinian freedom fighter agrees to work as an informant after he's tricked into an admission of guilt by association in the wake of an Israeli soldier's killing.
Director:
Hany Abu-Assad
Stars:
Adam Bakri, Leem Lubany, Eyad Hourani, Samer Bisharat
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Paradise Now director Hany Abu-Assad is back in the Foreign Language Oscar race with a story that juxtaposes a young man's commitment to the Palestine rebellion with his seemingly impossible love for a woman who lives on the other side of the separation wall. Here's hoping we see more from star Adam Bakri.

In Secret (2013)

107 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 47/100 (32 reviews)
Set in the lower echelons of 1860s Paris, Therese Raquin, a sexually repressed beautiful young woman, is trapped into a loveless marriage to her sickly cousin, Camille, by her domineering aunt, Madame Raquin. Therese spends her days confined behind the counter of a small shop and her evenings watching Madame play dominoes with an eclectic group. After she meets her husband's alluring friend, Laurent, she embarks on an illicit affair that leads to tragic consequences.
Director:
Charlie Stratton
Stars:
Elizabeth Olsen, Tom Felton, Jessica Lange, Oscar Isaac
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Screenwriter Charlie Stratton has directed Emile Zola's 19th Century novel Therese Raquin for the stage, and now he makes his feature film directorial debut with a neo-noir spin on the material and an incredibly able cast to serve up the lusty, greedy, impulsive themes. Stratton seems to be heavily influenced by writers and visual artists, so our interest is high and we sensing serious art-house potential here.

Elaine Stritch: Shoot Me (2013)

81 min   -   Documentary

Metascore: 81/100 (27 reviews)
The uncompromising Tony and Emmy Award-winner is showcased both on and off stage via rare archival footage and intimate cinema vérité.
Director:
Chiemi Karasawa
Stars:
Elaine Stritch, Tina Fey, Alec Baldwin, James Gandolfini
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Elaine Stritch packs more content and humor into an interview than most half-hour television comedies, and the fact that she has taken umbrage with aspects of Chiemi Karasawa's documentary makes us wish we could take in a post-screening Q&A with the filmmaker and her subject. And we simply will not miss what serves as her goodbye to New York City.

Highway (2014)

133 min   -   Adventure | Drama | Romance

Metascore: 40/100 (5 reviews)
Right before her wedding, a young woman finds herself abducted and held for ransom. As the initial days pass, she begins to develop a strange bond with her kidnapper.
Director:
Imtiaz Ali
Stars:
Alia Bhatt, Randeep Hooda, Durgesh Kumar, Arjun Malhotra
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Randeep Hooda is an emerging leading man who has worked steadily since making his big-screen debut in Monsoon Wedding, while his co-star Alia Bhatt, whose parents are both filmmakers, is just getting her start in the industry. Their director, Imtiaz Ali, first developed this story idea as a TV program but didn't have a set script when production began on his latest film.

February 28 

Non-Stop (2014)

106 min   -   Action | Mystery | Thriller

Metascore: 56/100 (41 reviews)
An air marshal springs into action during a transatlantic flight after receiving a series of text messages that put his fellow passengers at risk unless the airline transfers $150 million into an off-shore account.
Director:
Jaume Collet-Serra
Stars:
Liam Neeson, Julianne Moore, Scoot McNairy, Michelle Dockery
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Liam Neeson is the new Jason Statham since we're having difficulty distinguishing one of his action roles from another these days. This time out, he reunites with his Unknown director Jaume Collet-Serra and takes to the skies over the Atlantic Ocean -- something we don't often see anymore in mainstream movies. We see Julianne Moore aboard the plane and don't imagine she just sits there the entire time, but we're wondering how exactly Oscar nominee Lupita Nyong'o fits into the narrative. Is she playing a baddie? Hope so!

Son of God (2014)

138 min   -   Biography | Drama

Metascore: 37/100 (25 reviews)
The life story of Jesus is told from his humble birth through his teachings, crucifixion and ultimate resurrection.
Director:
Christopher Spencer
Stars:
Diogo Morgado, Amber Rose Revah, Sebastian Knapp, Paul Knops
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: It's easy to see why "Survivor" producer Mark Burnett put this film together in the wake of "The Bible"'s success after Burnett and company received huge ratings and three Emmy nominations for that mini-series. Any by "put together" we mean: the 10-hour mini-series was edited down into a 2-hour film. Once lower profile than Darren Aronofsky's Noah, advanced reports of the religious groups buying out entire theaters for opening weekend have us all wondering what the opening-weekend tally will be.

The Bag Man (2014)

108 min   -   Crime | Drama | Thriller

Metascore: 28/100 (18 reviews)
A criminal bides his time at a seedy motel, waiting for his boss after killing several men and making away with a mystery bag.
Director:
David Grovic
Stars:
John Cusack, Rebecca Da Costa, Robert De Niro, Crispin Glover
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: First-time director David Grovic gets some serious on-screen support from John Cusack and Robert De Niro for his genre exercise. And we're always happy to see Crispin Glover, a model of eccentric behavior in Hollywood (take some notes, Shia) on the big or small screen.

The Lunchbox (2013)

104 min   -   Drama | Romance

Metascore: 76/100 (29 reviews)
A mistaken delivery in Mumbai's famously efficient lunchbox delivery system connects a young housewife to an older man in the dusk of his life as they build a fantasy world together through notes in the lunchbox.
Director:
Ritesh Batra
Stars:
Irrfan Khan, Nimrat Kaur, Nawazuddin Siddiqui, Lillete Dubey
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Something would be amiss if the union of superstar Irrfan Khan and renown stage actress Nimrat Kaur fails to engage art-house audiences of the next couple of months. Director Ritesh Batra's feature-film debut won an audience award at Cannes and yet somehow missed out on being India's nomination for this year's Foreign Language Oscar.

Stalingrad (2013)

131 min   -   Action | Drama | War

Metascore: 49/100 (23 reviews)
A band of Russian soldiers fight to hold a strategic building in their devastated city against a ruthless German army, and in the process become deeply connected to two Russian women who have been living there.
Director:
Fedor Bondarchuk
Stars:
Mariya Smolnikova, Yanina Studilina, Pyotr Fyodorov, Thomas Kretschmann
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Russia's first-ever 3D production has been a huge success on its home turf, and we're wondering if part of the reason Columbia Pictures imported the movie for a special run in IMAX theaters is to road test director Fedor Bondarchuk's effects-heavy approach to filmmaking on American audiences. Bondarchuk already has the Inhabited Island franchise to his credit so it's not hard to imagine him being eyed for his first English-language project.

Repentance (2013)

90 min   -   Drama | Horror | Thriller

A successful author and spiritual advisor takes on a troubled man as a client, completely unaware that the man's fixation on his mother's death will soon put his life in jeopardy.
Director:
Philippe Caland
Stars:
Anthony Mackie, Forest Whitaker, Sanaa Lathan, Mike Epps
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Forest Whitaker has made a film already with director Philippe Caland, the little-seen morality tale Ripple Effect, and here they've re-teamed for what's being promoted to general audiences as a Misery-like thriller since it appears the movie's Buddhist themes have been toned down in its marketing.

Anchorman 2: The Legend Continues (2013)

119 min   -   Comedy

Metascore: 61/100 (40 reviews)
With the 70s behind him, San Diego's top rated newsman, Ron Burgundy, returns to take New York's first 24-hour news channel by storm.
Director:
Adam McKay
Stars:
Will Ferrell, Christina Applegate, Paul Rudd, Steve Carell
Watch Trailer

The Buzz: Ron Burgundy and company did everything we expected in their second outing: they outperformed their first adventure, expanded their overseas audience, and set themselves up for a possible trilogy. This re-release is essentially a re-edited version retrofitted with an R rating and allegedly 800 new jokes.
